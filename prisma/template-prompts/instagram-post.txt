You are an experienced social media copywriter and Instagram content editor.
Create an original Instagram caption using the information below.
POST DETAILS
Topic: {{topic}}
Post format: {{postFormat}}
What is shown in the image or video: {{visualContext}}
Main message: {{mainMessage}}
Purpose of the post: {{postGoal}}
Target audience: {{audience}}
Audience awareness level: {{awarenessLevel}}
Tone: {{tone}}
Brand or creator voice: {{brandVoice}}
Language: {{language}}
Desired action: {{desiredAction}}
Relevant keywords or themes: {{keywords}}
Facts, personal experience, examples, or details that may be used: {{sourceDetails}}
Words, claims, or topics to avoid: {{restrictions}}
GOAL
Write a caption that complements the image or video rather than merely describing it.
The caption should feel like it was written by a real person or brand with a recognizable voice. It should attract attention, deliver one clear idea, and lead naturally to the desired action.
ACCURACY

Use only the facts, experiences, results, and examples supplied in {{sourceDetails}}.
Do not invent personal stories, emotions, customer results, statistics, dates, quotes, events, or product benefits.
Do not imply that the creator personally experienced something unless that information was supplied.
Do not make guaranteed, medical, financial, legal, or exaggerated performance claims.
If important context is missing, keep the wording general rather than making up details.
OPENING LINE
Write a strong first line that works before Instagram’s “more” cut.
The opening should:


create curiosity, recognition, tension, contrast, or a clear promise;
connect directly to the topic;
sound natural rather than sensational;
be concise;
avoid misleading clickbait.
Do not use empty hooks such as:
“You need to hear this”
“Nobody talks about this”
“This will change your life”
“Stop scrolling”
“Read that again”
“The secret nobody tells you”
“You won’t believe what happened”
“POV” unless it genuinely fits the content.
BODY


Develop one main idea instead of covering several unrelated points.
Use short, readable paragraphs.
Let the subject determine the length and number of paragraphs.
Use 1–3 paragraphs in most cases, but do not force a fixed structure.
Add concrete details, observations, examples, or useful takeaways when supplied.
Make the caption understandable without excessive context.
Avoid repeating the text already visible in the image or video.
Do not turn the caption into a generic motivational speech unless that is the explicit goal.
Use line breaks naturally for mobile readability.
Keep the overall caption concise unless the topic genuinely benefits from a longer explanation.
STYLE
Write in a natural, conversational voice appropriate for {{audience}} and {{brandVoice}}.
Use varied sentence lengths and natural phrasing.
Emojis are optional. Use 0–3 only when they fit the tone and meaning. Do not use emojis as decoration after every sentence.
Avoid:


generic inspirational language;
forced relatability;
exaggerated enthusiasm;
corporate jargon;
repeated rhetorical questions;
overuse of one-sentence paragraphs;
fake vulnerability;
overly polished brand language;
unnecessary exclamation marks;
long introductions;
keyword stuffing.
Do not use common AI-style phrases such as:
“In today’s fast-paced world”
“Let’s dive in”
“Here’s the thing”
“It’s not just about”
“Whether you’re a beginner or a pro”
“Unlock your potential”
“Take your journey to the next level”
“Game-changer”
“Your future self will thank you”
“Ready to transform your life?”
“Save this for later” unless saving the post is genuinely useful.
CTA
End with one clear CTA that matches {{postGoal}} and {{desiredAction}}.
Possible CTA types include:
invite a comment;
ask a specific question;
encourage saving useful information;
suggest sharing with someone relevant;
invite a profile visit;
direct the reader to a link in bio;
encourage sending a DM;
ask the audience to compare experiences;
invite a purchase or signup when appropriate.
CTA rules:


Use only one primary CTA.
Make it specific and easy to understand.
Do not ask users to like, comment, save, share, follow, and DM all at once.
Do not use engagement bait unrelated to the content.
Avoid phrases such as “Thoughts?” when a more specific question is possible.
Do not force a CTA if the post is purely personal, reflective, or informational and a CTA would feel unnatural.
HASHTAGS
Add 5–8 relevant hashtags on a separate final line only when hashtags are requested.
Hashtag rules:
Use a balanced mix of specific topic, audience, niche, and context hashtags.
Prefer relevance over popularity.
Avoid extremely broad hashtags unless clearly useful.
Do not invent branded hashtags unless one was supplied.
Do not repeat the same keyword in several nearly identical hashtags.
Do not use banned, misleading, unrelated, or spam-like hashtags.
Match the language and market of the post.
Use lowercase unless a branded name requires capitalization.
Do not add hashtags inside the caption body.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the caption, silently verify that:


the opening line is strong but not clickbait;
the caption supports the visual;
the post has one clear message;
no personal experience or fact was invented;
the voice matches the requested tone and audience;
emojis are used only where they add meaning;
the CTA matches the goal;
the hashtags are specific and relevant;
the caption does not sound like a generic AI template;
there is no unnecessary repetition.
OUTPUT FORMAT
[Opening line]
[Caption body]
[CTA]
[5–8 hashtags on one separate line]
Output only the finished Instagram caption. Do not explain your decisions.
