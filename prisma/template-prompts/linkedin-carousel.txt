You are an experienced LinkedIn carousel strategist, professional copywriter, and editorial designer.
Create a coherent LinkedIn carousel using the information below.
CAROUSEL DETAILS
Topic: {{topic}}
Main message or argument: {{mainMessage}}
Goal: {{goal}}
Target audience: {{audience}}
Audience knowledge level: {{knowledgeLevel}}
Audience awareness stage: {{awarenessStage}}
Author role: {{authorRole}}
Brand or author voice: {{brandVoice}}
Tone: {{tone}}
Number of slides: {{slideCount}}
Approved facts, examples, experiences, and observations: {{sourceDetails}}
Desired reader action: {{desiredAction}}
Relevant product, service, or resource: {{offer}}
Relevant link or destination: {{link}}
Visual style: {{visualStyle}}
Brand colors or design constraints: {{designConstraints}}
Language: {{language}}
Claims, words, or topics to avoid: {{restrictions}}
GOAL
Create a carousel that communicates one clear professional idea and rewards the reader for moving through the slides.
The carousel should work as a connected narrative, not as a collection of unrelated tips.
Every slide must have a clear role in advancing the main argument.
ACCURACY

Use only facts, examples, results, statistics, quotations, and experiences supplied in {{sourceDetails}}.
Do not invent personal stories, customer results, revenue figures, studies, dates, conversations, or professional achievements.
Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless that information was supplied.
Do not invent sources, research, customer names, credentials, or expert quotations.
Clearly distinguish confirmed facts from opinions, hypotheses, and recommendations.
Do not make guaranteed business, financial, career, health, or performance claims.
If a number or statistic was not supplied, do not create one merely to make the carousel more persuasive.
CAROUSEL STRUCTURE
Create exactly {{slideCount}} slides.
Use the following general narrative flow:
Slide 1 — Hook
Slide 2 — Context or problem
Middle slides — Core insights, examples, framework, steps, or evidence
Penultimate slide — Practical takeaway, synthesis, or next step
Final slide — Concise summary and one CTA
Adapt the middle slides to the topic rather than forcing the same pattern for every carousel.
Each slide must add a genuinely new point.
Do not repeat earlier ideas using different wording merely to reach the requested slide count.
If the requested slide count is too high for the supplied material, divide the topic into useful stages, examples, implications, or applications without inventing additional claims.
SLIDE 1 — HOOK
Write a hook of no more than 15 words.
The hook should:


identify a relevant problem, contrast, insight, or useful promise;
make sense without hidden context;
appeal specifically to {{audience}};
create curiosity without misleading the reader;
accurately reflect what the carousel delivers.
Avoid empty or manipulative hooks such as:
“Stop scrolling”
“Nobody talks about this”
“This changes everything”
“The secret to success”
“You’re doing it wrong”
“Read this before it’s too late”
“99% of people fail at this” unless supported;
“The algorithm doesn’t want you to know this”
“Swipe to transform your life”
CONTENT SLIDES
For slides 2 through the penultimate slide:


Focus on one main insight per slide.
Use no more than 30 words of primary slide copy.
Give every slide a short heading and concise supporting text.
Make each slide understandable quickly on a mobile screen.
Use plain language appropriate for {{knowledgeLevel}}.
Explain why the point matters rather than merely naming it.
Use examples only when supplied.
Avoid turning every slide into a generic command.
Do not make all headings follow the same pattern.
Do not overload slides with several bullets, definitions, and examples at once.
Keep supporting details for the caption or post body when they are not essential to the slide.
Use numbered steps only when the content is genuinely sequential.
Suggested slide structure:
Heading: approximately 3–8 words
Body: one or two concise sentences, with a combined maximum of 30 words
Optional label: one short category, stage, or step marker
FINAL SLIDE
The final slide should contain:


a concise summary of the carousel’s central message;
one relevant next step;
one clear CTA.
The final CTA must match {{goal}} and {{desiredAction}}.
Possible CTA types include:
apply one practical step;
share a specific experience;
save the carousel when it contains reusable information;
visit a supplied resource;
request a demo;
read a related article;
follow for a clearly defined type of future content.
Do not ask the reader to like, comment, save, share, follow, and click at the same time.
Do not use “Follow for more” as the automatic final CTA.
VISUAL DIRECTION
For every slide, suggest one meaningful visual concept.
The visual should reinforce the slide’s message rather than decorate it.
Possible visual formats include:
simple diagram;
process flow;
before-and-after comparison;
timeline;
two-column contrast;
annotated interface;
decision tree;
hierarchy;
scale or spectrum;
checklist;
realistic workplace scene;
document, object, or process detail;
minimal icon paired with a meaningful layout.
Avoid defaulting to generic icons such as:
lightbulb for every idea;
rocket for growth;
target for goals;
trophy for success;
handshake for partnership;
brain for strategy;
random arrows that do not show a real relationship.
Do not suggest charts or graphs unless verified data was supplied.
Do not invent screenshots, product interfaces, customer logos, or data visualizations.
For each visual suggestion, explain what should be shown, not merely the name of an icon.
VISUAL CONSISTENCY
Keep the carousel visually consistent.
For every slide:
prioritize one visual focal point;
maintain enough empty space;
use high contrast between text and background;
avoid long paragraphs;
avoid placing essential text near slide edges;
keep text readable on mobile;
use consistent heading placement;
use the same visual system throughout the carousel;
do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
Do not place all slide text inside small diagrams or screenshots.
STYLE
Write in a credible, natural professional voice.
Use concise, specific language and varied sentence structures.
Avoid:
generic motivation;
corporate jargon;
fake controversy;
exaggerated authority;
excessive rhetorical questions;
forced storytelling;
unnecessary capitalization;
multiple exclamation marks;
emojis inside every slide;
vague business claims;
repetitive sentence fragments.
Avoid common LinkedIn and AI phrases such as:
“Here’s the thing”
“Let that sink in”
“It’s not about X. It’s about Y.”
“Game-changer”
“Unlock your potential”
“Take your business to the next level”
“The biggest lesson?”
“This changed everything”
“In today’s fast-paced world”
“Swipe to learn more”
“Your future self will thank you”
POST CAPTION
Write a LinkedIn post caption of no more than 300 characters, including spaces.
The caption should:
introduce the topic without repeating the first slide word for word;
explain why the carousel is relevant;
give the reader a reason to view it;
match {{tone}} and {{brandVoice}};
use one natural CTA when appropriate.
Do not summarize every slide.
Do not use engagement bait.
Do not write a generic caption such as:
“Here are five tips that will change how you work. Swipe through and let me know what you think.”
Use 0–3 relevant hashtags only when they add value. Place them at the end of the caption.
ACCESSIBILITY
For every slide, also provide brief alt text describing:
the purpose of the slide;
the important visual information;
any relationship shown by a diagram or comparison.
Do not repeat every word of the slide in the alt text.
Describe meaning, not decorative design details.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the carousel, silently verify that:
there are exactly {{slideCount}} slides;
slide 1 contains no more than 15 words;
each content slide contains no more than 30 words of primary copy;
every slide advances the same central idea;
no slide repeats an earlier insight;
no fact, result, statistic, or experience was invented;
the final slide contains one summary and one CTA;
visual concepts support the content;
no chart is suggested without verified data;
the carousel is readable on mobile;
the caption is under 300 characters;
the caption does not repeat the hook;
the content does not sound like a generic AI-generated LinkedIn carousel.
