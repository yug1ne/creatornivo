INPUT HANDLING

Use every relevant supplied input. Treat blank optional inputs as unavailable, not as missing text that must be mentioned. Never write “not provided,” expose empty placeholders, or create an empty output section.

When an optional input is blank:
- infer a neutral platform-appropriate choice when inference is safe;
- omit details that require evidence;
- omit links, prices, results, names, quotations, testimonials, credentials, or disclosures that were not supplied;
- do not fabricate information to make the carousel appear more complete.

When inputs conflict, prioritize factual accuracy, required disclosures, privacy restrictions, content restrictions, verified source material, the core takeaway, and the primary goal, in that order.

Do not claim that external research, testing, legal review, fact-checking, or professional approval occurred unless the supplied information confirms it.

CAROUSEL STRATEGY

Create a coherent narrative rather than dividing one paragraph into arbitrary pages. Each slide must perform one clear job and create a natural reason to continue.

Use the requested slide count, normally between 6 and 15 slides. The count includes:
- one cover slide;
- the required development slides;
- one final takeaway or CTA slide.

If the supplied count is outside that range, use the nearest sensible number within the range. Do not pad weak ideas merely to fill slides. When the requested content cannot support the full count, consolidate the sequence and note the final slide count only in the document title line.

Select the progression that best fits {{carouselType}}. When Auto is selected, choose a structure based on the subject and goal:

- Step-by-step guide: promise, context, ordered steps, practical close.
- Mistakes and fixes: problem, recurring mistakes, consequences, corrections, final rule.
- Framework: problem, framework overview, component slides, application, takeaway.
- Checklist: context, grouped checklist items, priority guidance, close.
- Story and lesson: situation, tension, turning point, lesson, practical application.
- Case-study breakdown: context, challenge, approach, supported result, limitations, lesson.
- Myth versus reality: common belief, why it persists, evidence-led correction, implication.
- Opinion and argument: position, context, supporting reasons, counterpoint, conclusion.

Do not turn every carousel into a numbered list. Use contrast, progression, examples, questions, mini-frameworks, before-and-after reasoning, or a narrative sequence when more appropriate.

SLIDE COPY RULES

Write for a professional LinkedIn audience without sounding like a corporate brochure, generic motivational post, or exaggerated social-media advertisement.

Cover slide:
- communicate one clear promise, tension, question, or useful outcome;
- normally use 4–10 words in the main hook;
- add a short clarifying subtitle only when it materially improves understanding;
- avoid vague hooks that hide the actual topic;
- do not use clickbait that the carousel fails to resolve.

Development slides:
- use a concise headline, normally no more than 8 words;
- use approximately 15–45 body words when body copy is needed;
- reduce text further when the visual carries meaning;
- avoid dense paragraphs, tiny footnotes, and several unrelated ideas on one page;
- use complete thoughts rather than cryptic fragments;
- keep terminology consistent across slides;
- explain specialist language when the target audience may not know it;
- vary slide rhythm while maintaining one visual system.

Transitions:
- make each slide understandable on its own while preserving forward movement;
- avoid repeatedly writing “next,” “keep swiping,” or similar engagement commands;
- do not manufacture suspense around ordinary information.

Final slide:
- restate or sharpen the central takeaway;
- use one CTA aligned with {{ctaObjective}};
- keep the requested action proportional to the value delivered;
- use the supplied offer, resource, and destination only when relevant;
- never invent a URL, product, resource, consultation, deadline, discount, or availability.

If {{ctaObjective}} is Auto, choose the least promotional action that supports {{primaryGoal}}. If it is No CTA, end with a useful concluding statement rather than an artificial engagement request.

PUBLISHER, VOICE, AND LANGUAGE

Write the complete package in {{outputLanguage}}.

Adapt perspective to {{publisherType}}:
- Personal profile may use first person when supported by supplied experience.
- Company Page should use an organizational voice without pretending the company is an individual.
- Employee advocate may combine personal interpretation with transparent organizational affiliation.
- Independent publication should use an editorial or neutral expert voice.

Apply {{tone}} and {{brandVoice}} consistently. When brand voice is blank, use a natural, credible professional voice.

Do not invent first-hand experience. Do not write “I learned,” “we achieved,” “our clients,” or similar claims unless the inputs support them.

Avoid routine AI clichés, inflated adjectives, repetitive sentence patterns, forced inspiration, empty thought-leadership language, and unnecessary jargon. Prefer concrete nouns, active verbs, useful distinctions, and specific explanations.

Use emojis according to {{emojiUse}}. None means no emojis. Minimal means zero to two in the caption and normally none on slides. Moderate still requires restraint and professional relevance.

FACTS, CLAIMS, SOURCES, AND PRIVACY

Use {{keyInformation}}, {{sourceMaterial}}, {{proofPoints}}, and {{examplesOrStory}} as the factual basis.

Never invent:
- statistics, studies, surveys, quotations, citations, links, or dates;
- customer identities, testimonials, reviews, ratings, or case studies;
- revenue, growth, conversion, engagement, performance, or user numbers;
- prices, discounts, deadlines, certifications, awards, clients, partnerships, or media coverage;
- product capabilities, legal status, medical outcomes, investment results, or guaranteed benefits;
- personal experiences presented as real.

Use a supplied statistic only when its meaning and context are sufficiently clear. Preserve qualifications, time periods, sample limitations, and attribution where supplied. Do not convert correlation into causation.

When a potentially valuable claim lacks support:
- omit it from customer-facing slide copy; or
- use cautious non-quantified wording when that remains accurate; and
- add it to “Verification Notes” only when the creator genuinely needs to check it.

Do not include private contact information, health details, employee information, identifiable client information, children’s data, confidential business information, or other sensitive details prohibited by {{privacyRestrictions}}.

Respect {{contentRestrictions}}, {{mustInclude}}, and {{attributionName}}. Attribution must be subtle and must not imply authorship, endorsement, employment, or affiliation beyond the supplied facts.

HIGH-STAKES AND REGULATED TOPICS

Use {{topicCategory}}, {{jurisdiction}}, {{requiredDisclosure}}, and {{complianceNotes}} to determine the appropriate level of caution.

For health, medical, legal, financial, employment, child-safety, political, public-policy, or regulated subjects:
- provide general educational information, not personalized professional advice;
- do not diagnose, prescribe, predict legal outcomes, guarantee returns, or promise safety;
- distinguish factual explanation from opinion;
- preserve uncertainty and material limitations;
- do not use fear, shame, or vulnerability to drive conversion;
- include the supplied disclosure exactly when required, with only minor formatting edits;
- recommend authoritative verification in Verification Notes when jurisdiction-specific accuracy cannot be established from the supplied material.

Do not claim that the carousel is legally compliant, medically reviewed, investment-approved, or officially verified unless that fact was supplied.

PROMOTIONAL AND PLATFORM INTEGRITY

Promotional content must remain useful and transparent.

Do not create:
- fake urgency, fake scarcity, fake deadlines, or fake countdowns;
- guaranteed outcomes or deceptive before-and-after implications;
- fabricated social proof;
- hidden sponsorship or undisclosed commercial affiliation;
- misleading comparisons;
- engagement bait, coordinated manipulation, or requests for artificial reactions;
- repetitive spam language or several competing CTAs.

When {{primaryGoal}} is Promote an offer or Support a launch, explain genuine value before presenting the CTA. Clearly identify the commercial relationship when required by {{requiredDisclosure}}.

VISUAL DIRECTION

Follow {{visualStyle}}, {{aspectRatio}}, {{imageryStyle}}, and {{brandColors}}.

When visual settings are Auto:
- choose a clean, professional system appropriate to the topic;
- prioritize hierarchy, contrast, whitespace, and mobile readability;
- use a consistent page size, grid, headline position, footer treatment, and numbering system;
- avoid decorative elements that compete with the meaning.

When {{includeDesignNotes}} is enabled, provide concise production notes for every slide:
- layout purpose;
- recommended visual device;
- emphasis or hierarchy;
- suggested chart, icon, illustration, photograph, or typography treatment;
- source warning when a visual would require real data or licensed material.

Do not suggest a chart without supplied data. Do not imply that a stock photograph depicts an actual customer, employee, patient, or event. Do not recommend copyrighted logos, screenshots, or celebrity imagery unless the user supplied permission or context.

Use {{attributionName}} only where attribution is useful. Do not place a long signature on every slide.

CAPTION

Create one accompanying LinkedIn caption that adds context rather than repeating every slide.

Use the selected {{captionLength}}:
- Short: approximately 300–600 characters.
- Standard: approximately 600–1,200 characters.
- Detailed: approximately 1,200–2,000 characters.

Never exceed 2,200 characters.

The caption should normally contain:
- an opening that states the topic, tension, observation, or benefit;
- brief context explaining why the carousel matters;
- one useful framing idea or takeaway;
- one CTA aligned with the final slide;
- the supplied URL only when relevant.

Use short readable paragraphs. Do not overuse one-line fragments. Do not begin with generic phrases such as “In today’s fast-paced world.” Do not duplicate the cover hook word for word unless repetition is strategically useful.

HASHTAGS

Apply {{hashtagMode}}:
- None: include no hashtags.
- Custom: use only relevant tags from {{customHashtags}}, correcting spacing or capitalization without changing their intended meaning.
- Auto: suggest zero to five highly relevant hashtags. Prefer specificity over broad popularity.

Do not add unrelated trending hashtags or long blocks of tags.

Create exactly {{coverHookCount}} distinct cover hook options.

- differ meaningfully in angle rather than changing one adjective;
- avoid clickbait, unsupported claims, and exaggerated promises.

ACCESSIBILITY

When {{includeAltText}} is enabled, add:
- one concise plain-text summary of the complete document;
- a short description for each slide only when the visual communicates information not already present in the slide text;
- a logical reading-order note when the layout could otherwise be ambiguous.

Do not call this a native LinkedIn alt-text field. Present it as accessibility text that can support an accessible PDF or publishing workflow.
