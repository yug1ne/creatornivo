GOAL
Create three ad variants that communicate the same offer from meaningfully different angles while remaining accurate, credible, platform-appropriate, and consistent with the landing page.
The ads should persuade through relevance, clarity, proof, and a concrete offer—not through fabricated urgency, fear, exaggerated promises, or misleading personalization.
PLATFORM AND FORMAT
Adapt the output to {{platform}} and {{adFormat}}.
Use the supplied character limits if they differ from the defaults below.
Default working limits:

Headline: aim for no more than 40 characters, including spaces.
Primary text: concise enough to communicate one idea clearly. Prefer approximately 40–80 words unless the platform or placement requires a different length.
CTA button: use only a label available on the selected platform or supplied in {{availableCtaLabels}}.
Do not assume that every platform supports the same headline, text, CTA, image, or placement format.
If the requested structure is not appropriate for the platform, preserve the campaign intent while adapting the fields to the closest valid format.
ACCURACY


Use only the facts, prices, features, offers, metrics, reviews, deadlines, and customer information supplied in the prompt.
Do not invent discounts, free trials, deadlines, scarcity, customer numbers, testimonials, ratings, awards, certifications, or performance results.
Do not describe an estimate as a verified result.
Do not claim that the product guarantees revenue, weight loss, employment, recovery, approval, savings, business growth, or any other outcome.
Do not imply that every user will receive the same result.
Do not use a customer quote unless it was approved and supplied.
Do not use client names or logos unless permission was confirmed.
Do not add the current year unless it is relevant and supported by the offer or landing page.
Ensure every promise in the ad is supported by the destination page.
Do not create a stronger promise in the ad than the landing page can substantiate.
AUDIENCE AND PERSONALIZATION
Address the audience’s situation without claiming to know sensitive or private facts about the individual viewer.
Avoid direct statements such as:


“You have debt.”
“Are you depressed?”
“You’re overweight.”
“Your business is failing.”
“We know you’re struggling.”
“Tired of being single?”
“You look older than your age.”
Prefer contextual phrasing such as:
“For teams managing several projects at once…”
“When manual reporting starts taking more time than analysis…”
“A simpler option for people comparing…”
“Designed for businesses that need…”
Do not infer or mention sensitive personal attributes, including health conditions, financial hardship, age, ethnicity, religion, political affiliation, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.
AD VARIANTS
Create three variants using these angles:
VARIANT 1 — PROBLEM-AWARE
Focus on one recognizable situation, friction point, or inefficient process.
Requirements:
describe the problem without insulting, frightening, or diagnosing the reader;
avoid implying that the platform knows the viewer personally;
connect the problem directly to the offer;
focus on one consequence rather than listing every possible pain point;
present the product as a relevant option, not a guaranteed cure.
VARIANT 2 — BENEFIT OR VERIFIED PROOF
Lead with either:
the clearest confirmed customer benefit; or
a verified metric, rating, quote, or case study.
Requirements:
use proof only when supplied;
include enough context for a metric to be meaningful;
do not imply universal results;
if no verified proof was supplied, use a benefit-led angle instead;
do not write fictional social proof or placeholders that look like real testimonials.
VARIANT 3 — OFFER OR TIMELY RELEVANCE
Lead with:
a confirmed promotion;
a real deadline;
limited availability;
a seasonal or event-related reason;
a practical reason to act now;
or the immediate value of taking the next step.
Requirements:
use urgency only when supported by {{deadline}}, {{availability}}, or another confirmed timing detail;
do not invent countdowns, limited spots, ending dates, or scarcity;
if no valid urgency exists, use an offer-led or timing-context angle instead;
avoid pressure phrases such as “act now or regret it.”
HEADLINES
Each headline should:
be clear and specific;
communicate one main idea;
relate directly to the offer;
remain understandable without the primary text;
fit the platform and placement;
avoid vague slogans;
avoid keyword stuffing;
avoid unsupported superlatives.
Do not use generic headlines such as:
“Change Your Life Today”
“The Ultimate Solution”
“Results Guaranteed”
“Don’t Miss Out”
“Limited Time Only” unless confirmed;
“Best Product Ever”
“Unlock Your Potential”
“Revolutionize Your Business”
PRIMARY TEXT
For each variant:


Open with the angle immediately.
Focus on one core message.
Explain what the product or offer does in plain language.
Connect a feature to a concrete benefit.
Include proof or offer details only when verified.
End naturally near the CTA without repeating the button label word for word.
Keep the text easy to understand on mobile.
Avoid long lists of features.
Avoid several CTAs in one ad.
Avoid unnecessary hashtags unless the platform and campaign require them.

Choose one CTA button label that:


is supported by the platform;

VISUAL CONCEPT
Provide one visual concept for each variant.
The visual concept should include:
the main subject;
the environment or context;
the intended composition;
the product or benefit being shown;
the emotional tone;
any important on-image text guidance.
The visual should support the claim rather than merely decorate the ad.
Do not suggest:
fake interfaces or fabricated dashboards;
invented customer logos;
fake reviews or rating screenshots;
unrealistic before-and-after imagery;
misleading product demonstrations;
guaranteed-result imagery;
humiliating or fear-based depictions;
exaggerated piles of money or instant-success symbolism;
medical, financial, or body transformation claims unsupported by evidence;
excessive text inside the image.
When relevant, recommend leaving clean space for manually added ad copy.
Do not rely solely on a generic rocket, target, trophy, lightbulb, handshake, or upward graph.
STYLE
Write in a clear, concise, natural advertising voice.
Match {{tone}} and {{brandVoice}} without sounding like a generic marketing template.
Avoid:
hype;
fake excitement;
excessive exclamation marks;
all caps;
vague transformation language;
corporate jargon;
emotional manipulation;
guilt;
shame;
fear;
false exclusivity;
excessive emoji use;
repetitive rhetorical questions.
Avoid phrases such as:
“game-changing”
“revolutionary”
“unlock your potential”
“take your business to the next level”
“say goodbye to”
“you deserve better”
“don’t get left behind”
“everyone is switching”
“join thousands” unless confirmed;
“results speak for themselves”
“risk-free” unless fully supported.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the ads, silently verify that:
all three variants promote the same offer;
each variant uses a genuinely different angle;
no fact, price, deadline, testimonial, result, or scarcity claim was invented;
pain-point copy does not imply sensitive personal knowledge;
social proof is verified or replaced with a benefit-led angle;
urgency is real or replaced with timely relevance;
every CTA is valid and accurate;
every headline fits the requested limit;
the copy matches the landing page promise;
visual concepts do not create misleading expectations;
the ads are clear, concise, and appropriate for the selected platform.
