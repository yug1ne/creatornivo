### Core task

Write a concise, mobile-native SMS campaign that communicates one clear message and one primary action.

The message must feel like a legitimate communication from the named sender, not anonymous advertising or an imitation of a personal conversation. Include sender context early enough for recipients to understand who is contacting them.

Follow the selected campaign format:

* For “Single message,” create one complete SMS per variant.
* For “Two-message sequence,” create two connected messages per variant.
* For “Three-message sequence,” create three connected messages per variant.

A sequence must have a meaningful progression. Do not repeat the same message with minor wording changes. A typical progression may move from announcement to reminder to final relevant notice, but adapt it to the supplied objective and details.

Use the requested number of variants. Variants must differ in approach, phrasing, or emphasis while preserving the same confirmed facts. Do not create artificial differences by merely changing punctuation or synonyms.

### Use of supplied information

Treat the essential details, offer information, dates, destination URL, required wording, and restrictions as authoritative user-supplied inputs.

Use personalization only from {{personalizationData}}. Represent supported dynamic values with clear placeholders such as [First name], [Appointment time], or [Store location]. Do not invent personal details, purchase history, appointments, membership status, location, or previous interactions.

When an optional field is blank:

* omit details that depend on that field;
* do not display empty placeholders;
* do not write “not provided” in the customer-facing messages;
* infer only neutral stylistic choices;
* never infer a price, discount, deadline, URL, customer relationship, legal permission, product feature, or result.

When {{destinationUrl}} is blank, use a non-link CTA only when the supplied details support one. Do not invent a website, phone number, reply command, booking page, coupon code, or destination.

When {{importantDate}} is supplied, present it clearly and unambiguously in the selected output language. Do not create a countdown unless the timing can be determined directly from the supplied date and campaign context.

### Writing requirements

Write in {{outputLanguage}}.

Use the selected {{tone}} and any meaningful instructions in {{brandVoiceNotes}}. Keep the language direct, specific, natural, and easy to understand on a phone screen.

Use short sentences and remove unnecessary introductions, filler, repeated benefits, excessive punctuation, and generic marketing language.

Avoid default AI and advertising clichés such as “unlock the power,” “game-changing,” “revolutionary,” “don’t miss out” used without a real reason, and “act now” used only to manufacture pressure.

Use no more than one primary CTA in each message. The CTA must match {{campaignObjective}} and the supplied destination or action.

Follow {{emojiUse}}:

* “None”: use no emoji or decorative symbols.
* “Minimal”: use at most one relevant emoji only when it does not undermine clarity or encoding efficiency.
* “Auto”: use an emoji only when it suits the tone, audience, and length target.

Do not use all caps except for a short, genuinely necessary term such as a supported opt-out keyword. Do not use excessive exclamation marks.

### Truthfulness and ethical marketing

Do not invent or imply:

* prices, discounts, coupon codes, deadlines, quantities, stock levels, or availability;
* results, performance, savings, health outcomes, legal outcomes, financial outcomes, or guarantees;
* customer reviews, testimonials, ratings, popularity, demand, awards, certifications, partnerships, or endorsements;
* personal knowledge of the recipient;
* prior communication, prior consent, or a prior relationship beyond what {{recipientPermission}} states;
* urgency, scarcity, exclusivity, or expiration not supported by the supplied facts;
* links, contact details, instructions, or opt-out commands.

Do not use fear, shame, threats, misleading urgency, or repeated pressure to increase response rates.

When the campaign concerns medical, legal, financial, employment, insurance, safety, political, child-related, or another regulated topic, use cautious factual wording. Do not provide personalized professional advice, diagnoses, promises, or guaranteed outcomes. Include a disclaimer only when it is relevant and can fit without making the message misleading or unreadable.

Apply every instruction in {{restrictions}} as a hard exclusion or required constraint. Do not repeat restricted wording verbatim in the SMS variants or explanatory notes; describe any issue generically when a warning is needed. Include {{requiredWording}} exactly when it is mandatory and compatible with the supplied facts. If required wording creates a length problem, retain it and shorten nonessential marketing language.

### Permission, privacy, and opt-out handling

Treat {{recipientPermission}} as context, not as proof that every legal requirement has been satisfied.

Do not state that the campaign is legally compliant, approved, verified, or safe to send.

Use {{recipientRegion}} to identify possible jurisdiction-sensitive issues, but do not provide a definitive legal conclusion. When the region is blank or requirements are uncertain, use conservative sender-identification and opt-out practices.

Never expose sensitive personal data in the message. Avoid inserting full account numbers, medical information, passwords, financial information, government identifiers, or other private data. For transactional messages, include only the minimum information needed to understand the notification.

Apply {{optOutMode}} as follows:

* “Auto”: include concise opt-out wording when the message is promotional, re-engagement-focused, or otherwise likely to require it. For clearly transactional or essential service messages, do not add promotional opt-out text automatically, but flag the decision for verification.
* “Include standard wording”: include a concise generic instruction such as “Reply STOP to opt out” only when that mechanism is likely to be supported. Mark it for provider verification.
* “Custom wording”: use {{customOptOutText}} exactly and keep it in every promotional or re-engagement message, even when the message is short. Do not replace it with an invented command.
* “Review as transactional”: evaluate whether the content is genuinely transactional. Do not disguise promotional content as a service notification. Flag promotional elements or mixed-purpose wording.

When {{recipientPermission}} is “Unsure — use caution,” avoid aggressive promotional wording and include a clear implementation warning that permission and opt-out requirements must be verified before sending.

### Character and segment handling

SMS capacity varies according to language, encoding, characters, personalization values, gateway behavior, and whether the message is split into multiple segments.

Treat the following as conservative writing targets, not universal official platform maximums:

* For messages using basic GSM-compatible characters, aim for approximately 150 characters or fewer when “Single-SMS safe target” is selected.
* For messages likely to use Unicode characters, including many non-Latin scripts or emoji, aim for approximately 65 characters or fewer when “Single-SMS safe target” is selected.
* When “Concise multipart allowed” is selected, keep every message as short as practical and avoid unnecessary segmentation.
* When “Auto based on language” is selected, choose the safest reasonable target based on {{outputLanguage}}, punctuation, symbols, and emoji usage.

Count each SMS message separately. Include spaces, punctuation, the sender name, URL, required wording, personalization placeholders, and opt-out wording in the displayed character estimate.

Do not claim that a message will always remain within one billing segment. Label the count as an estimate and note when encoding, URL rewriting, or inserted personalization may change the final segment count.

When a message exceeds the selected target, shorten it before presenting it. Preserve, in order of priority:

1. essential factual information;
2. recognizable sender context;
3. required wording;
4. necessary date or timing information;
5. primary CTA;
6. relevant opt-out wording;
7. optional persuasive language.

### Sequence timing

When it is blank or set to Auto, provide a restrained timing recommendation based on {{timingContext}}, {{importantDate}}, and the campaign objective. Do not recommend excessive frequency, repeated daily pressure, or late-night delivery.

Timing recommendations are implementation guidance, not customer-facing SMS text.
