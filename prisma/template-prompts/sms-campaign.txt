GOAL

Create a concise, accurate, permission-based messaging campaign that helps the recipient understand:

- who is sending the message;
- why they are receiving it;
- what happened or what is being offered;
- what action, if any, is required;
- whether a deadline or condition applies;
- how to get help;
- how to stop promotional messages where required.

The campaign should:

- respect the recipient’s consent;
- use one primary purpose per message;
- avoid misleading urgency;
- avoid unnecessary message segments;
- remain understandable without relying on an image;
- work naturally in {{language}} and {{targetLocale}};
- stop when the recipient converts, opts out, or meets {{exitCondition}};
- avoid messages that resemble fraud, phishing, or spam.

CHARACTER ENCODING AND SEGMENTS

Estimate the encoding and number of SMS segments for every message.

Use these planning assumptions unless the sending platform specifies different rules:

- GSM-7 single segment: up to approximately 160 characters.
- GSM-7 concatenated segment: approximately 153 characters per segment.
- UCS-2 single segment: up to approximately 70 characters.
- UCS-2 concatenated segment: approximately 67 characters per segment.

Treat these as planning estimates, not guaranteed carrier billing.

The final count must be checked in the actual sending platform before launch.

For every message provide:

- character count including spaces;
- expected encoding;
- estimated number of segments;
- characters causing Unicode encoding, when identifiable;
- shorter alternative when the message exceeds {{maximumSegments}}.

Do not transliterate the recipient’s language merely to reduce segment cost unless explicitly requested.

Do not remove accents, diacritics, or meaningful characters when doing so would damage clarity or correctness.

Do not alter names, legal terms, product names, or safety information to save characters.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the campaign, silently verify that:

- the campaign type is correct;
- promotional consent is confirmed or the campaign is blocked;
- opted-out recipients are excluded;
- sender identity is clear;
- every message has one purpose;
- every CTA leads to an approved destination;
- opt-out wording is appropriate for the sender and jurisdiction;
- transactional messages contain no unauthorized promotion;
- no price, offer, deadline, feature, result, personal action, or link was invented;
- personalization fields are confirmed and have safe fallbacks;
- sensitive data is not exposed;
- quiet hours and recipient-local timing are considered;
- sequence messages stop after conversion or opt-out;
- character counts include spaces;
- expected encoding and segments are estimated;
- Unicode-triggering characters are flagged;
- required legal wording is not removed to reduce length;
- restricted content is flagged;
- urgency is based on a real condition;
- the campaign does not resemble spam or phishing;
- final legal, carrier, and sending-platform review is requested where necessary.
