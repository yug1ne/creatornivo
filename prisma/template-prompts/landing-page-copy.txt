TASK

Build a coherent landing page that moves the reader from recognition of the problem to confidence in the offer and then to one clear next step. Adapt the message hierarchy to the offer type, audience awareness, conversion path, objections, and selected length. Include a hero, clear value proposition, benefits, offer explanation, objection handling, and final CTA. Add pricing, proof, urgency, comparisons, privacy language, and FAQ only when relevant and supported.

BLANK OPTIONAL FIELDS

Treat blank optional fields as unavailable, not as permission to guess. Infer only neutral choices such as section order, transitions, and a reasonable presentation style. Omit unsupported claims, pricing, deadlines, guarantees, statistics, testimonials, certifications, client names, legal statements, and feature details. Never write “not provided” in public copy. When a missing fact is necessary before publication, mention it only in Editorial Verification Notes using a brief instruction such as “[Add verified price].”

POSITIONING AND CONVERSION

* Make the audience feel understood without exaggerating fears or pretending to know personal facts.
* Express the main promise as a credible value proposition, not a guaranteed result.
* Translate supplied features into practical benefits. Do not invent benefits that are not reasonably supported.
* Use differentiators only when specific. Avoid unsupported “best,” “leading,” or superiority claims.
* Use alternatives or competitor context to clarify positioning, not to attack competitors or create misleading comparisons.
* Match the awareness level:

  * Unaware or problem-aware: explain the problem before presenting the offer.
  * Solution-aware: emphasize category value and differentiators.
  * Product-aware or most-aware: reduce explanation and emphasize fit, terms, proof, and action.
* Address objections calmly. Do not use pressure, shame, fear, or false inevitability.
* Keep one primary conversion action dominant. Use a secondary CTA only when supplied and helpful.

SECTION RULES

Hero

* Headline target: usually 6–14 words.
* Subheadline target: usually 18–40 words.
* CTA label target: usually 2–5 words.
* Clarify what the offer is, who it helps, and why it matters.
* Avoid vague hype and unsupported superlatives.

Problem and solution

* Describe only the supplied friction.
* Explain the offer’s mechanism or approach in plain language.
* Connect the offer to the desired outcome without implying certainty.
* Avoid shame-based or fear-based framing.

Benefits and features

* Prioritize three to six distinct benefits.
* Give each benefit a short heading and concise explanation.
* Include only supplied features or deliverables.
* Preserve material limitations and conditions.
* Do not merely rename features as benefits.

How it works

* Use three to five steps only when the process is known.
* If the process is missing, create a simpler “What happens next” section based only on the selected conversion path.

Proof

* Use testimonials, ratings, results, client names, certifications, statistics, awards, partnerships, or media mentions only when supplied in proof details.
* Preserve attribution, numbers, dates, and qualifiers.
* Never turn paraphrased feedback into a quotation.
* When proof is unavailable, omit the section instead of creating “trusted by” language.
* When {{proofAvailable}} is “Off” or {{proofDetails}} is blank, do not create proof strips, testimonials, logos, reviews, case studies, metrics, ratings, or trust badges.

Pricing and urgency

* State only supplied prices, billing periods, discount conditions, trial terms, refunds, eligibility rules, deadlines, quantities, or capacity.
* Do not calculate savings unless the required numbers are supplied and unambiguous.
* Do not call something “free” when material payment or renewal conditions apply.
* Use urgency only when the time-sensitive setting is enabled and concrete details are supplied.
* Never invent countdowns, “last chance,” “selling fast,” or limited availability.
* If urgency details are insufficient, omit urgency and flag the missing fact in Editorial Verification Notes.
* When {{timeSensitiveOffer}} is disabled, blank, or unsupported by {{deadlineOrScarcityDetails}}, do not create urgency, deadlines, scarcity, limited-seat language, or pressure CTAs.

CTA

* Match the CTA to the primary goal and conversion path.
* When the CTA field is Auto or blank, choose a clear action suited to the goal and conversion path.
* Do not use deceptive labels that hide what happens next.
* Use the destination URL only as a reference; never invent or modify a URL.
* Keep any secondary CTA subordinate.

FAQ

* Include 5–8 questions when FAQ mode is Include.
* In Auto mode, include 4–6 questions only when objections, delivery, eligibility, pricing, privacy, setup, or purchase uncertainty justify them.
* Omit the FAQ when mode is Omit.
* Base every answer only on supplied facts.
* Do not create legal, medical, refund, security, or compatibility promises.

STYLE

Write in the selected output language. Follow the selected brand voice and supplied examples, but do not imitate a living writer or public figure. Preserve required terminology. Use short paragraphs, informative headings, purposeful bullets, varied sentences, and restrained emphasis. Keep the writing natural, specific, useful, and mobile-scannable. Avoid filler, corporate jargon, fake conversational intimacy, repeated sentence patterns, and familiar AI clichés.

USER RESTRICTIONS AND DISCLOSURES — HARD EXCLUSIONS

Treat {{restrictionsAndDisclosures}} as hard user restrictions and required disclosure instructions, not optional style notes. Do not soften this as “respect,” “consider,” or “try to avoid.”

When the field lists words, phrases, claims, or wording to avoid:

* do not use those exact words or phrases anywhere in the package (hero, sections, FAQ, CTA, SEO metadata, social metadata, or editorial notes);
* do not use close variants that keep the same banned wording (for example, if “streamline” is prohibited, do not write “streamlined” or “streamlining” unless a different form was explicitly allowed);
* do not repeat prohibited wording verbatim in editorial notes; describe any concern generically when a warning is needed;
* replace banned wording with grounded substitutes that still match only the supplied offer facts.

When the field requires disclosures, qualifications, or required wording, include them clearly and proportionately near the relevant claim or conversion point. Do not hide required disclosures.

ACCURACY, ETHICS, PRIVACY, AND COMPLIANCE

Never invent or embellish facts, statistics, studies, testimonials, reviews, ratings, identities, case studies, performance, prices, discounts, deadlines, capabilities, certifications, awards, partnerships, approvals, personal stories, links, or sources.

Do not create guaranteed outcomes, fake urgency or scarcity, fabricated proof, deceptive before-and-after claims, hidden advertising, misleading comparisons, manipulative fear or shame, or unsupported safety, security, compliance, approval, risk-free, or superiority claims. Do not imply research, testing, verification, review, or legal approval unless supplied.

Do not invent conversion, growth, engagement, or sales promises such as “increase sales,” “boost conversions,” “drive engagement,” “guaranteed growth,” or similar outcome claims unless the exact claim is supplied and allowed. Prefer grounded wording about what the offer includes and how it can help the audience based only on supplied facts.

For medical, health, legal, financial, employment, insurance, safety, political, child-related, or other regulated topics:

* use cautious factual wording;
* respect the supplied jurisdiction;
* avoid personalized professional advice, diagnosis, and guaranteed results;
* preserve supplied disclosures;
* recommend authoritative verification only when genuinely necessary;
* keep disclaimers relevant and concise.

For lead forms, accounts, downloads, trials, purchases, or other personal-data collection:

* use the supplied privacy or data-use notes;
* do not claim anonymity, encryption, non-sharing, or legal compliance unless explicitly supported;
* do not request sensitive data unless necessary and lawfully justified;
* use clear consent wording when supplied.
* Do not turn {{privacyOrDataNotes}} or {{restrictionsAndDisclosures}} into broader legal, security, compliance, privacy, or data-protection guarantees.

SEO AND METADATA

After the page copy, create:

* SEO title;
* meta description;
* suggested slug;
* social title and social description only when they meaningfully differ.

Use the primary keyword naturally when supplied and supporting keywords only where relevant. Do not stuff keywords or distort grammar. Align metadata with the actual offer and search intent. Use these as safe writing targets, not official hard limits:

* SEO title: approximately 50–60 characters when practical;
* meta description: approximately 140–160 characters when practical;
* slug: short, descriptive, lowercase, and hyphenated.

When keywords are blank, optimize around the offer name, audience, problem, and goal without inventing search data.

LENGTH CONTROL

Apply the selected page length:

* Auto: use the shortest length that fully explains the offer and handles the main objections.
* Concise: about 500–800 words of public landing-page copy.
* Standard: about 800–1,300 words.
* Detailed: about 1,300–2,000 words.

These are editorial targets, not platform limits. Do not pad the page. Hero alternatives, Recommended Direction, metadata, and editorial notes do not count toward the public-page range.
