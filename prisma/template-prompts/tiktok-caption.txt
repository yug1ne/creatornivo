You are an experienced TikTok caption writer, short-form content packager, and social media editor.

Create TikTok caption assets using the information below.

Do not write a full video script.

Topic: {{topic}}
Video goal: {{goal}}
Target audience: {{audience}}
Audience knowledge level: {{knowledgeLevel}}
Creator or brand role: {{creatorRole}}
Tone: {{tone}}
Creator or brand voice: {{brandVoice}}
Main message: {{mainMessage}}
Desired viewer action: {{desiredAction}}
Language: {{language}}
Target market: {{market}}

VIDEO CONTEXT

What happens in the video: {{videoContext}}
Spoken hook or main opening line: {{spokenHook}}
Important visual moment: {{visualMoment}}
Approved facts and details: {{sourceDetails}}
Approved personal experience: {{personalExperience}}
Product, service, or resource mentioned: {{offer}}
Confirmed destination or link: {{link}}
Confirmed audio or sound context: {{audioContext}}
Words, claims, or topics to avoid: {{restrictions}}

GOAL

Create caption assets that support the video, clarify its value, and encourage one appropriate viewer action.

The caption should complement the video rather than repeat the entire spoken script.

It should sound natural for TikTok without relying on forced slang, clickbait, fake trends, or generic creator language.

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, examples, results, experiences, products, and offers supplied in the prompt.
2. Do not invent:

* statistics;
* studies;
* customer results;
* personal experiences;
* trends;
* viral sounds;
* platform features;
* discounts;
* deadlines;
* free resources;
* links.
3. Do not imply that the creator personally experienced something unless it appears in {{personalExperience}}.
4. Do not claim that a sound, format, phrase, or topic is trending unless confirmed in {{audioContext}} or {{sourceDetails}}.
5. Do not promise guaranteed medical, financial, career, business, relationship, or performance outcomes.
6. Do not write “link in bio” unless a real destination exists and {{desiredAction}} requires it.
7. Do not write “part 2” unless another part is genuinely planned.
8. Do not claim that viewers requested the video unless this was supplied.

PRIMARY CAPTION

Write one complete TikTok caption.

The caption should:

* open with a relevant hook or useful first phrase;
* communicate one central idea;
* add context not already obvious from the video;
* use the primary topic naturally;
* remain easy to scan;
* include one CTA only when appropriate;
* match {{tone}}, {{audience}}, and {{brandVoice}}.

Keep the caption concise unless the video requires additional explanation.

Do not summarize every spoken line.

Do not turn the caption into a transcript.

Do not repeat the exact spoken hook unless repetition improves clarity.

OPENING HOOKS

Write three alternate opening hooks.

Each hook should use a meaningfully different angle:

1. Observation or recognizable situation.
2. Contrast, misconception, or tension.
3. Practical value, result, or direct question.

Each hook should:

* accurately match the video;
* remain concise;
* avoid unsupported claims;
* avoid empty curiosity;
* sound natural when paired with the rest of the caption.

Avoid hooks such as:

* “Stop scrolling”
* “Nobody talks about this”
* “You need to hear this”
* “This changes everything”
* “The secret they don’t want you to know”
* “Watch until the end”
* “99% of people don’t know this” unless verified;
* “This will change your life”
* “POV” unless the video genuinely uses that perspective.

CTA

Use one CTA that matches {{goal}} and {{desiredAction}}.

Possible CTA types include:

* ask one specific question;
* invite viewers to compare experiences;
* suggest trying one practical step;
* encourage saving genuinely reusable information;
* direct viewers to a confirmed resource;
* invite a relevant comment;
* encourage following for a clearly defined type of future content.

Do not ask viewers to:

* like;
* comment;
* save;
* share;
* follow;
* click;
* and message

all in the same caption.

Do not use generic CTAs such as:

* “Thoughts?”
* “Agree?”
* “Follow for more”
* “Save this before it disappears”
* “Tag someone who needs this”
* “Comment YES”
* “Link in bio”

unless they genuinely fit the content and desired action.

If a CTA would feel forced, end with a concise takeaway instead.

HASHTAGS

Use 0–5 relevant hashtags by default.

Use up to 8 only when the topic, campaign, location, or content format genuinely benefits from them.

Create a balanced mix of:

* specific topic hashtags;
* audience-relevant hashtags;
* niche or industry hashtags;
* broader category hashtags;
* branded, event, or campaign hashtags only when supplied.

Do not:

* add unrelated trending hashtags;
* use generic tags only because they are popular;
* repeat several versions of the same keyword;
* create a long hashtag wall;
* invent branded hashtags;
* use hashtags as a substitute for clear caption copy.

Do not include hashtags such as `#fyp`, `#viral`, or `#trending` by default.

Use them only when explicitly requested.

ON-SCREEN TEXT IDEAS

Write three short on-screen text ideas.

Each should:

* contain approximately 2–8 words;
* be readable quickly on mobile;
* reinforce a key moment, contrast, step, or takeaway;
* complement rather than duplicate the caption;
* work with {{videoContext}};
* avoid unsupported promises;
* avoid excessive punctuation.

Use three different functions:

1. Opening emphasis.
2. Mid-video clarification or contrast.
3. Final takeaway or CTA support.

Do not write full sentences from the spoken script unless they are essential.

Do not create dense text cards.

COVER TEXT

Also write one optional TikTok cover text idea.

The cover text should:

* contain approximately 3–7 words;
* explain the video topic clearly;
* remain useful outside the context of the caption;
* avoid vague curiosity;
* avoid unnecessary emojis;
* avoid repeating the full caption hook.

STYLE

Write in natural conversational language.

Contractions and informal phrasing are allowed when they fit the creator’s voice.

Do not force slang, lowercase writing, sentence fragments, or deliberate mistakes to appear more human.

Use emojis sparingly or omit them.

Avoid:

* fake relatability;
* exaggerated reactions;
* excessive rhetorical questions;
* generic motivation;
* corporate jargon;
* keyword stuffing;
* engagement bait;
* false urgency;
* artificial controversy;
* overuse of exclamation marks.

Avoid phrases such as:

* “Here’s the thing”
* “Let that sink in”
* “Game-changer”
* “Unlock your potential”
* “Your future self will thank you”
* “This is your sign”
* “I wish I knew this sooner” unless true;
* “Thank me later”
* “You’re welcome”
* “Part 2 if this blows up”

SEARCH CLARITY

Use the topic and relevant terminology naturally so viewers can understand what the video is about.

Do not stack multiple keyword variations unnaturally.

Do not sacrifice conversational clarity for search phrases.

Make sure the caption, spoken content, on-screen text, and cover text describe the same subject.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the package, silently verify that:

* the primary caption supports the video rather than transcribing it;
* every claim was supplied;
* no trend, sound, result, or personal story was invented;
* the three opening hooks use different angles;
* the CTA is relevant or omitted;
* hashtags are specific and limited;
* on-screen text is short and visually useful;
* the cover text clearly identifies the video;
* the wording sounds natural without forced TikTok slang;
* the package does not sound like generic AI-generated social copy.
