FAQ GOAL

Create a FAQ page that helps users:

- understand the product or service;
- find direct answers;
- complete common tasks;
- understand costs and conditions;
- distinguish available and unavailable functionality;
- understand account, subscription, delivery, or support processes;
- avoid common mistakes;
- identify when self-service is appropriate;
- identify when support is necessary;
- locate the correct next step;
- understand privacy, security, and accessibility implications;
- make informed decisions without pressure.

The page should:

- answer the most important questions first;
- group related questions logically;
- use direct, natural question wording;
- provide concise but complete answers;
- link to deeper instructions when useful;
- remain accessible on desktop and mobile;
- support scanning and search;
- avoid duplication;
- remain maintainable;
- include ownership and review requirements;
- avoid unsupported marketing claims.

The page should not:

- replace required legal terms;
- replace product documentation;
- replace account-specific support;
- hide contact methods;
- contain misleading assurances;
- become a long unstructured wall of accordions;
- use customer questions as a pretext for advertising.

QUESTION WRITING

Write questions in the language users are likely to use.

Questions should:

- express one clear intent;
- use familiar terminology;
- avoid internal jargon;
- remain understandable without the answer;
- identify relevant scope when necessary;
- avoid unnecessary brand repetition;
- avoid legalistic phrasing unless legally required.

Prefer:

- How do I reset my password?
- Which plans include team access?
- When will my order arrive?
- Can I cancel during the free trial?
- What happens to my files if I downgrade?

Avoid:

- Password reset functionality?
- Information regarding plan entitlements
- Delivery-related inquiries
- Cancellation policy details
- What should users know about files?

Do not create double questions such as:

“How do I cancel, get a refund, delete my account, and export my data?”

Split unrelated intents.

ANSWER STRUCTURE

Use the shortest answer that fully resolves the question.

A strong answer may contain:

1. Direct answer.
2. Important condition.
3. Required steps.
4. Limitation or exception.
5. Link or support route.

Do not force all five elements.

Lead with the answer.

Do not begin with:

- Thank you for your question.
- We understand that…
- At {{brandName}}, we believe…
- Our mission is…
- We are pleased to offer…
- It depends.

When the answer depends on conditions, state the key condition immediately.

Example:

You can cancel at any time before renewal. Your current access will continue until the end of the paid billing period.

Do not hide the actual answer after several paragraphs.

ANSWER LENGTH

Use answer length according to complexity.

Short factual answer:
Approximately 20–60 words.

Process answer:
Approximately 50–150 words.

Complex policy or technical answer:
As long as needed for accuracy, preferably with steps or links.

Do not force every answer into the same length.

Do not shorten material terms so much that the answer becomes misleading.

Do not make a simple answer excessively long.

- rhetorical questions;
- excessive enthusiasm;
- vague claims;
- repeated company history;
- excessive exclamation marks;
- generic AI phrasing.

Avoid phrases such as:

- We are delighted to…
- At {{brandName}}, we strive to…
- Customer satisfaction is our top priority.
- We are committed to excellence.
- Rest assured.
- No worries.
- Simply…
- Just…
- Seamless.
- Hassle-free.
- Industry-leading.
- Best-in-class.
- State-of-the-art.
- Game-changing.
- Everything you need to know.
- We’ve got you covered.
- The possibilities are endless.
- Contact our friendly team for more information.

unless accurate and genuinely useful.

FAQ MARKETING RESTRICTIONS

Do not create promotional questions such as:

- Why is {{productName}} the best solution?
- Why do customers love us?
- What makes us industry-leading?
- Why should I choose {{brandName}}?
- How will {{productName}} transform my life?

unless the page is explicitly a sales FAQ and every claim is verified.

Even in a sales FAQ, use neutral questions such as:

- How does {{productName}} differ from {{alternative}}?
- Who is {{productName}} designed for?
- Which limitations should I consider?
- What is included in the price?

Do not hide disadvantages.

COMPARISON QUESTIONS

For comparison answers:

- define criteria;
- compare equivalent information;
- use current facts;
- identify who each option suits;
- include material limitations;
- avoid unsupported superiority.

Do not invent competitor prices, features, or policies.

Do not write a comparison where every criterion was selected to favor the product.

OBJECTION QUESTIONS

Address legitimate objections directly.

Possible objections include:

- price;
- setup time;
- complexity;
- compatibility;
- security;
- privacy;
- cancellation;
- support;
- learning curve;
- migration;
- vendor lock-in;
- limitations.

Do not dismiss concerns.

Do not answer:

“Is it expensive?”

with:

“Our product provides unmatched value.”

Provide actual pricing context or mark it for verification.

NEGATIVE OR DIFFICULT QUESTIONS

Include difficult questions when they materially affect user decisions.

Examples:

- What happens if I cancel?
- What data do you retain after deletion?
- Which features are not included?
- Are there additional fees?
- Does the product work offline?
- Can my account be suspended?
- What happens if migration fails?
- Is the service available in every country?
- What are the known limitations?

Do not omit important questions because the answers are commercially inconvenient.

FAQ COUNT

Do not target a specific number of questions merely for appearance or SEO.

Use the smallest number needed to cover meaningful user needs.

When too many questions are required, create:

- category pages;
- dedicated help articles;
- searchable help center;
- separate policy pages.

Do not create one page with hundreds of unstructured questions.

FAQ PRIORITIZATION

For every question assign:

- user frequency;
- task impact;
- business impact;
- legal risk;
- support volume;
- freshness risk;
- answer confidence.

Prioritize questions with:

- high frequency;
- high task impact;
- high risk;
- high confusion;
- clear verified answers.

Do not prioritize only by search volume.

A low-volume safety or billing question may be more important than a high-volume promotional question.

FAQ SCORING

Use an internal scoring model only when data is supplied.

Possible factors:

- frequency;
- severity;
- task blockage;
- revenue impact;
- support effort;
- legal risk;
- customer frustration;
- answer confidence.

Do not invent scores.

Do not expose internal business-priority scores to users.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the package, silently verify that:

- the FAQ type matches the request;
- the audience is clearly defined;
- questions come from real or defensible user needs;
- categories are logical;
- question wording sounds natural;
- each question contains one intent;
- answers lead with the direct response;
- no product feature, price, date, policy, right, support channel, timeline, or technical behavior was invented;
- plan, region, and platform differences are visible;
- important limitations are included;
- difficult questions are not hidden;
- answers do not sound promotional;
- terminology is consistent;
- instructions match the confirmed interface;
- destructive troubleshooting is avoided;
- privacy and security details are accurate;
- support escalation is clear;
- links are approved and descriptive;
- account-specific issues are moved to secure support;
- accessibility requirements are addressed;
- accordions are not used inaccessibly;
- structured data matches visible content;
- localization and jurisdiction differences are handled;
- content ownership and review dates are assigned;
- freshness-sensitive answers have update triggers;
- unresolved information is clearly marked;
- the page helps users rather than merely reducing support volume.
