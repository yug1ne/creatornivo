GOAL
Write an FAQ section that helps a potential customer understand the product, decide whether it fits their needs, get started, and resolve common concerns.
The FAQ should reduce uncertainty without sounding promotional, defensive, or artificially reassuring.
QUESTION SELECTION
Create 8–10 useful question-and-answer pairs.
Cover the most relevant topics from the following list:
what the product is;
who it is designed for;
what problem it solves;
how it works;
key requirements;
setup and getting started;
pricing;
free plan or trial availability;
common objections or concerns;
limitations;
cancellation or refunds;
security or privacy, when relevant;
integrations or compatibility, when relevant;
customer support.
Do not force every topic into the FAQ if the supplied product information does not support it.
Prioritize questions that a real potential customer would ask before signing up, purchasing, or contacting support.
Avoid questions that merely repeat headings or marketing claims already explained elsewhere on the page.
ACCURACY RULES


Use only the product information supplied in the prompt.
Do not invent prices, discounts, free trials, guarantees, features, integrations, certifications, response times, refund terms, customer numbers, or support channels.
Do not assume the product has a feature simply because similar products usually offer it.
If required information is missing, write a neutral answer that directs the user to check the current product details or contact support.
Do not present estimates, planned features, or possible outcomes as confirmed facts.
Do not promise guaranteed results.
Clearly mention important limitations, requirements, exclusions, or extra costs when they are relevant.
For medical, financial, legal, security, or safety-related products, avoid definitive claims and use appropriately careful language.
QUESTION STYLE
Write questions in natural language that real users might type into search engines, support chat, or a website search box.
Use a mix of question patterns where natural, such as:


“What is {{productName}}?”
“How does {{productName}} work?”
“Who is {{productName}} for?”
“How much does {{productName}} cost?”
“Do I need any technical experience?”
“Can I cancel at any time?”
“What happens if I need help?”
Do not awkwardly insert the product name into every question.
Do not stuff keywords into questions.
Avoid overly formal wording such as:
“What are the primary functionalities of…”
“How may one commence utilization of…”
“What advantages does the solution provide…”
ANSWER STYLE


Keep each answer as short as the question allows, usually 1–4 sentences.
Answer the question directly in the first sentence.
Add explanation, conditions, or next steps only when useful.
Use clear, conversational language appropriate for {{audience}}.
Explain technical terms in plain language.
Be reassuring through clarity and honesty, not through exaggerated promises.
Mention limitations or conditions openly when they affect the answer.
Avoid repeating the same product description, benefit, or CTA in multiple answers.
Do not begin every answer with the product name.
Do not end every answer with “contact us,” “learn more,” or another generic CTA.
Use contractions when they fit the selected language and tone.
Avoid excessive enthusiasm, exclamation marks, and promotional adjectives.
AVOID GENERIC AI LANGUAGE
Do not use phrases such as:


“In today’s fast-paced world”
“Whether you’re a beginner or an expert”
“The short answer is yes”
“Absolutely!”
“Rest assured”
“We’ve got you covered”
“Seamless experience”
“Powerful solution”
“Revolutionary platform”
“Unlock the full potential”
“Take your experience to the next level”
“It’s important to note that”
PRICING QUESTIONS
When answering questions about pricing:
use only the supplied pricing information;
clearly distinguish between free, paid, recurring, one-time, and usage-based costs;
mention additional charges only when confirmed;
do not describe the product as affordable or cost-effective without supporting context;
recommend checking the pricing page for current information if prices may change.
OBJECTION QUESTIONS
Address objections honestly rather than dismissing them.
Possible objections may relate to:
difficulty of setup;
lack of technical experience;
cost;
security or privacy;
compatibility;
time required;
cancellation;
suitability for a particular use case;
limitations compared with alternatives.
Do not invent objections that are irrelevant to the supplied product.
SUPPORT QUESTIONS
Explain:
which support channels are actually available;
when support is available, if confirmed;
where users can find documentation or tutorials;
what users should include when reporting a problem, when relevant.
Do not promise response times unless they were supplied.
SEO AND USABILITY


Use natural variations of the product name, product type, and user intent.
Optimize for clarity rather than keyword density.
Make each question understandable when viewed independently.
Avoid duplicate questions with slightly different wording.
Arrange the questions in a logical order:
   * basic understanding;
   * suitability;
   * how it works;
   * getting started;
   * pricing and policies;
   * objections and limitations;
   * support.
Keep answers suitable for collapsible FAQ accordions.
Do not include unnecessary introductions or conclusions unless requested.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the FAQ, silently verify that:


every factual statement is supported by the supplied information;
no price, policy, feature, or promise was invented;
the questions reflect real customer intent;
the most important buying concerns are addressed;
answers are direct and easy to scan;
limitations are not hidden;
the product name is not repeated unnaturally;
answers do not sound like advertisements;
no two questions cover essentially the same issue;
the section contains 8–10 useful Q&A pairs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

Output only the completed FAQ section. Do not describe your process or explain your choices.
