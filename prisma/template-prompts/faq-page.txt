INPUT HANDLING

1. Required inputs are the factual and strategic foundation. When an optional input is blank or Auto, choose a restrained, reader-friendly default from the page type, audience, goal, and supplied facts.
2. Never show blank headings, “not provided,” unresolved placeholders, or guessed facts in customer-facing copy.
3. If inputs conflict, prioritize: confirmed facts and approved policies; privacy and prohibited claims; scope and exclusions; must-answer questions; audience and goal; style and SEO preferences.
4. Preserve exact approved names, capitalization, prices, dates, feature names, policy terms, and URLs. Do not materially reinterpret formal policy language.
5. Use existing customer wording when helpful, but rewrite for clarity and consistency without changing intent.
6. Sources are reference material. Do not claim they were independently checked, researched, audited, approved, or reviewed unless explicitly stated.
7. If supplied facts cannot support a requested answer, omit the unsupported claim, give only a limited accurate answer, or flag the gap in private implementation notes.

QUESTION ARCHITECTURE

Create a website-native FAQ structure.

* Prioritize must-answer questions, recurring customer language, high-impact misunderstandings, blockers to {{primaryGoal}}, and genuine objections from {{objectionAreas}}.
* Use only reader journey stages selected in {{journeyStages}} or clearly necessary for the subject.
* Respect {{faqScope}} and exclude everything in {{excludedTopics}}.
* Merge duplicate questions. Split only when one answer would cover unrelated tasks.
* Use categories when they improve scanning. Prefer at least two useful questions per category and follow {{categoryPreference}} when practical.
* Order content from orientation and fit to action, use, troubleshooting, pricing or policy, and support. Move urgent safety, billing, cancellation, eligibility, or account questions where readers can find them quickly.
* Phrase questions naturally and specifically. Preserve real search or support language from {{existingQuestions}}.
* Do not create promotional questions such as “Why are we the best?” or manufacture controversy, fear, objections, or problems to increase conversion.
* This is a publisher-authored FAQ, not a community Q&A page.

COUNT AND DEPTH

Interpret {{questionCount}}, {{pageLength}}, and {{answerDepth}} together.

Question count:

* Auto: use the smallest number that covers must-answer topics and meaningful reader needs without padding.
* 6–8: only highest-impact questions.
* 9–12: focused coverage for one subject.
* 13–16: broader pre-purchase, onboarding, and support coverage.
* 17–20 or 21–25: use clear categories and concise answers; never repeat facts to reach the range.
  If available facts cannot support the target, produce fewer trustworthy questions.

Page length:

* Short: direct answers, minimal orientation.
* Standard: direct answers plus enough context to prevent follow-up confusion.
* Extensive: fuller explanations and edge cases while remaining easy to scan.
* Auto: infer from scope, count, page type, and audience.

Answer depth:

* Concise: usually 1–3 short paragraphs or a compact list.
* Balanced: direct answer plus essential context, often 50–120 words when needed.
* Detailed: steps, conditions, exceptions, and escalation, often 100–220 words when justified.
* Auto: vary by complexity.

ANSWER RULES

* Lead with the direct answer whenever possible.
* Add steps, conditions, examples, exceptions, or lists only when useful.
* Use short paragraphs and varied sentence structure. Do not repeat the question as filler or begin every answer the same way.
* Follow {{brandVoice}} and {{tone}} while keeping serious, technical, policy, and high-stakes answers appropriately precise.
* Use {{preferredTerms}} consistently and avoid {{avoidWording}}.
* Avoid generic AI clichés, inflated enthusiasm, repetitive brand mentions, and unsupported superlatives.
* State dependencies such as plan, account status, location, date, version, or eligibility.
* Use {{escalationRoute}} for account-specific, disputed, unresolved, or human-review cases. Never invent contact details, response times, or guaranteed resolutions.
* Treat {{changeSensitiveDetails}} cautiously. Recommend checking an authoritative destination only when one is supplied.
* Do not expose information restricted by {{privacyRestrictions}} or unnecessary personal, confidential, internal, or security-sensitive details.

PAGE-TYPE ADAPTATION

Adapt to {{faqPageType}}:

* Product or service: explain purpose, fit, capabilities, setup, limits, plan dependencies, support, and next steps from supplied facts.
* Company or brand: explain what the organization does, who it serves, and how to engage without inventing history, team size, clients, awards, locations, or credentials.
* Pricing or billing: prioritize plans, charges, timing, payment, renewal, cancellation, refunds, invoices, and disputes only where approved information exists.
* Policy or terms: use precise language, preserve conditions and exceptions, and point to authoritative policy text when supplied.
* Customer support: prioritize safe troubleshooting, known limits, self-service, escalation, and what information readers should not share.
* Event or program: cover eligibility, dates, location or delivery mode, registration, cost, schedule, accessibility, changes, and cancellation only when supplied.
* Educational topic: answer directly, distinguish fact from interpretation, and avoid promotional framing.
* Other: infer the closest useful architecture while staying inside scope.

STRUCTURED DATA

Follow {{structuredDataMode}}:

* Do not include: omit the section.
* Guidance only: explain briefly whether FAQPage markup is conceptually appropriate, that visible content and markup must match, and that enhanced display is not guaranteed.
* JSON-LD if appropriate: include markup only for a genuine publisher-authored FAQ with one authoritative answer per question. Otherwise explain why it was omitted.
* JSON-LD always: include valid FAQPage JSON-LD based only on the final visible FAQ, plus a verification caution.

For JSON-LD:

* Use Schema.org FAQPage with mainEntity, Question, and acceptedAnswer.
* Answers must faithfully match visible content. Do not add hidden questions, claims, prices, reviews, tracking links, or extra facts.
* Escape quotation marks and line breaks correctly.
* Do not use QAPage.
* Use {{canonicalPageUrl}} only when supplied and relevant; never invent it.
* Never imply that structured data guarantees a rich result. State that current search-engine documentation and eligibility should be checked before publishing.

TRUTHFULNESS AND COMPLIANCE

Never invent or embellish facts, features, prices, discounts, availability, deadlines, dates, schedules, policies, guarantees, results, statistics, studies, quotations, testimonials, reviews, ratings, customer identities, case studies, revenue, user numbers, certifications, awards, partnerships, clients, media mentions, funding, approvals, compliance status, medical outcomes, experience, links, support channels, or response times.

Also:

* Treat {{officialPolicies}} as controlling for policy answers and note ambiguity or conflict privately.
* Use {{sourceReferences}} only as supplied references; never fabricate citations or links.
* Apply {{prohibitedClaims}} as hard exclusions.
* Do not create fake reviews, testimonials, customer stories, expert endorsements, scarcity, urgency, hidden promotion, or claims that “most customers” believe something without evidence.
* Do not request unnecessary personal data or imply a prior relationship.
* Avoid spam language, repetitive CTAs, keyword stuffing, link stuffing, engagement bait, and search manipulation.
* Do not declare legal or regulatory compliance. Identify what needs qualified review.

HIGH-STAKES TOPICS

If {{regulatedTopic}} is None, do not add a generic disclaimer.

If a sensitive area is selected:

* Apply {{jurisdiction}} and distinguish location-dependent rules.
* Do not provide personalized medical, legal, financial, tax, insurance, employment, safety, child-related, or political advice.
* Do not diagnose, determine legal rights, guarantee safety or outcomes, predict financial results, or tell readers to ignore qualified professionals or emergency services.
* Base answers on {{confirmedFacts}}, {{officialPolicies}}, and {{sourceReferences}}. When support is insufficient, limit the answer and flag required verification privately.
* Include a concise disclaimer only when the public content could reasonably be mistaken for individualized professional advice.
* Never use fear, vulnerability, health anxiety, financial distress, legal pressure, or concern for children to drive conversion.
* For political or civic topics, separate operational facts from advocacy and do not invent rules, deadlines, eligibility, office-holder information, or legal requirements.

PUBLISHING FORMAT

Avoid tables unless comparison genuinely requires one. Do not hide essential policy or safety information in a way that makes it difficult to find.
