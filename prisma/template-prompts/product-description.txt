## Platform Adaptation

Adapt the product description to the selected platform.

Possible platforms may include:

* E-commerce product page
* Online marketplace
* Amazon-style listing
* Etsy-style listing
* Shopify store
* WooCommerce store
* Product catalog
* Landing page
* Social commerce page
* Mobile shopping application
* B2B product page
* Digital product marketplace
* App or software directory
* Subscription product page

Do not use the same structure for every platform.

For example:

* Marketplace listings should be highly scannable and specification-focused.
* Brand-store descriptions may include more storytelling and product positioning.
* B2B descriptions should emphasize practical value, compatibility, implementation, and business use cases.
* Luxury-product descriptions may use more refined language while remaining specific.
* Technical products should prioritize clarity, specifications, compatibility, and limitations.
* Everyday consumer products should emphasize usability, convenience, and real-life benefits.
* Digital products should explain access, format, delivery, compatibility, licensing, and required software.
* Handmade products should clearly explain materials, production method, possible variations, and care.
* Subscription products should explain billing frequency, included benefits, renewal terms, and cancellation conditions when provided.

## Core Requirements

Write a product description that:

* Identifies the product clearly in the opening.
* Explains its main purpose without unnecessary buildup.
* Focuses on customer-relevant benefits rather than listing features alone.
* Connects each important feature to a practical use or outcome.
* Uses specific information from the product details.
* Helps the customer imagine using the product in a realistic situation.
* Addresses important concerns or objections when information is available.
* Makes technical information easy to understand.
* Remains accurate and does not exaggerate performance.
* Uses a logical hierarchy suitable for mobile and desktop reading.
* Avoids filler, repetition, vague praise, and excessive promotional language.
* Includes the most important purchasing information.
* Clearly distinguishes confirmed facts from missing information.
* Does not hide relevant limitations.
* Uses the requested tone consistently.
* Ends with one clear and appropriate call to action.

## Feature-to-Benefit Conversion

Do not simply repeat the supplied feature list.

For every important feature, explain:

1. What the feature is
2. What it does
3. Why it matters to the customer
4. In which situation it may be useful

Example logic:

Feature:
Adjustable height

Weak description:
“Features adjustable height.”

Improved description:
“The adjustable height allows the product to be adapted to different users, surfaces, or working positions, making everyday use more comfortable and flexible.”

Do not claim that a feature guarantees a result unless that guarantee is explicitly supported.

The first paragraph should communicate:

* Who it is suitable for
* Its primary practical value

Avoid generic openings such as:

* “Introducing our revolutionary product.”
* “Take your experience to the next level.”
* “In today’s fast-paced world.”
* “Designed with you in mind.”
* “Say goodbye to all your problems.”
* “This game-changing product is a must-have.”
* “Whether you are a beginner or a professional.”

Do not begin with empty praise or exaggerated claims.

## Benefit Hierarchy

Prioritize benefits in this order:

1. Primary customer outcome
2. Ease of use
3. Practical functionality
4. Comfort or convenience
5. Compatibility or flexibility
6. Quality or construction
7. Maintenance
8. Secondary features

Change this order when the product category requires a different priority.

Do not give equal space to every minor feature.

## Tone and Style

Follow the requested tone of voice.

Unless otherwise specified, the description should be:

* Clear
* Specific
* Helpful
* Persuasive without pressure
* Professional without sounding corporate
* Confident without exaggeration
* Easy to scan
* Natural and human
* Focused on real customer value

Use varied sentence lengths and natural transitions.

Prefer concrete verbs and nouns over vague adjectives.

Use words such as “may,” “helps,” “allows,” “supports,” or “designed to” when a result is not guaranteed.

Avoid excessive use of:

* Best
* Perfect
* Ultimate
* Revolutionary
* Unmatched
* Unbeatable
* Incredible
* Amazing
* Premium
* Innovative
* Cutting-edge
* State-of-the-art

## SEO Requirements

When SEO keywords are provided:

* Include the primary keyword naturally in the product title or opening paragraph when appropriate.
* Include secondary keywords only where they fit naturally.
* Use natural variations and related terminology.
* Prioritize readability and conversion over keyword density.
* Avoid keyword stuffing.
* Do not repeat the exact product name unnaturally.
* Do not add irrelevant search terms.
* Do not make unsupported claims merely to include a keyword.

The description should remain useful even when the SEO keywords are removed.

## Formatting Requirements

Use a structure appropriate for an e-commerce product page.

Recommended structure:

### Product Title

Create a clear title that identifies the product and its main differentiating detail.

### Short Product Summary

Write a concise summary of approximately 40–80 words unless another length is requested.

### Main Product Description

Explain:

* What the product is
* Who it is for
* The main problem it addresses
* The primary benefit
* How it fits into the customer’s life or workflow

### Key Benefits

Provide 4–7 benefit-led bullet points.

Each bullet should begin with a meaningful benefit or feature and include a short explanation.

Avoid one- or two-word bullets.

### Key Features

Present the most important factual features in a clear, scannable format.

Do not duplicate the Key Benefits section word for word.

### How to Use

Provide clear usage instructions when relevant and when information has been supplied.

### Suitable For

Describe the most relevant customer types, settings, or use cases.

### Specifications

Organize confirmed specifications clearly.

Possible fields include:

* Material
* Dimensions
* Weight
* Capacity
* Color
* Size
* Model
* Compatibility
* Power requirements
* File format
* Operating system
* License type
* Package contents

Omit irrelevant fields.

### Important Information

Include relevant limitations, warnings, care instructions, compatibility notes, variation notices, or purchase considerations.

### Call to Action

End with one concise call to action appropriate to the product and platform.
