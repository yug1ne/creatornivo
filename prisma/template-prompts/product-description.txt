GOAL
Write a product description that helps the right buyer quickly understand:

what the product is;
what it helps them do;
which buyer needs it addresses;
how its features support its benefits;
what makes it different;
what conditions or limitations matter;
what the buyer should do next.
Persuade through clarity, relevance, concrete details, and verified proof rather than hype or exaggerated promises.
ACCURACY


Use only facts, features, specifications, benefits, prices, policies, ratings, reviews, and results supplied in the prompt.
Do not invent materials, dimensions, compatibility, integrations, certifications, guarantees, delivery times, stock levels, or performance results.
Do not invent discounts, free trials, warranties, return policies, bonuses, or included accessories.
Do not create fictional customer quotes, ratings, review counts, or testimonials.
Do not claim that the product guarantees a result.
Do not imply that every buyer will achieve the same outcome.
Do not describe an expected or possible benefit as a confirmed result.
Do not claim that the product is “best,” “number one,” “leading,” “premium,” “clinically proven,” or “expert-approved” unless confirmed.
Clearly mention relevant requirements, limitations, compatibility conditions, or exclusions.
Do not hide recurring costs, extra equipment, setup needs, or important restrictions when they were supplied.
FEATURES, BENEFITS, AND OUTCOMES
Distinguish clearly between:


Feature: what the product contains or does.
Benefit: how that feature helps the buyer.
Outcome: the practical result the buyer may experience.
Every benefit must be connected to a supplied feature, specification, material, service, or process.
Do not write unsupported benefits such as:
saves money;
improves productivity;
increases revenue;
eliminates stress;
guarantees comfort;
delivers professional results;
lasts for years;
unless the supplied information supports them.
Use careful wording for outcomes that depend on usage, environment, skill, health, business conditions, or other external factors.
HEADLINE
Write a headline of no more than 10 words.
The headline should:
communicate the clearest product value;
identify the main use case when useful;
sound specific rather than slogan-like;
be understandable without the product name when possible;
avoid unsupported promises;
avoid unnecessary adjectives.
Do not use vague headlines such as:
“Experience the Difference”
“Upgrade Your Life”
“Designed for Better”
“The Future Is Here”
“Everything You Need”
“A Product You’ll Love”
SUBHEADLINE
Write one concise subheadline that:
explains what the product is;
identifies who it is for when useful;
connects the key feature or method to the main benefit;
adds information instead of repeating the headline;
remains easy to scan on mobile.
BENEFIT BULLETS
Create up to three distinct benefit bullets.
Each bullet should include:


A short benefit heading.
One concise explanation.
A suggested icon or visual symbol.
Each benefit must:


address a different buyer need;
connect to a real product feature;
explain practical value;
avoid guaranteed results;
avoid repeating the same benefit with different wording.
If fewer than three confirmed benefits were supplied, use only the supported number.
Do not invent additional benefits to fill the format.
ICON SUGGESTIONS
Suggest simple, meaningful icons that communicate the benefit.
Avoid defaulting to generic icons such as:
rocket for growth;
trophy for quality;
lightning bolt for speed;
shield for every trust claim;
star for every benefit;
crown for premium quality.
Explain the icon concept briefly when needed.
Do not suggest an icon that implies a feature or guarantee the product does not have.
PRODUCT DESCRIPTION
Write two concise paragraphs.
Paragraph 1 should explain:
what the product is;
the main buyer problem it addresses;
how it works or is used;
the primary benefit.
Paragraph 2 should explain:
the most relevant features or specifications;
how they support the benefits;
what makes the product different;
any important suitability, compatibility, or usage details.
Do not repeat the headline, subheadline, and bullets word for word.
Do not list every specification unless it affects the buying decision.
Use plain language and explain technical terms where necessary.
SOCIAL PROOF
Use only approved proof.
If an approved quote was supplied, format it as:
“[Approved customer quote]”
— [Approved attribution]
If a verified rating was supplied, include the exact rating and confirmed review count.
If no approved social proof was supplied, write:
[Insert approved customer quote, verified rating, review summary, or trust signal here]
Do not generate a fictional testimonial.
Do not create realistic-looking placeholder names, companies, ratings, or quotes.
CTA
Write one CTA button label that matches {{desiredAction}}.
The CTA should:
clearly describe the next step;
use approximately 2–5 words;
match the actual commercial offer;
avoid suggesting something is free unless confirmed;

Possible CTA labels include:

Use only a CTA appropriate for the supplied product type and buying process.
STYLE
Write in a clear, natural, confident voice suitable for {{buyer}}.
Keep the copy concise, specific, and easy to scan.
Use varied sentence lengths and plain language.
Avoid:
excessive hype;
long feature lists;
keyword stuffing;
repetitive adjectives;
excessive exclamation marks;
vague lifestyle promises;
artificial urgency;
emotional manipulation;

generic motivational language.

“must-have”

“seamless experience”

FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the copy, silently verify that:
the product is clearly explained;
the headline is under 10 words;
every benefit is supported by a real feature or specification;
no benefit, metric, quote, rating, policy, or result was invented;
the three benefit bullets are genuinely distinct;
limitations and requirements are not hidden;
the description focuses on buyer value without ignoring how the product works;
the social proof is approved or shown as an explicit placeholder;
the CTA accurately describes the next step;
the copy does not sound like a generic AI product description.
