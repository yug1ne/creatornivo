GOAL

Create App Store metadata that clearly communicates:

what the app does;

who it is designed for;

which real problem it addresses;

how its confirmed features support the main benefit;

what is free and what requires payment;

which requirements or limitations matter;

why the app differs from relevant alternatives.

Prioritize clarity, relevance, localization, and factual accuracy over aggressive keyword targeting.

All metadata must describe the app’s current, reviewable functionality.

FIELD LIMITS

Follow these Apple App Store limits:

App Name: maximum 30 characters.

Subtitle: maximum 30 characters.

Promotional Text: maximum 170 characters.

Description: maximum 4000 characters.

Keyword field: maximum 100 bytes.

What’s New: maximum 4000 characters and only for updates after the initial release.

Count spaces and punctuation where applicable.

For non-ASCII keyword fields, treat the byte limit as more important than the visual character count.

ACCURACY

Use only features, results, integrations, prices, policies, credentials, and claims supplied in the prompt.

Do not invent:

app functionality;

AI capabilities;

integrations;

offline support;

cloud sync;

encryption;

privacy protections;

awards;

ratings;

review counts;

rankings;

downloads;

customer results;

professional endorsements;

regulatory approval;

free trials;

subscription terms.

Do not describe planned or beta functionality as currently available.

Clearly distinguish:

available now;

available only in a paid plan;

available in beta;

planned for a future release.

Do not claim that Apple recommends, endorses, certifies, or approves the app.

Do not imply guaranteed medical, financial, business, educational, legal, or safety outcomes.

Do not use competitor app names, unauthorized trademarks, celebrity names, or irrelevant popular terms.

Do not describe a web feature as an in-app feature unless it is available through the submitted app.

Do not mention another mobile platform unless directly relevant to an approved app feature.

Make sure the listing matches the app experience submitted for review.

APP NAME

If {{appNameStatus}} is “finalized”:

keep the supplied name unchanged;

do not generate replacement names;

verify only whether it fits the 30-character limit;

suggest a shortened version only when it exceeds the limit.

If {{appNameStatus}} is “open to suggestions”:

Create three possible app names.

Each name should:

contain no more than 30 characters;

be distinctive and easy to spell;

work as a brand rather than a keyword list;

hint at the app’s purpose when natural;

avoid generic category-only names;

avoid copying or closely imitating an existing product name;

avoid claims such as “best,” “official,” or “number one.”

Do not assume that a generated name is legally available.

Label all generated names:

Availability and trademark check required

SUBTITLE

Write one recommended subtitle and three alternate subtitles.

Each subtitle must:

contain no more than 30 characters;

clarify the app’s main function, benefit, or use case;

add information not already clear from the app name;

use natural user language;

avoid generic slogans;

avoid unverifiable claims;

avoid pricing information;

avoid competitor names.

Use meaningfully different approaches:

Direct function.

Audience or use case.

Benefit-led.

Differentiator-led.

PROMOTIONAL TEXT

Write one optional Promotional Text field of no more than 170 characters.

Use Promotional Text for:

a current confirmed feature;

a real seasonal update;

a confirmed event;

an important new capability;

a verified limited-time offer;

a concise current value message.

Do not use Promotional Text as a keyword list.

Do not invent a sale, deadline, feature, event, or upcoming release.

If there is no timely or useful promotional message, write:

No promotional text recommended for this version.

DESCRIPTION

Write one complete App Store description of no more than 4000 characters.

Use plain text only.

Recommended structure:

Opening paragraph:
Explain the app’s central value in the first sentence.

Main use case:
Explain who the app is for and what it helps them do.

Key features:
Present a short, scannable list of confirmed functionality.

Benefits:
Connect each major feature to practical user value.

Monetization and requirements:
Explain relevant subscriptions, in-app purchases, account requirements, device requirements, or limitations.

Closing:
End with a concise, accurate invitation to use the app.

DESCRIPTION RULES

Make the first sentence understandable without expanding the full description.

Use short paragraphs and a concise feature list.

Focus on the app’s current functionality.

Use terminology appropriate for {{audience}}.

Explain technical terms when necessary.

Do not stuff keywords into the prose.

Do not repeat the app name in every paragraph.

Do not include specific regional prices.

Do not claim that a paid function is free.

Clearly state when important features require a subscription or in-app purchase.

Mention account, internet, hardware, regional, or compatibility requirements when relevant.

Do not include unsupported awards or testimonials.

Do not make the description read like a landing page filled with hype.

Avoid phrases such as:

“The world’s best app”

“Number one”

“Revolutionary”

“Game-changing”

“Ultimate solution”

“Guaranteed results”

“Transform your life”

“Official app” unless accurate;

“Clinically proven” unless supported;

“Trusted by millions” unless verified.

KEYWORD FIELD

Create one ready-to-paste Apple App Store keyword field.

Requirements:

maximum 100 bytes;

separate terms with commas;

do not add spaces after commas;

spaces may be used inside genuine multi-word phrases;

prioritize relevant search terms;

use natural local-language terminology;

include useful spelling variations only when relevant;

avoid excessively broad terms.

Do not include:

words already used in the finalized app name;

words already used in the selected subtitle;

category names;

the word “app” or “game” unless genuinely necessary and permitted;

duplicate singular and plural forms;

repeated words;

competitor names;

company names already searchable with the listing;

unauthorized trademarks;

celebrity names;

irrelevant trending terms;

hashtags;

claims such as “best,” “free,” or “number one” unless factually and strategically appropriate.

Also provide:

estimated byte count;

terms excluded because they already appear in the name or subtitle;

optional backup keyword field.

Do not insert the keyword string into the public description.

Screenshot overlay text should:

add meaning rather than describe obvious UI;

avoid unsupported outcomes;

avoid dense paragraphs;

avoid tiny feature lists.

Do not invent screens, features, dashboards, metrics, or interface states that do not exist.

Do not use real private user data in screenshots.

If {{releaseStage}} is “app update”:

Requirements:

describe significant features specifically;

do not invent user feedback;

do not claim that all bugs were fixed;

do not add features that are not included in the submitted version.

For a minor maintenance update, concise wording such as performance improvements and bug fixes is acceptable when accurate.

REGULATED CLAIMS

For health or medical apps:

do not claim diagnosis, treatment, prevention, or measurement accuracy without approved evidence;

describe methodology and limitations when relevant;

include appropriate advice to consult a qualified professional when required.

For financial apps:

do not guarantee savings, income, returns, approval, debt reduction, or investment performance;

clearly distinguish educational information from regulated advice.

For safety, legal, education, or employment apps:

avoid guaranteed outcomes;

clearly state meaningful limitations;

do not overstate professional authority.

Keep product names and protected feature names unchanged when required.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the package, silently verify that:

app name options fit 30 characters;

subtitles fit 30 characters;

Promotional Text fits 170 characters;

Description fits 4000 characters;

keyword field fits 100 bytes;

What’s New is omitted for the initial version;

no award, rating, ranking, result, or endorsement was invented;

no competitor or unauthorized trademark appears;

keywords are not stuffed into the description;

screenshot text matches existing screens;

the listing sounds useful and credible rather than promotional.
