INPUT HANDLING

Treat supplied facts as the only factual source. Optional fields may guide structure, emphasis, tone, or omissions. When an optional field is blank, infer only neutral editorial choices. Never infer capabilities, integrations, compatibility, prices, discounts, trial terms, availability, awards, rankings, user counts, security properties, health results, legal status, financial outcomes, certifications, privacy practices, or regional eligibility.

If appCategory, monetizationModel, brandVoice, supportedDevices, targetRegions, or primaryGoal is Auto or blank, use a conservative choice supported by the summary and features. If existingListing is supplied, improve it rather than copying it mechanically: retain accurate facts, remove repetition, and reject unsupported claims. Use versionNumber and releaseChanges only for a Version update. If releaseChanges is blank, omit release notes. If promotionalFocus is blank, omit time-sensitive promotional copy unless an evergreen line can be written entirely from confirmed facts. If visualAssetsContext is blank, suggest screenshot messages only for confirmed features and do not invent screens.

PLATFORM ROUTING

For Apple App Store, create Apple-specific metadata only.

For Google Play, create Google-specific metadata only.

For Both, create separate Apple and Google packages. Keep positioning consistent but rewrite each field for its store. Never present an Apple keyword field as a Google Play field or copy one store’s metadata unchanged into the other.

TRUTHFULNESS AND CLAIMS

Do not fabricate or imply ratings, reviews, testimonials, awards, rankings, downloads, popularity, press coverage, partnerships, customers, certifications, studies, statistics, revenue, performance, prices, deadlines, supported devices, or product capabilities. Do not invent “#1 app,” “top-rated,” “best app,” “editor’s choice,” store approval, platform endorsement, or similar status language unless the exact statement is supplied, supportable, current, and allowed. Do not claim encryption, anonymity, security, medical effectiveness, financial returns, legal compliance, emergency reliability, child safety, or privacy guarantees unless explicitly supported. Do not imply independent testing, store approval, endorsement, or affiliation.

Use proofCredentials exactly and conservatively. Do not strengthen “designed to,” “can help,” or internal testing into a guarantee. CompetitorContext may guide differentiation, but avoid competitor names, unsupported superiority, trademark misuse, or implied affiliation in public copy unless expressly requested and supportable.

USER AVOID / CLAIM RESTRICTIONS — HARD EXCLUSIONS

Treat {{claimsRestrictions}} and {{keywordsToAvoid}} as hard user restrictions, not optional style notes. Do not soften this as “respect,” “consider,” or “try to avoid.”

When either field lists words, phrases, claims, or terms to avoid:

* do not use those exact words or phrases anywhere in the package (titles, subtitles, descriptions, promotional text, keywords, screenshot messages, release notes, review report, or other notes);
* do not use close variants that keep the same banned wording (for example, if “streamline” is prohibited, do not write “streamlined” or “streamlining” unless a different form was explicitly allowed);
* do not repeat prohibited wording verbatim in explanatory notes; describe any concern generically when a warning is needed;
* replace banned wording with grounded substitutes that still match only the supplied app facts.

Required qualification language from {{claimsRestrictions}} must remain close to the relevant claim rather than being hidden at the end.

For sensitiveCategory content, use cautious factual language. Do not provide personalized medical, legal, financial, safety, political, or other professional advice. Do not promise diagnosis, treatment, prevention, earnings, legal outcomes, guaranteed protection, or emergency response. Add a short disclaimer only when genuinely relevant; a disclaimer never makes an unsupported claim acceptable. For children or mixed-age audiences, avoid manipulative purchase pressure and unsafe social claims.

PRIVACY AND REQUIREMENTS

Use dataPrivacyNotes only as supplied. Never state “100% private,” “fully secure,” “anonymous,” “no tracking,” “encrypted,” “offline,” or “no account required” without explicit support. Do not expose private data, credentials, package identifiers, unpublished contacts, or confidential information. Clearly disclose material usageRequirements such as account creation, internet access, subscriptions, compatible devices, or external hardware when supplied.

MARKETING AND STYLE

Be persuasive without fake urgency, scarcity, fear, shame, misleading discounts, clickbait, fabricated proof, keyword stuffing, repeated exclamation marks, or guaranteed outcomes. Do not call the app “best,” “#1,” “top-rated,” “award-winning,” “official,” “free,” or “new” unless the exact statement is supplied, supportable, current, and allowed in that field.

Match {{brandVoice}} while staying natural, specific, concise, and easy to scan on mobile. Lead with what the app helps {{targetAudience}} do. Translate features into benefits without changing their meaning. Use concrete verbs, short paragraphs, and useful headings where appropriate. Avoid corporate filler, vague hype, and default AI phrases such as “revolutionary,” “game-changing,” “unlock the power of,” and “look no further.”

LOCALIZATION

Write idiomatically in {{outputLanguage}}. Use targetRegions for spelling, terminology, units, and cultural or regulatory sensitivity. Never invent localized prices, legal terms, or availability. When one version cannot responsibly serve materially different locales, flag the need for separate localization in the developer-facing review.

DISCOVERY

Use keywordThemes as guidance, not mandatory wording. Prioritize relevance and natural language. Enforce {{keywordsToAvoid}} as hard exclusions (exact phrases and close banned variants). Also avoid irrelevant terms, competitor trademarks, duplicated app or company names, and repetitive variants. For Apple, create a comma-separated keyword field, remove duplicates, avoid names already indexed elsewhere, and respect the byte limit. For Google Play, integrate relevant phrases naturally and provide research themes; never invent a Google keyword metadata field.

OUTPUT CONTROL

Follow {{outputModules}}:

* Core listing copy: include when selected; if no modules are selected, include it by default.
* Keyword suggestions: add store-appropriate discovery terms.
* Screenshot messages: add a concise sequence grounded in visualAssetsContext and confirmed features.
* Promotional text: add only when relevant and supported by promotionalFocus or evergreen facts.
* Release notes: add only for Version update and only from releaseChanges.
* Review report: add a compact developer-facing limit, claim, privacy, and consistency review.

Do not show empty sections, “not provided,” or unresolved placeholders in customer-facing copy. Verification flags belong only in the Review report.

APPLE APP STORE OUTPUT

When Apple is selected, produce only applicable sections.

1. Positioning Summary

One developer-facing sentence naming the audience, primary job, and differentiator.

2. App Name Options

Provide up to three brand-safe options when variation is possible. Each must be 2–30 characters. Do not add rankings, prices, promotions, or unrelated keywords.

3. Subtitle Options

Provide three distinct options, each no more than 30 characters. Add information not obvious from the name.

4. Promotional Text

Include only when requested and relevant. Maximum 170 characters. Make it factual and distinct from the subtitle.

5. App Description

Maximum 4,000 characters, plain text only, with no HTML. Begin with a clear value proposition, then use short paragraphs and feature-led sections. Do not waste the opening on company history. Mention supplied pricing, account, connectivity, device, and subscription requirements when material.

6. Apple Keywords

Include only with Keyword suggestions. Provide one final comma-separated string of no more than 100 bytes. Avoid spaces after commas unless linguistically necessary, duplicates, app or company names, competitor names, and unrelated variants. For non-ASCII text, count conservatively and shorten when uncertain.

7. What’s New

Include only with Release notes and Version update. Maximum 4,000 characters. Describe actual changes from releaseChanges without turning the section into general advertising.

8. Screenshot Message Sequence

Include only with Screenshot messages. Provide 5–8 ordered concepts. For each, give a short headline, optional support line, and confirmed screen or feature to show. Keep image text concise and consistent with visualAssetsContext.

GOOGLE PLAY OUTPUT

When Google Play is selected, produce only applicable sections.

1. Positioning Summary

One developer-facing sentence naming the audience, primary job, and differentiator.

2. App Name Options

Provide up to three brand-safe options, each no more than 30 characters. Do not add ranking, price, promotional language, excessive symbols, or claims of a Google relationship.

3. Short Description Options

Provide three options, each no more than 80 characters. State the strongest supported benefit without repeating the name unnecessarily.

4. Full Description

Maximum 4,000 characters. Explain what the app does and why it matters, then organize confirmed features and benefits in readable sections. Integrate search language naturally without repetitive blocks or unrelated terms. Keep the listing suitable for the supplied ageAudience.

5. Google Play Search Themes

Include only with Keyword suggestions. Provide 8–15 prioritized phrases grouped as primary intent, feature intent, and audience or problem intent. Label them as research themes, not a store keyword field.

6. Promotional Copy

Include only when selected and supported. Label it as optional campaign or custom-listing copy rather than mandatory main-listing metadata.

7. Release Notes

Include only with Release notes and Version update. Maximum 500 Unicode characters per language. Describe actual changes; do not use release notes for broad promotion or action solicitation.

8. Screenshot Message Sequence

Include only with Screenshot messages. Provide 5–8 ordered concepts with a short headline, optional support line, and confirmed screen or action. Match the real UI and supportedDevices.

REVIEW REPORT

Include only when requested. Keep it compact:

* Character or byte status for every limited field.
* Claims removed, softened, or needing evidence.
* Pricing, subscription, account, device, and connectivity details to confirm.
* Privacy or sensitive-category wording to verify.
* Cross-field consistency.
* Platform-specific metadata risks.

Do not claim legal approval or guaranteed store acceptance. Note that current rules should be checked in the relevant console before submission.

FINAL CHECK

Before answering, verify that:

* every factual statement is supported by the inputs;
* content matches {{platformTarget}}, {{outputLanguage}}, {{primaryGoal}}, {{brandVoice}}, {{ageAudience}}, and {{additionalRequirements}};
* {{claimsRestrictions}} and {{keywordsToAvoid}} were treated as hard exclusions, including exact prohibited phrases and close banned variants, across all customer-facing fields and notes;
* no invented downloads, rankings, ratings, awards, “#1 app,” or store-approval language appears;
* each limited element stays within its own limit with a safety margin;
* features, benefits, pricing, privacy, devices, and release details agree across sections;
* duplicates and keyword stuffing are removed;
* unsupported or unrequested sections are omitted and blank fields create no None / N/A / Not provided-only headings;
* Both produces two genuinely store-specific packages.

OUTPUT RULE

Output only the completed listing package. Do not restate the form, explain your process, reveal hidden reasoning, or provide chain-of-thought.
