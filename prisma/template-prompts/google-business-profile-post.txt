GOAL

Write a concise Google Business Profile post that communicates a timely and locally relevant update to customers viewing the business on Google Search or Maps.

The post should quickly explain:

* what is happening;
* who it is relevant to;
* when and where it applies;
* what practical details matter;
* what the customer can do next.

Prioritize clarity, accuracy, and usefulness over promotional language.

POST TYPE

Adapt the output to {{postType}}.

Supported post types:

1. UPDATE

Use for:

* general business news;
* new products or services;
* operational updates;
* seasonal information;
* new availability;
* business announcements;
* useful customer information.

An Update may include:

* concise post text;
* an image or video;
* one available action button.

Do not invent a date range when one is not necessary.

2. OFFER

Use only when a real promotion or deal was supplied.

An Offer should include:

* a specific offer title;
* confirmed start and end dates;
* concise description;
* confirmed price or discount when relevant;
* coupon code only when supplied;
* terms and conditions only when supplied;
* the platform’s offer action.

Do not create an Offer post when no real offer exists.

Do not invent:

* discounts;
* coupon codes;
* sale prices;
* deadlines;
* limited quantities;
* eligibility requirements;
* free items;
* exclusive conditions.

3. EVENT

Use only when a real event was supplied.

An Event should include:

* a descriptive event title;
* confirmed start and end dates;
* confirmed times when available;
* event location when relevant;
* concise explanation of what will happen;
* one appropriate action button.

Do not invent event dates, speakers, activities, ticket prices, capacity, or availability.

If exact times were not supplied, do not create them.

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, dates, hours, prices, discounts, locations, availability, services, and offers supplied in the prompt.
2. Do not invent:

* opening hours;
* temporary closures;
* prices;
* promotions;
* stock availability;
* event dates;
* delivery areas;
* booking availability;
* customer results;
* ratings;
* awards;
* guarantees.
3. Do not imply that a product, service, or appointment is available when availability was not confirmed.
4. Do not add the current year unless it is relevant and confirmed.
5. Do not describe an offer as exclusive, limited, free, or ending soon unless this is supported.
6. Do not create urgency from an ordinary update.
7. Clearly distinguish a planned service or event from something already available.
8. Ensure that all information matches the business’s real profile, website, and destination page.
9. If essential information is missing, omit the claim rather than inventing it.

LOCAL RELEVANCE

Use location details only when they genuinely help the customer understand the post.

Possible locally useful information includes:

* the city or neighborhood served;
* where an event takes place;
* whether the update applies to one specific location;
* delivery or service coverage;
* a seasonal or local circumstance;
* practical arrival or access information.

Do not repeat the full business address when it is already visible in the Business Profile unless the address is necessary for the update.

Do not insert nearby city names merely for local keyword optimization.

Do not create unnatural lists of neighborhoods or service areas.

OPENING

Lead immediately with the update, offer, event, or practical customer benefit.

Good opening approaches include:

* what has changed;
* what is now available;
* the confirmed offer;
* the event and date;
* an important customer notice;
* a seasonal service update.

Avoid generic openings such as:

* “We have exciting news!”
* “We’re thrilled to announce”
* “Attention everyone”
* “Don’t miss out”
* “Big things are happening”
* “Your search is over”
* “Looking for the best business in town?”

BODY

1. Keep the post concise and easy to scan.
2. Prefer approximately 40–120 words.
3. Use fewer words when a simple update can be explained clearly.
4. Use short paragraphs.
5. Focus on one update, offer, or event.
6. Include practical what, when, and where details when they are relevant and confirmed.
7. Mention important conditions or exclusions.
8. Avoid repeating information already displayed in dedicated Offer or Event fields.
9. Avoid long company introductions or general brand history.
10. Do not turn the post into an SEO article.
11. Do not add hashtags unless explicitly requested.
12. Do not include multiple unrelated announcements in one post.

PHONE NUMBERS AND CONTACT DETAILS

Do not include a phone number in the post text.

When the desired action is calling the business, use the available “Call now” button connected to the verified Business Profile phone number.

Do not invent:

* email addresses;
* phone numbers;
* contact people;
* booking links;
* website addresses.

Use a destination link only when it was supplied in {{link}}.

CTA

Use one clear CTA matching {{desiredAction}}.

Select only a CTA supported by {{availableCtaButtons}}.

The CTA should accurately describe the next step.

Possible actions may include:

* Learn more
* Book
* Order online
* Sign up
* Call now
* View offer

Do not write a CTA that implies:

* a free offer that does not exist;
* guaranteed availability;
* immediate confirmation;
* instant approval;
* a discount that was not supplied.

Do not add several competing CTAs.

If no action button is needed, end with a concise practical conclusion.

REGULATED AND RESTRICTED CONTENT

Do not create promotional post content for regulated products or services when Google Business Profile policies prohibit that content.

Treat the following categories carefully:

* alcohol;
* tobacco;
* pharmaceutical products;
* recreational drugs;
* gambling;
* weapons;
* fireworks;
* financial services;
* medical devices;
* adult services.

Do not assume that a regulated business may promote all of its products through posts.

For hotel businesses, do not create Offer posts or posts promoting deals, discounts, or special offers.

When eligibility or policy compliance is uncertain, produce a neutral operational update rather than promotional copy.

STYLE

Write in plain, professional, locally relevant language.

Sound like a real business communicating directly with nearby customers.

Avoid:

* keyword stuffing;
* lists of locations for SEO;
* exaggerated claims;
* excessive adjectives;
* artificial urgency;
* excessive emojis;
* all caps;
* repeated exclamation marks;
* vague promotional slogans;
* corporate jargon.

Avoid phrases such as:

* “In today’s fast-paced world”
* “Unlock”
* “Game-changing”
* “Best in town”
* “Number one”
* “Unbeatable deal”
* “Act now”
* “Once-in-a-lifetime offer”
* “Revolutionary service”
* “Take your experience to the next level”

VISUAL CONCEPT

Suggest one practical image or video concept supporting the post.

The concept should:

* show the real business, location, service, staff, product, or event when possible;
* reinforce the specific update;
* be understandable without excessive text;
* avoid fake reviews, fabricated results, or misleading before-and-after imagery;
* avoid generic stock photography when an authentic business image is available;
* leave clean space for a short overlay only when useful.

Do not invent visual details not supported by {{visualContext}} or {{sourceDetails}}.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the post, silently verify that:

* the post type is appropriate;
* the update appears in the opening;
* all dates, times, prices, locations, and offers were supplied;
* no urgency or scarcity was invented;
* the post contains no phone number;
* the CTA is valid and matches the desired action;
* the content is locally useful without keyword stuffing;
* any important restrictions or terms are included;
* the post is concise and readable;
* the visual concept supports the update;
* the post does not sound like a generic advertisement.
