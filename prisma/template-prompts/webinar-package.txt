GOAL

Create a complete webinar package that attracts the right registrants, sets accurate expectations, delivers meaningful value, and guides attendees toward one appropriate next step.

The package should:

- accurately explain what the webinar covers;
- clearly identify who it is for;
- communicate a specific and realistic attendee benefit;
- provide a logical registration and reminder journey;
- support a well-paced live or recorded presentation;
- keep the webinar useful even when no purchase is made;
- distinguish education from promotion;
- include transparent pricing, limitations, and offer conditions;
- prepare the presenter for questions and technical issues;
- support follow-up after the webinar;
- avoid unsupported claims, fake urgency, and generic event copy.

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, statistics, examples, credentials, features, prices, dates, policies, results, links, and resources supplied in the prompt.

2. Do not invent:

- presenter credentials;
- speaker biographies;
- customer results;
- testimonials;
- case studies;
- attendee counts;
- company partnerships;
- product functionality;
- integrations;
- prices;
- discounts;
- bonuses;
- promo codes;
- deadlines;
- certificates;
- replay availability;
- downloadable resources;
- platform features;
- event capacity;
- guarantees;
- links;
- sources.

3. Do not claim that attendees will achieve a guaranteed result.

4. Do not describe the webinar as:

- exclusive;
- limited;
- sold out;
- highly requested;
- award-winning;
- certified;
- free;
- live;
- interactive;
- hands-on;

unless the supplied information confirms that description.

5. Do not say that thousands of people have registered, attended, benefited, or requested the webinar unless verified.

6. Do not imply that the presenter personally achieved, tested, or experienced something unless it appears in {{presenterExperience}}.

7. Do not invent audience questions, poll results, chat messages, speaker reactions, or live interactions.

8. Clearly distinguish:

- currently available features;
- beta features;
- planned features;
- educational information;
- presenter opinion;
- customer experience;
- hypothetical examples.

9. Do not claim that a webinar recording or replay will be available unless confirmed.

10. When important information is missing, use an explicit placeholder:

[INFORMATION REQUIRED: exact event time]

Do not fill gaps with plausible assumptions.

WEBINAR FORMAT

Adapt the package to {{webinarFormat}}.

LIVE EDUCATIONAL WEBINAR

For a live educational webinar:

- establish the learning outcome;
- provide useful instruction before any commercial offer;
- include realistic interaction points;
- reserve time for questions;
- avoid overloading the session with too many concepts;
- include a concise recap;
- make the CTA optional and relevant.

EVERGREEN WEBINAR

For an evergreen webinar:

- do not pretend that the webinar is live;
- do not include fake chat references;
- do not create false countdowns or artificial seat scarcity;
- clearly explain replay or on-demand access;
- use evergreen timing language;
- avoid phrases such as “today only” unless a real personalized deadline system exists and is disclosed.

LIVE WORKSHOP

For a workshop:

- define the task attendees will complete;
- list prerequisites;
- explain what materials or software are required;
- include work periods and pauses;
- provide checkpoints;
- state what attendees should have by the end;
- avoid promising completion when the task depends on individual circumstances.

PRODUCT DEMONSTRATION

For a demonstration:

- show real available functionality;
- organize the demo around user tasks;
- explain setup requirements;
- identify limitations;
- avoid demonstrating mock features as live functionality;
- include fallback screenshots or a recorded backup when available;
- reserve time for product questions.

PANEL DISCUSSION

For a panel:

- establish one central question;
- introduce each panelist concisely;
- give each participant a distinct role or perspective;
- create balanced moderator questions;
- prevent repetitive answers;
- reserve time for audience questions;
- do not script panelist answers unless approved responses were supplied.

INTERVIEW

For an interview:

- create the host introduction;
- explain why the guest is relevant;
- organize questions into a logical progression;
- include optional follow-ups;
- do not invent guest answers;
- leave realistic time for responses.

When no guest answers were supplied, use:

HOST:
[Question]

FOLLOW-UP OPTIONS:
- [Follow-up]
- [Follow-up]

GUEST:
[Response not scripted]

SALES WEBINAR

For a sales webinar:

- provide genuine educational value;
- establish the problem and decision context;
- explain the method or framework;
- transition clearly into the offer;
- disclose that the webinar includes a commercial offer;
- explain pricing, eligibility, limitations, and terms accurately;
- avoid withholding the entire solution solely to force a purchase;
- avoid exaggerated transformation stories.

CUSTOMER ONBOARDING WEBINAR

For onboarding:

- help users complete the first meaningful task;
- explain account and access requirements;
- demonstrate the core workflow;
- prevent common setup mistakes;
- direct users to approved support resources;
- avoid turning onboarding into an unrelated upsell presentation.

DURATION AND PACING

Create a realistic agenda matching {{duration}} minutes.

Account for:

- opening and housekeeping;
- presenter introduction;
- core teaching;
- examples;
- demonstrations;
- audience interaction;
- pauses;
- transitions;
- offer presentation;
- Q&A;
- closing.

Do not divide all sections evenly.

Prioritize the sections that deliver the main promise.

Suggested pacing guidance:

For a 30-minute webinar:

- 2–4 minutes: opening and expectations;
- 18–22 minutes: core content;
- 3–5 minutes: recap or CTA;
- 3–5 minutes: questions.

For a 45–60-minute webinar:

- 3–5 minutes: opening;
- 30–40 minutes: teaching or demonstration;
- 5–10 minutes: offer or next step;
- 8–15 minutes: Q&A.

Use these only as guidance.

Adjust timing to the actual format.

Do not create a 15-minute sales pitch inside a short educational session unless explicitly requested.

Do not:

- repeat the same point in several sections;
- introduce unrelated background;
- overload slides with paragraphs;
- use several unsupported statistics;
- turn every point into a product pitch;
- create artificial suspense.

- do not claim ownership of a known framework;
- avoid forced acronyms;
- provide a practical example;
- explain where the framework may not apply.

STYLE

Write in clear, natural language appropriate for {{audience}}, {{tone}}, and {{brandVoice}}.

Use:

- concise sentences;
- practical explanations;
- specific outcomes;
- realistic confidence;
- natural spoken transitions;
- mobile-friendly copy;
- clear visual hierarchy.

Avoid:

- corporate event jargon;
- artificial excitement;
- exaggerated promises;
- forced inspiration;
- fake exclusivity;
- manipulative scarcity;
- keyword stuffing;
- excessive exclamation marks;
- generic AI copy;
- repeated rhetorical questions;
- overly polished presenter banter.

Avoid phrases such as:

- “We’re thrilled to announce”
- “This is a game-changer”
- “Unlock your full potential”
- “Don’t miss this incredible opportunity”
- “Seats are filling fast” unless confirmed;
- “Transform your business overnight”
- “The secrets nobody tells you”
- “A once-in-a-lifetime event”
- “You can’t afford to miss this”
- “Stay until the end for a surprise”
- “Packed with value”
- “Actionable insights”
- “Take your results to the next level”

unless the wording is accurate, specific, and consistent with the brand voice.

- the webinar format is correct;

- slides support rather than duplicate the narration;
- interactions are supported by the platform;
- no presenter credentials, facts, statistics, customer results, prices, bonuses, deadlines, links, or features were invented;
- panelist and guest answers were not fabricated;
- educational content provides real value;
- commercial content is disclosed;
- urgency is based on real constraints;
- replay language is accurate;
- accessibility claims are confirmed;
- reminder emails match the event type;
- Q&A answers do not speculate;
- technical and fact-check issues are clearly marked;
- the package does not sound like generic AI webinar copy.
