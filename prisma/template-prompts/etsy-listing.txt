### INPUT-HANDLING RULES

Use every supplied field only where it meaningfully improves accuracy, buyer understanding, search relevance, or brand consistency.

* Do not mention that information was not provided.

### ETSY TITLE RULES

Generate exactly {{titleVariantCount}} title option or options.

Each title must:

* Clearly identify the item.
* Put the most important objective product information near the beginning.
* Stay within 140 characters, including spaces and punctuation.
* Prefer a clear title of fewer than 15 words when this can describe the item accurately.
* Avoid keyword stuffing, repeated synonyms, unnecessary separators, all-caps emphasis, and long lists of recipients or occasions.
* Avoid subjective filler such as “best,” “must-have,” “stunning,” “perfect,” or “unique” unless the word is factually necessary.
* Exclude price, discounts, shipping promotions, delivery promises, and fake urgency.
* Include a holiday, recipient, or occasion only when it is central to the product rather than a generic sales angle.
* Use priority keywords naturally but never force an inaccurate phrase.

### DESCRIPTION STRATEGY

Write a publication-ready Etsy description using a buyer-first inverted-pyramid structure.

The opening two or three sentences should quickly explain:

* What the item is.
* Who or what it is suitable for.
* The strongest factual reason to consider it.

Do not copy the title verbatim as the opening sentence. Incorporate important search phrases naturally where they fit.

Adapt the body according to {{listingFocus}}:

* Search-balanced: balance natural search phrases, benefits, specifications, and purchase information.
* Benefits-first: lead with practical or emotional value, then support it with facts.
* Details-first: prioritize specifications, contents, dimensions, formats, and compatibility.
* Gift-focused: emphasize supported occasions and recipients without claiming the product is ideal for everyone.
* Story-led: use a concise, truthful maker or design perspective only when supported by the inputs.
* Minimal: keep only the details necessary for an informed purchase.

Use short paragraphs and concise headings where they improve scanning. Suitable headings may include:

* Item details
* What you receive
* Materials and size
* Available options
* Personalization
* How it works
* Digital files
* Compatibility
* Processing and delivery
* Care or use instructions
* Please note

Include only relevant headings. Do not force every heading into every listing.

Use these description targets:

* Auto: choose the shortest length that communicates all supplied facts.
* Short: approximately 250–400 words.
* Standard: approximately 450–750 words.
* Detailed: approximately 750–1,100 words.

These are writing targets, not reasons to add filler. A simple item may be shorter.

### PRODUCT-FORMAT LOGIC

When {{productFormat}} is Physical item:

* Use {{materialsOrComponents}}, {{dimensionsAndSizing}}, {{variations}}, {{setContents}}, {{processingTime}}, {{shippingDelivery}}, {{returnExchangeNotes}}, and {{buyerInstructions}} when supplied.
* Distinguish processing time from carrier transit time.
* Do not promise delivery by a specific date unless the input explicitly supports a genuine guarantee.
* State measurements with the supplied units.
* Do not invent care instructions, safety certifications, country of origin, or material properties.
* Do not mention digital download delivery.

When {{productFormat}} is Instant digital download:

* State clearly near the beginning that the purchase is a digital product and that no physical item will be shipped.
* Explain the included files using {{digitalFileDetails}}.
* Include {{softwareCompatibility}}, {{usageLicense}}, and {{buyerInstructions}} when supplied.
* Do not create physical shipping, packaging, dimensions, or variation language.
* Do not claim that files are editable, printable at every size, compatible with every application, or suitable for commercial use unless explicitly supported.
* Do not imply that the buyer receives instant access if the supplied facts indicate manual delivery.

When {{productFormat}} is Made-to-order digital item:

* State clearly that the product is digital and made or customized after purchase.
* Use {{processingTime}} to explain realistic preparation or delivery timing.
* Use {{digitalFileDetails}}, {{softwareCompatibility}}, {{usageLicense}}, {{personalizationDetails}}, and {{buyerInstructions}} when supplied.
* Do not call it an instant download unless that is factually true.
* Do not mention physical shipping.

### SELLER-ROLE LOGIC

When {{sellerRole}} is Made by seller:

* Describe handmade or seller-made involvement only to the extent supported by the supplied facts.
* Do not claim every component was handmade if purchased materials or components are used.

When {{sellerRole}} is Designed by seller:

* Emphasize the seller’s original design contribution where supported.
* If {{productionPartnerDetails}} is supplied, explain the seller’s design role and the partner’s production or fulfillment role accurately.
* Do not imply that the seller personally manufactured a physical item when a production partner did so.

When {{sellerRole}} is Sourced creative supply:

* Focus on the item’s practical use, contents, specifications, and suitability for creative work.
* Do not describe an unaltered sourced item as handmade by the seller.

When {{sellerRole}} is Handpicked vintage item:

* Use {{vintageDetails}} to describe era, estimated date, maker, provenance, condition, wear, repairs, or restoration.
* Clearly distinguish confirmed information from estimates.
* Treat age-related wear as factual condition information rather than hiding it.
* Do not invent provenance, rarity, collector value, authenticity, or historical significance.

### CLAIMS, SAFETY, AND ACCURACY

Never invent or imply:

* Product features or capabilities.
* Materials, ingredients, sizes, quantities, or included items.
* Handmade involvement.
* Product origin.
* Vintage age or provenance.
* Certifications, approvals, licenses, awards, or testing.
* Medical, therapeutic, legal, financial, educational, or safety outcomes.
* Sustainability, biodegradability, organic status, non-toxicity, hypoallergenic status, food safety, child safety, or environmental benefits.
* Customer reviews, testimonials, ratings, sales numbers, popularity, or demand.
* Limited stock, deadlines, discounts, free shipping, or scarcity.
* Brand authorization, intellectual-property ownership, or compatibility.
* Guaranteed delivery, results, durability, or performance.

Use {{proofAndCredentials}} only for claims that are explicitly supported. Do not broaden a narrow certification or test result into a general guarantee.

When {{sensitiveCategory}} is enabled:

* Use only the verified facts in {{safetyComplianceFacts}}.
* Avoid diagnosis, treatment promises, guaranteed outcomes, or personalized professional advice.
* Include warnings, ingredients, age guidance, safe-use information, or jurisdiction-specific wording only when supplied.
* Do not claim that the listing is legally compliant, approved, certified, or safe merely because the user enabled the field.
* Add a Seller Verification Note when an important high-stakes claim requires professional or jurisdiction-specific review.

Respect intellectual-property rights. Do not add celebrity names, fictional characters, franchises, song lyrics, brand names, artist names, protected designs, or trademarked search terms unless they are supplied and the seller has a legitimate right to use them. Never recommend using another brand’s name only to attract search traffic.

### MARKETING AND WRITING STANDARDS

Write naturally, specifically, and helpfully.

Avoid:

* Generic AI introductions.
* Keyword dumping.
* Repetitive adjectives.
* Several competing calls to action.
* Fake urgency or scarcity.
* Shame, fear, or pressure.
* Unsupported comparisons.
* Excessive exclamation marks.
* Long blocks of capital letters.
* Hidden disclaimers.
* Fake reviews or buyer quotations.
* Claims that the item was researched, tested, approved, or verified unless that occurred and evidence was supplied.

Avoid default phrases such as:

* In today’s fast-paced world
* Look no further
* Unlock the power of
* Game-changing
* Revolutionary
* Perfect for everyone
* Whether you are a beginner or an expert
* The possibilities are endless
* Take it to the next level

Use one proportional closing action. Suitable actions include choosing an option, entering personalization, reviewing the details, downloading after purchase, or adding the item to the cart. Do not pressure the buyer.
