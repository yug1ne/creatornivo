GOAL

Create an Etsy listing that helps a buyer quickly understand:

- exactly what is being sold;
- whether it is physical or digital;
- who made, designed, sourced, or selected it;
- what the buyer receives;
- how the product is used;
- which options and personalization choices are available;
- what materials, dimensions, formats, and compatibility conditions apply;
- how processing, delivery, or digital access works;
- which limitations or requirements matter;
- what makes the item meaningfully different.

The listing should support Etsy search while remaining natural, accurate, easy to scan, and useful to a real buyer.

Prioritize clarity, trust, product accuracy, and purchase confidence over keyword density or exaggerated sales language.

ACCURACY AND COMPLIANCE

1. Use only facts, specifications, materials, dimensions, formats, features, policies, prices, timelines, and benefits supplied in the prompt.

2. Do not invent:

- materials;
- dimensions;
- file formats;
- file sizes;
- colors;
- variations;
- compatibility;
- included accessories;
- production methods;
- shipping times;
- processing times;
- certifications;
- sustainability claims;
- warranties;
- refund terms;
- commercial-use rights;
- personalization options;
- customer results;
- awards;
- ratings;
- sales numbers;
- scarcity;
- discounts;
- guarantees.

3. Do not describe the product as handmade, hand-drawn, hand-sewn, hand-painted, vintage, organic, sustainable, recycled, non-toxic, hypoallergenic, licensed, certified, professional-grade, or ethically sourced unless that claim was explicitly confirmed.

4. Do not label a product as vintage unless its eligibility was confirmed.

5. Do not imply that a mass-produced item was personally handmade by the seller.

6. Clearly and accurately represent the seller’s role:

- made by the seller;
- designed by the seller;
- sourced by the seller;
- handpicked by the seller;
- produced by a disclosed production partner.

7. If a production partner is used, do not describe the physical manufacturing as performed entirely by the seller.

8. If AI-assisted creation was involved, do not claim that the work was completely hand-drawn or manually illustrated unless accurate.

9. Do not invent or conceal commercial printing, print-on-demand production, manufacturing, or fulfillment arrangements.

10. Do not use competitor names, celebrity names, fictional franchises, song lyrics, movie titles, sports teams, designer brands, unauthorized characters, or other protected intellectual property unless the rights were explicitly confirmed.

11. Do not imply endorsement by Etsy.

12. Do not claim guaranteed medical, financial, emotional, educational, safety, or performance outcomes.

13. If necessary information is missing, use an explicit placeholder such as:

[Information required: exact material]

Do not fill missing information with assumptions.

TITLE

Write one recommended Etsy listing title.

Title requirements:

- maximum 140 characters, including spaces;
- preferably fewer than 15 words when the product can be described clearly within that length;
- state what the item actually is;
- place the most important identifying information early;
- include only the strongest objective product traits;
- remain easy to understand on mobile;
- use the primary search phrase naturally;
- avoid unnecessary repetition;
- avoid turning the title into a list of keyword fragments.

A useful title structure may be:

[Item type] + [most important objective trait] + [material, size, style, or use case]

Include recipient, occasion, or holiday terminology only when it is essential to the nature of the item.

Do not automatically add phrases such as:

- gift for her;
- gift for him;
- birthday gift;
- Christmas gift;
- perfect gift;
- unique gift;

unless they genuinely help identify the product and are supported by the item.

Do not include:

- price;
- discount;
- free shipping;
- sale language;
- shop name unless necessary;
- subjective praise;
- excessive punctuation;
- ALL CAPS;
- keyword repetition;
- competitor brands;
- unsupported claims.

Avoid title language such as:

- beautiful;
- amazing;
- perfect;
- best;
- must-have;
- premium;
- luxury;
- unique;

unless the term is objectively meaningful and supported.

Provide:

- recommended title;
- character count;
- word count;
- primary phrase used.

DESCRIPTION RULES

- Use short paragraphs and meaningful section headings.
- Use bullets where they improve readability.
- Explain concrete product details before emotional or decorative language.
- Connect confirmed features to practical benefits.
- Keep the buyer’s likely questions in mind.
- Explain what is not included when a photo or mockup could create confusion.
- State whether props, frames, furniture, devices, or accessories shown in images are excluded.
- Mention natural variation when handmade materials may differ, but only when relevant.
- Explain color-display or printing variation only when relevant.
- Keep policy language concise and consistent with actual shop policies.
- Do not duplicate the entire listing title.
- Do not repeat the same keyword in every paragraph.
- Do not make the description a keyword list.
- Do not include irrelevant shop history.
- Do not invent testimonials.
- Do not use fake urgency.
- Do not pressure the buyer.

DIGITAL PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

For digital products, clearly state near the beginning:

- this is a digital product;
- no physical item will be shipped;
- whether it is an instant download or made-to-order file;
- which files the buyer receives;
- file formats;
- dimensions or resolution;
- software or account requirements;
- editing limitations;
- license terms;
- printing or use instructions;
- how the buyer accesses the files.

For instant digital downloads:

- do not promise email delivery unless that is the actual process;
- explain that access follows Etsy’s digital-download process;
- ensure file count and file size are based on supplied information;
- do not generate variation options when the digital listing does not support them.

For made-to-order digital products:

- explain what the buyer must submit;
- state the confirmed production timeline;
- explain proof and revision handling;
- distinguish the preview from the final file.

Do not imply that:

- Canva Pro is included;
- paid fonts are included;
- a commercial license is included;
- files can be edited in every program;
- all printers will produce identical colors;

unless confirmed.

PHOTO RULES

- The first image should clearly show the item being sold.
- Do not use a collage as the primary image when it makes the product unclear.
- Do not visually imply that excluded props are included.

- Do not show product variations that are unavailable.
- Do not use copyrighted logos or protected characters without approval.
- Use mockups carefully and ensure they accurately represent scale, color, and format.
- Do not obscure the product with excessive text.
- Avoid misleading watermarks or overlays.
