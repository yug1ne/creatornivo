GOAL

Create a complete Kickstarter campaign package that helps potential backers understand:

- what the project will create;
- why the project matters;
- who is creating it;
- what work has already been completed;
- what remains to be done;
- why crowdfunding is needed;
- how the funding goal was calculated;
- what rewards are available;
- what delivery and shipping conditions apply;
- what risks exist;
- what backers should realistically expect;
- how the project differs from buying a finished product from a normal store.

The campaign should inspire support through:

- a meaningful creative goal;
- credible preparation;
- clear evidence;
- transparent budgeting;
- realistic rewards;
- honest risks;
- achievable production planning;
- direct communication from the creator.

Do not rely on:

- exaggerated claims;
- fake scarcity;
- fabricated social proof;
- guaranteed delivery;
- misleading product readiness;
- unsupported environmental claims;
- speculative investment language;
- emotional pressure;
- generic crowdfunding clichés.

PLATFORM-FIT GATE

Before writing the finished campaign, silently evaluate whether the project appears suitable for Kickstarter based on the supplied information.

The project should:

- create something new;
- have a finite and understandable goal;
- result in something that can be shared with backers;
- have a clear path toward completion;
- fall within an eligible creative category;
- comply with current Kickstarter rules;
- avoid prohibited or heavily restricted items;
- avoid offering equity, financial return, profit sharing, interest, or monetary rewards.

Do not generate a ready-to-launch campaign when:

- the project only resells an existing product;
- the project’s end goal is ordinary retail inventory with no new creative work;
- the product or reward appears prohibited;
- the creator’s role is unclear;
- the project offers investment returns;
- the project makes unsupported medical or financial claims;
- no credible completion plan exists;
- essential production or reward information is missing;
- the campaign relies on deceptive representations.

Instead output:

PLATFORM-FIT REVIEW REQUIRED

Reason:
[Specific concern]

Information or changes required:
- [Required clarification]
- [Required production evidence]
- [Required rule review]
- [Required reward revision]

Do not claim that Kickstarter has approved the project.

ALL-OR-NOTHING FUNDING

Treat the campaign as all-or-nothing.

Explain accurately that:

- pledges are collected only if the project reaches its funding goal by the deadline;
- the funding goal should represent the amount needed to complete the project and fulfill the promised rewards;
- the creator should not set an artificially low goal merely to show early success;
- the goal should account for production, fulfillment, fees, taxes, and reasonable contingency;
- the funding goal and deadline cannot be casually adjusted after launch.

Do not describe a pledge as an immediate completed purchase.

Do not claim that the project will proceed exactly as planned regardless of the funding result unless confirmed external funding makes that statement accurate.

CAMPAIGN DURATION

Use the confirmed duration.

Do not create a campaign longer than the current platform maximum.

Do not automatically recommend the maximum duration.

Evaluate whether the proposed duration fits:

- audience size;
- launch preparation;
- campaign complexity;
- promotional capacity;
- production timing;
- real deadline.

Do not manufacture urgency merely because the campaign has a deadline.

FUNDING-GOAL REVIEW

Review the supplied funding goal against the confirmed budget.

The budget should consider:

- development or creative work;
- materials;
- manufacturing;
- tooling;
- labor;
- packaging;
- freight;
- fulfillment;
- replacement units;
- platform fees;
- payment-processing fees;
- taxes;
- currency fluctuations;
- contingency;
- stretch-goal impact.

Do not invent costs.

When costs are missing, insert:

[BUDGET INFORMATION REQUIRED: describe the missing cost]

Do not calculate a funding goal from assumptions that were not supplied.

If the confirmed funding goal is lower than the supplied minimum viable cost, flag:

FUNDING-GOAL REVIEW REQUIRED

Do not hide concerns merely to produce persuasive copy.

CREATOR ACCOUNTABILITY

Represent the creator’s identity, qualifications, role, and experience accurately.

Do not invent:

- credentials;
- employers;
- awards;
- years of experience;
- previous projects;
- manufacturing expertise;
- technical expertise;
- fulfillment history;
- partnerships;
- advisers;
- press coverage.

When the creator has limited experience, build trust through:

- transparent preparation;
- documented progress;
- realistic scope;
- identified partners;
- testing;
- clear risk mitigation;
- honest communication.

Do not compensate for limited experience by exaggerating authority.

PROJECT READINESS

Clearly distinguish:

- concept;
- rendering;
- design mockup;
- non-functional prototype;
- functional prototype;
- engineering validation unit;
- production sample;
- final production unit.

Do not describe:

- a rendering as a prototype;
- a prototype as a production-ready item;
- a sample as mass production;
- a planned feature as complete;
- a supplier conversation as a manufacturing agreement;
- an estimate as a confirmed quote;
- a patent application as a granted patent;
- internal testing as independent certification.

When visual assets show a prototype, label it accurately.

For product-based projects, explain what has been tested and what remains untested.

CAMPAIGN TITLE

Write one recommended Kickstarter campaign title and four alternate options.

The title should:

- accurately identify the project;
- communicate what is being created;
- use concrete language;
- be understandable outside Kickstarter;
- remain concise;
- avoid unsupported claims;
- avoid keyword stuffing;
- avoid excessive punctuation.

Use meaningfully different approaches:

1. Direct project description.
2. Benefit- or use-case-led.
3. Creative vision-led.
4. Differentiator-led.
5. Audience-led.

Avoid title patterns such as:

- “The Future of…”
- “The World’s Best…”
- “Revolutionary…”
- “The Ultimate…”
- “Game-Changing…”
- “The First Ever…” unless verified;
- “The Only…” unless verified;
- “Join the Revolution”
- “Change the World With Us”

Do not imply that Kickstarter endorses the project.

It should:

- explain what is being created;
- identify the primary use or audience;
- add information not already clear from the title;
- avoid empty slogans;
- avoid unsupported superlatives;
- avoid several clauses packed together.

OPENING

Begin with a clear explanation of the project.

The first section should answer:

- What is being created?
- Who is creating it?
- Why does it need crowdfunding?
- Why might a backer care?

Do not begin with several paragraphs of vague inspiration.

Avoid openings such as:

- “We have always had a dream.”
- “Imagine a world where…”
- “In today’s fast-paced world…”
- “We are thrilled to announce…”
- “This changes everything.”
- “After years of hard work…”

unless supported and relevant.

Do not withhold the project identity to create artificial curiosity.

PROJECT PURPOSE

Explain why the project exists.

Use only supplied information.

Possible foundations include:

- an unmet creative need;
- a practical limitation;
- a personal experience;
- a new artistic work;
- a technical opportunity;
- preservation of a craft;
- community demand;
- a new edition or expansion;
- an original game, book, film, album, product, or performance.

Do not invent an emotional founder story.

Do not imply that the project will solve a major social, medical, environmental, or economic problem without evidence.

FEATURES AND BENEFITS

Clearly distinguish:

- feature: what the project includes or does;
- benefit: how that feature helps the backer;
- outcome: a possible result that requires evidence.

Every benefit must be connected to a confirmed feature, design decision, material, process, or creative element.

Do not claim:

- guaranteed comfort;
- guaranteed performance;
- guaranteed learning;
- guaranteed health improvement;
- guaranteed income;
- universal compatibility;
- permanent durability;
- professional results;
- complete safety.

- use only approved screenshots or footage;
- identify prototype limitations;
- show actual interactions where possible;
- avoid simulations presented as working functionality;
- distinguish final intended design from current prototype;
- explain compatibility and requirements;
- state whether an account, app, subscription, or third-party service is needed.

Do not invent:

- screens;
- controls;
- animations;
- benchmark results;
- battery life;
- compatibility;
- app functionality;
- multiplayer features;
- integrations;
- manufacturing finishes.

Do not invent food-safety certifications, nutritional claims, organic status, or international shipping eligibility.

BUDGET SECTION

- creative production;
- manufacturing;
- materials;
- tooling;

- taxes;

Do not imply that every pledged unit goes directly into manufacturing when fees, taxes, and fulfillment also apply.

Do not present marketing or creator compensation as zero unless confirmed.

TIMELINE SECTION

Create a realistic post-campaign timeline.

Include confirmed stages such as:

- campaign completion;
- surveys;
- final design;
- testing;
- ordering materials;

- quality control;
- freight;
- fulfillment;
- digital delivery.

For each stage provide:

- planned period;
- main work;
- dependency;
- buffer or risk;
- backer update milestone.

Use estimated language.

Do not present estimated delivery as guaranteed.

Do not compress the schedule to make the campaign more attractive.

Do not omit known dependencies.

REWARDS

Create a reward strategy using only confirmed reward items and costs.

When enough information exists, organize approximately 3–10 clear reward tiers.

The exact number should depend on:

- project complexity;
- audience;
- production capacity;
- reward differences;
- fulfillment burden.

Do not create extra tiers merely to fill a quota.

For every reward tier provide:

- tier title;
- pledge amount;
- intended backer;
- exact included items;
- digital or physical status;
- quantity limit;
- early-bird or standard status;
- estimated delivery;
- shipping treatment;
- tax or duty note;
- variation options;
- production cost;
- fulfillment concern;
- concise buyer-facing description.

Do not invent amounts, contents, quantities, or delivery dates.

When information is missing, write:

[REWARD INFORMATION REQUIRED]

REWARD-TIER PRINCIPLES

Reward tiers should:

- be easy to compare;
- clearly list included items;
- avoid confusing bundle names;
- avoid hidden dependencies;
- avoid too many minor variations;
- reflect real production and fulfillment costs;
- leave room for fees, replacements, and uncertainty;
- avoid promising delivery before production can realistically finish.

Do not offer:

- equity;
- profit sharing;
- interest;
- financial return;
- raffle entries;
- lottery chances;
- monetary prizes;
- prohibited products;
- rewards the creator cannot lawfully provide.

Do not describe rewards as investments.

EARLY-BIRD REWARDS

Use early-bird tiers only when confirmed.

Early-bird rewards should:

- have a real quantity or time limit;
- remain financially sustainable;
- not create an impossible first production batch;
- clearly state how they differ from standard tiers.

Do not invent an early-bird discount.

Do not label an unlimited tier as limited.

ADD-ONS

Create add-ons only from confirmed items.

For every add-on provide:

- title;
- amount;
- included item;
- compatibility with reward tiers;
- estimated delivery;
- shipping impact;
- production impact;
- quantity limit.

Do not use add-ons to hide the real cost of receiving the main project output.

Do not offer an add-on that cannot be fulfilled independently or bundled reliably.

PLEDGING WITHOUT A REWARD

Acknowledge that a backer may support the project without selecting a reward.

Do not pressure backers to choose a reward.

Do not imply that higher pledges give ownership, investment rights, or control over the project unless a specific non-financial participatory reward was confirmed.

SHIPPING

Create a clear shipping section.

State:

- shipping origin;
- regions served;
- excluded regions;
- whether shipping is included;
- whether shipping is charged during or after the campaign;
- whether estimates may change;
- how address collection works;
- whether tracking is expected;
- how taxes and duties are handled;
- what backers should expect for international delivery.

Do not invent shipping rates.

Do not claim worldwide shipping when some locations are excluded or untested.

Do not use “free shipping” when shipping cost is embedded in the pledge amount unless that representation is accurate and appropriate.

When shipping is collected after the campaign, clearly explain:

- the planned collection method;
- why the amount may vary;
- what estimate is currently available;
- which backers may face higher costs.

TAXES, VAT, DUTIES, AND TARIFFS

Create a separate section when physical rewards cross regions.

Explain only confirmed information about:

- sales tax;
- VAT;
- customs duties;
- import fees;
- tariffs;
- handling fees;
- when these amounts are collected;
- who is responsible.

Do not promise duty-free or tax-free delivery unless confirmed.

Do not assume all backers pay the same tax.

Do not hide potential import costs.

Use careful wording because tax treatment can depend on:

- origin;
- destination;
- reward type;
- declared value;
- local law;
- pledge-manager setup.

Include:

Tax and customs treatment should be verified before launch.

RISKS AND CHALLENGES

Write an honest Risks and Challenges section.

Include the material risks supplied in the prompt.

For each risk explain:
