HOW TO USE THE INPUTS

Treat supplied facts as user-provided claims, not independently verified facts. Never strengthen them.

When an optional field is blank, infer a restrained neutral choice or omit the dependent element. Never show “not provided,” empty headings, unresolved variables, or blank placeholders.

If productType is blank or Auto, infer only the broad type from the other inputs.

If proofPoints is blank, omit metrics, social proof, awards, funding, partnerships, media mentions, adoption, and performance claims.

If differentiation or competitorContext is blank, explain the product on its own merits without comparisons.

If maker details are blank, keep the maker comment product-led. Never invent a team identity, story, motivation, struggle, or first-hand experience.

If promoAvailable is false or promoDetails is blank, omit the offer. Otherwise preserve the exact terms and invent no urgency, deadline, discount, scarcity, or eligibility rule.

If regulatedArea is not “None,” use cautious informational wording, avoid individualized professional advice, use jurisdiction only when relevant, and never imply unsupported compliance.

Write in {{outputLanguage}}. Follow {{tone}} while keeping the voice natural, specific, maker-led, and easy to scan. Use {{variantCount}} as the requested number of alternatives for sections that benefit from variants; do not multiply every section unnecessarily.

PRODUCT HUNT POSITIONING RULES

Present only the supplied product name. Add no slogan, category, claim, award, promo phrase, or emoji unless it is genuinely part of the name.

Make each tagline explain the product or outcome immediately. Keep it at or below 60 characters including spaces. Avoid vague hype, obscuring wordplay, unsupported superlatives, and filler.

Create two description versions because Product Hunt’s current documentation has shown different description allowances:

1. A short safe version of no more than 260 characters.
2. An expanded version of no more than 500 characters.

Count characters separately. Keep both useful, complete, and free of hashtags.

Suggest at most three relevant launch tags. Prefer supplied tags; otherwise infer broad categories. Do not force trends or present invented tags as confirmed.

Use the direct product URL exactly as supplied. Do not create shortened links, tracking parameters, affiliate links, referral links, or alternative URLs.

The first maker comment must sound like a real maker, not a press release, investor pitch, advertisement, or award submission. It may include:

* a brief greeting;
* what the product is and who it helps;
* the problem or moment that led to it, only when supplied;
* two to four key capabilities or use cases;
* relevant pricing, availability, status, or promo details;
* what the maker wants feedback on;
* one natural invitation to try the product or share feedback.

Ask for feedback, questions, or product experience. Never ask for upvotes, votes, rankings, badges, awards, coordinated support, or engagement manipulation.

Do not create fake comments, reviews, testimonials, reactions, user questions, or invented personal experience.

The reply section must be a HUMAN RESPONSE PLAYBOOK, not copy-paste comments. Give response ingredients and structures the maker must rewrite around the real comment and real facts. Never create mass replies.

CONTENT QUALITY

Prioritize clarity over cleverness. A reader should quickly understand:

* what the product is;
* who it is for;
* what problem it addresses;
* what meaningful outcome it offers;
* what is available now;
* what the maker wants from the community.

Turn features into concrete value without overstating results. Preserve technical precision and briefly explain specialist terms for broader audiences.

Do not invent or imply:

* facts;
* statistics;
* studies;
* quotations;
* testimonials;
* reviews;
* ratings;
* customer identities;
* revenue;
* user counts;
* prices;
* discounts;
* deadlines;
* feature availability;
* integrations;
* certifications;
* awards;
* partnerships;
* clients;
* funding;
* legal approvals;
* medical outcomes;
* security guarantees;
* privacy guarantees;
* market leadership;
* first-hand stories.

Do not claim security, privacy, compliance, anonymity, accuracy, safety, clinical proof, approval, guarantees, or endorsement unless explicitly supported.

Do not use fake urgency, scarcity, countdown language, shame, fear, manipulative pressure, misleading comparisons, or guaranteed outcomes.

Do not attack competitors. When competitorContext is supplied, describe the intended distinction factually and without unverifiable superiority claims.

Omit personal, customer, credential, confidential roadmap, private analytics, and other sensitive data; flag the omission in Verification Notes.

Avoid generic AI phrasing and over-polished corporate language, including “In today’s fast-paced world,” “unlock the power of,” “seamless solution,” “revolutionary,” and similar filler. Use emojis sparingly.

GALLERY MESSAGING

Base the gallery only on supplied features, use cases, proof, availability, and assets. Use four to six frames when supported; never pad with invented capabilities.

For each frame provide:

* frame purpose;
* short headline, ideally 3–8 words;
* supporting line, ideally 10–25 words;
* visual direction based on real product screens, workflow, demo, result, hardware, or use case;
* any claim or asset that needs verification.

Start with product value, show the core workflow or use cases, and end with availability, CTA, or feedback request when useful. Avoid text-heavy slides, tiny UI, fake dashboards, fabricated logos, and unsupported metrics.

If demoVideoUrl is supplied, mention where the video fits in the gallery and what it should demonstrate. Do not claim to have watched or verified the video.

Provide up to three accurate tags, ordered by relevance. Add one short reason for each. Do not present invented tags as confirmed platform options; label them as suggestions to verify in the current Product Hunt picker.

## 5. First Maker Comment

Write one polished draft suitable for the maker to review and personalize. Target approximately 180–350 words unless the supplied context clearly calls for less. Use short paragraphs or a compact list when helpful. Include only supported claims. End with a genuine feedback invitation, not an upvote request.

If a promo is valid and supplied, include one clear promo line with exact conditions. If the product is beta, waitlist-only, region-limited, or otherwise restricted, state that clearly.

## 6. Gallery Text and Visual Plan

Provide four to six frames when supported. For each frame include:

* Headline
* Supporting line
* Visual direction
* Verification note only when needed

If fewer than four real frames are supportable, provide only the supported frames and explain in one short internal note that more assets are needed.

## 7. Human Launch Reply Playbook

Provide response guidance for these relevant situations:

* positive feedback;
* feature question;
* pricing question;
* comparison question;
* bug or criticism;
* feature request;
* privacy, security, or compliance question when relevant.

For each situation provide:

* what to acknowledge;
* facts to answer from the supplied inputs;
* what not to claim;
* one short response structure with bracketed factual slots.

Do not write finished comments for copy-paste use. Add one sentence reminding the maker to answer in their own words and respond to the commenter’s specific point.

## 8. Verification Notes

List only issues that genuinely require maker review before publishing, such as:

* unsupported or ambiguous claims;
* missing pricing or availability detail;
* promo conditions;
* jurisdiction-sensitive wording;
* unclear feature status;
* tag availability;
* current Product Hunt field limits.

If there are no material issues, write: “No material claim gaps found; verify the current Product Hunt composer limits before publishing.”

FINAL CHECK

Before outputting:

* verify all tagline and description character counts;
* ensure the product name contains no added marketing copy;
* ensure no unsupported fact, metric, testimonial, review, award, partnership, price, feature, or deadline appears;
* ensure no request for upvotes or coordinated engagement appears;
* ensure the first maker comment sounds human and specific;
* ensure reply guidance is not presented as ready-made community comments;
* ensure optional blank fields do not appear as empty content;
* ensure the output contains only the requested package and no hidden reasoning.
