GOAL

Create a Product Hunt launch package that clearly explains:

* what the product is;
* who it is for;
* what problem it addresses;
* how it is different;
* what is currently available;
* what limitations or Early Access conditions apply;
* what kind of feedback the makers want.

The package should sound like it comes from a real maker speaking directly to the Product Hunt community.

It should not sound like a press release, corporate announcement, or exaggerated sales campaign.

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, features, prices, offers, dates, metrics, testimonials, and roadmap details supplied in the prompt.
2. Do not invent:

* user counts;
* download counts;
* revenue;
* customer results;
* ratings;
* reviews;
* rankings;
* awards;
* product features;
* integrations;
* roadmap commitments;
* launch offers;
* promo codes;
* expiration dates;
* free plans;
* trial periods.
3. Do not claim that the product is Product of the Day, trending, featured, or highly ranked unless that happened and was confirmed after launch.
4. Do not describe Early Access, beta, or waitlist access as a complete public release.
5. Do not imply that planned roadmap items are already available.
6. Clearly distinguish:

* currently available features;
* beta features;
* planned features;
* ideas being considered.
7. Do not guarantee future delivery dates unless approved.
8. Do not invent maker identities, roles, credentials, or personal stories.
9. Do not create fictional social proof.
10. Do not claim that the product is the first, only, best, leading, or revolutionary option without reliable evidence.

ACCOUNT AND MAKER VOICE

Write the package in the voice of an identifiable maker or team member.

Do not write as an anonymous company or logo.

The maker introduction should clearly state the author’s relationship to the product.

Use direct language such as:

* “I built…”
* “We created…”
* “I’m the founder of…”
* “I worked on…”

only when supported by {{makerRelationship}}.

Do not impersonate a customer, neutral reviewer, hunter, or unrelated community member.

TAGLINES

Write five tagline options.

Each tagline should:

* explain what the product does;
* identify the primary user or use case when useful;
* highlight the differentiator;
* remain short and easy to understand;
* avoid vague slogans;
* avoid excessive adjectives;
* avoid unnecessary brand repetition.

Use five meaningfully different approaches:

1. Direct category and function.
2. Audience-led.
3. Problem-led.
4. Differentiator-led.
5. Outcome-led.

Do not create five versions that merely replace one verb.

Avoid phrases such as:

* “The future of…”
* “Revolutionizing…”
* “Your all-in-one solution”
* “The ultimate platform”
* “Everything you need”
* “The world’s first” unless verified;
* “Powered by AI” as the entire value proposition.

SHORT DESCRIPTION

Write one Product Hunt description of no more than 260 characters, including spaces.

The description should:

* explain what the product is;
* identify who it helps;
* state the primary use case or value;
* include the differentiator when space allows;
* remain understandable without marketing jargon.

Do not:

* repeat the tagline word for word;
* add a long feature list;
* include unsupported claims;
* add several CTAs;
* include a promo code;
* use excessive emojis.

Provide the character count.

PRICING AND STATUS

Return the pricing status exactly as supplied:

* Free
* Paid
* Paid with a free trial or free plan

Do not infer the status from incomplete pricing information.

Clearly state the launch status:

* Available now
* Beta
* Early Access
* Waitlist
* Not yet available

Use only the supplied status.

PROMO

Include a Product Hunt promo only when all required details were supplied:

* offer;
* promo code;
* expiration date.

Do not create a promo section when one of these elements is missing.

Do not invent urgency, scarcity, exclusivity, or eligibility.

FIRST COMMENT / MAKER INTRO

Write one complete first comment from the maker.

Use a natural structure:

1. Brief greeting and maker identification.
2. Why the product was created.
3. The problem or situation behind it.
4. What the product currently does.
5. Who it is for.
6. A short list of the most relevant confirmed features or use cases.
7. Transparent pricing, launch status, limitations, or launch offer.
8. The specific feedback being requested.
9. A brief thank-you.

The first comment should:

* sound personal but not overdramatic;
* use simple language;
* stay reasonably concise;
* be easy to scan;
* disclose beta, Early Access, waitlist, or other limitations;
* ask for feedback, opinions, or questions.

Do not:

* ask for upvotes;
* mention rankings;
* pressure readers;
* claim that the product is perfect;
* describe every feature;
* write a long founder mythology;
* use fake vulnerability;
* imply that Product Hunt endorsed the product.

Use emojis sparingly or omit them.

TOPICS

Suggest up to five relevant Product Hunt topics.

Choose only categories strongly connected to the product.

Do not add broad or fashionable topics simply for visibility.

Do not invent a topic when uncertain that it exists.

When exact Product Hunt topic availability has not been confirmed, label them:

Potential topics to verify before submission

GALLERY PLAN

Create a gallery plan based on the supplied assets.

Provide 3–5 gallery items when enough material is available.

For each item include:

* purpose;
* main visual;
* short gallery caption;
* optional text overlay;
* what product value it demonstrates.

Recommended narrative:

1. What the product helps users accomplish.
2. The main interface or workflow.
3. A core use case.
4. The primary differentiator.
5. Pricing, availability, or CTA only when useful.

Do not invent screenshots, dashboards, integrations, customers, metrics, or results.

Do not put large paragraphs of text inside gallery images.

If there are fewer than two usable gallery images, clearly state:

At least two gallery images should be prepared before launch.

COMMUNITY REPLY TEMPLATES

Write reply templates for common Product Hunt community questions.

Create templates for:

1. Pricing
2. Free plan or trial
3. Current availability or Early Access
4. Roadmap
5. Difference from alternatives
6. Data privacy or security, when relevant
7. Feedback or feature requests

For every reply:

* use only supplied facts;
* answer directly;
* be transparent about unknowns;
* avoid defensive language;
* invite useful follow-up when appropriate;
* do not promise a roadmap item or delivery date that was not approved.

When information is missing, use an honest placeholder such as:

“We haven’t confirmed that yet. We’re currently evaluating [area], and we’ll update the product page when the details are final.”

Do not make the answer sound finalized when it is a template requiring maker review.

Do not invent pricing, a free tier, a roadmap, security certifications, or support guarantees.

LAUNCH-DAY SOCIAL POST

Write one social post announcing the Product Hunt launch.

The post should:

* state that the product is launching on Product Hunt;
* explain the product in one concise sentence;
* state who it is for;
* mention a confirmed offer only when supplied;
* invite feedback, questions, or help spreading the word;
* include the approved launch link when supplied.

Do not:

* ask for upvotes;
* offer rewards for votes;
* imply that voting is required;
* claim a ranking;
* use fake urgency;
* ask people to create Product Hunt accounts solely to vote;
* spam several CTAs.

Use a natural maker voice appropriate for the selected social platform.

LAUNCH-OFFER TRANSPARENCY

When a launch offer exists, clearly state:

* what the user receives;
* who is eligible;
* whether a code is required;
* when it expires;
* any relevant limits.

Do not hide recurring charges, plan restrictions, or eligibility requirements.

Do not describe the standard product price as a special Product Hunt offer.

STYLE

Write in direct, clear, maker-friendly language.

Sound helpful, open to feedback, and realistic.

Avoid:

* press-release language;
* corporate jargon;
* exaggerated founder storytelling;
* fake humility;
* excessive emojis;
* repeated exclamation marks;
* vague AI claims;
* overuse of “we’re excited”;
* manipulative urgency;
* generic startup slogans.

Avoid phrases such as:

* “We’re thrilled to announce”
* “Revolutionary”
* “Game-changing”
* “Disrupting the industry”
* “Unlock your potential”
* “Changing the world”
* “Built for everyone”
* “Join thousands” unless verified;
* “Don’t miss out”
* “Vote for us”
* “Support us with an upvote”

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the package, silently verify that:

* all five taglines are genuinely different;
* the description is no more than 260 characters;
* the pricing and launch status are accurate;
* no result, ranking, review, customer, feature, or metric was invented;
* the first comment clearly identifies the maker;
* Early Access and product limitations are disclosed;
* the maker asks for feedback rather than upvotes;
* promo details are included only when complete;
* gallery captions match real available assets;
* reply templates do not invent pricing, roadmap, or policies;
* the social post does not request votes;
* the package sounds like a maker, not a corporate press release.
