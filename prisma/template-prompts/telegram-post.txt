GOAL

Write a Telegram post that communicates one clear idea, provides useful context, and fits naturally into the selected channel or community.

The post should feel written specifically for Telegram rather than copied from a website, press release, newsletter, or another social platform.

Prioritize:

- clarity;
- useful information;
- natural language;
- mobile readability;
- factual accuracy;
- transparent commercial intent;
- one coherent message;
- an appropriate next step.

POST TYPE

Adapt the structure to {{postType}}.

Possible post types include:

- news update;
- channel announcement;
- educational post;
- practical guide;
- author opinion;
- personal observation;
- product update;
- product launch;
- promotional post;
- event announcement;
- service notice;
- community question;
- discussion post;
- poll;
- digest;
- checklist;
- case study;
- story;
- behind-the-scenes update;
- urgent operational notice;
- sponsored integration.

Do not use the same structure for every post type.

NEWS UPDATE

For a news or current-events post:

- state the confirmed development early;
- include the relevant date or period;
- separate facts from interpretation;
- explain why the development matters to the audience;
- use only supplied sources;
- avoid sensational language;
- include a source line when appropriate.

Do not invent breaking-news status, quotations, casualties, statistics, official statements, or causes.

Do not present an unverified report as confirmed fact.

Use careful wording such as:

- “According to the supplied source…”
- “The available information indicates…”
- “This has not yet been independently confirmed.”

Use these only when the supplied information supports them.

EDUCATIONAL POST

For an educational or practical post:

- explain one clear concept or process;
- provide useful context;
- use steps or bullets only when they improve understanding;
- include examples only when supplied;
- explain limitations or exceptions;
- avoid turning the post into a superficial list of generic tips.

AUTHOR OPINION

For an opinion or commentary post:

- make the author’s position clear;
- distinguish opinion from confirmed fact;
- support the argument with approved details;
- avoid presenting personal interpretation as objective truth;
- avoid insulting people who disagree;
- end with a specific discussion question only when it adds value.

PRODUCT OR PROJECT UPDATE

For a product, service, or project update:

- explain what changed;
- clarify who the update is relevant to;
- state what is currently available;
- mention limitations, beta status, eligibility, or rollout conditions;
- connect the update to practical user value;
- include one relevant CTA.

Do not invent features, release dates, pricing, availability, integrations, or customer results.

PROMOTIONAL POST

For a commercial or promotional post:

- make the commercial purpose clear;
- explain the offer in plain language;
- identify who it is relevant to;
- state confirmed price, deadline, eligibility, or conditions;
- include one clear CTA;
- disclose sponsorship, affiliation, or ownership when relevant.

Do not disguise an advertisement as an independent recommendation.

Do not invent scarcity, discounts, deadlines, bonuses, customer demand, or limited availability.

EVENT ANNOUNCEMENT

For an event post:

- state the event name;
- explain what will happen;
- include confirmed date, time, location, format, and eligibility;
- explain who the event is for;
- state whether registration is required;
- include one registration or information CTA.

Do not invent speakers, activities, ticket prices, participant limits, or registration deadlines.

DIGEST

For a digest:

- include only the supplied items;
- organize them by importance or topic;
- give every item a concise explanation;
- avoid copying full headlines without context;
- do not overwhelm the reader with too many links;
- end with one short takeaway or discussion prompt.

POLL

When {{pollRequired}} is confirmed:

- write one clear poll question;
- provide only the approved answer options;
- make the options mutually understandable;
- avoid leading or manipulative phrasing;
- avoid combining several questions into one poll;
- add a brief introduction explaining why the poll matters.

Do not invent poll options or claim that the result is representative of a broader population.

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, dates, examples, prices, offers, quotations, results, and experiences supplied in the prompt.

2. Do not invent:

- personal stories;
- statistics;
- studies;
- customer results;
- quotations;
- sources;
- links;
- product features;
- event details;
- prices;
- discounts;
- deadlines;
- user counts;
- revenue;
- reviews;
- awards;
- partnerships;
- official statements;
- future plans.

3. Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless it appears in {{personalExperience}}.

4. Do not write phrases such as:

- “I recently discovered…”
- “I tested this…”
- “People keep asking me…”
- “Many subscribers requested…”
- “Everyone is talking about…”

unless the supplied information supports them.

5. Clearly distinguish:

- confirmed fact;
- personal opinion;
- interpretation;
- estimate;
- hypothesis;
- planned feature;
- unverified report.

6. Do not describe planned or beta functionality as fully available.

7. Do not make guaranteed medical, financial, legal, career, business, safety, or performance claims.

8. Do not add a link unless it was supplied.

9. Do not invent the existence of a Telegram bot, private group, website, downloadable file, promo code, or registration page.

10. When important information is missing, omit the claim or use an explicit placeholder:

[Information required: exact date]

Do not fill missing information with assumptions.

OPENING

Begin with a first line that immediately establishes the subject or relevance of the post.

The opening may use:

- a confirmed update;
- a useful observation;
- a practical problem;
- a clear conclusion;
- a relevant contrast;
- a direct question;
- an important date;
- a concise announcement.

The first line should remain understandable when the post is forwarded outside the original channel.

Use a line break after the opening when it improves readability.

Do not force a dramatic hook for:

- formal announcements;
- sensitive news;
- emergency information;
- service notices;
- condolences;
- corrections;
- security warnings.

Avoid empty or manipulative hooks such as:

- “Срочно!”
- “Шок!”
- “Вы не поверите”
- “Никто об этом не говорит”
- “Это изменит всё”
- “Успейте, пока не удалили”
- “Сохраните, пока не поздно”
- “Главный секрет”
- “Вся правда”
- “Дочитайте до конца”

Use urgency only when the information is genuinely urgent and confirmed.

BODY STRUCTURE

Develop one central message.

Use the number of paragraphs the topic genuinely requires.

Prefer:

- short paragraphs;
- clear spacing;
- concise headings;
- short lists;
- meaningful emphasis;
- practical details.

Do not:

- split every sentence into a separate paragraph;
- write one enormous block of text;
- repeat the opening in different words;
- include several unrelated announcements;
- overload the post with several products or links;
- add a long introduction before reaching the point;
- restate the entire post in the conclusion.

For a short post, use approximately:

- one opening;
- one or two explanatory paragraphs;
- one conclusion or CTA.

For a longer educational post, use:

- a short opening;
- context;
- several clearly separated points;
- practical takeaway;
- one relevant CTA or conclusion.

FORMATTING

Use only formatting supported by {{formattingMode}}.

Possible formatting modes:

- plain text;
- Telegram Markdown;
- Telegram MarkdownV2;
- Telegram HTML;
- formatting-neutral output.

When the exact publishing method is unknown, use formatting-neutral output.

Formatting rules:

- use bold emphasis sparingly;
- use italics only for meaningful emphasis;
- use headings only when the post is long enough to need them;
- use bullets for lists, not for every sentence;
- keep links readable;
- do not use decorative symbols excessively;
- do not create complex nested lists;
- do not output unsupported HTML or broken Markdown;
- do not place the entire post in bold;
- do not use code blocks unless the post contains actual code.

Do not use formatting merely to make an ordinary sentence appear more important.

MOBILE READABILITY

Make the post easy to read on a phone.

Use:

- short paragraphs;
- visible spacing;
- concise lines;
- limited emphasis;
- clear hierarchy.

Avoid:

- dense walls of text;
- excessive indentation;
- too many emojis;
- long unbroken lists;
- several large headings;
- excessive punctuation;
- tiny details hidden inside long sentences.

EMOJIS

Follow {{emojiPreference}}.

When no preference is supplied:

- use 0–3 emojis in most posts;
- use them only when they support meaning or structure;
- omit them for serious, formal, sensitive, or emergency posts.

Do not:

- place an emoji after every sentence;
- use several decorative emojis in the opening;
- use warning emojis when there is no real warning;
- use money, fire, alarm, or explosion emojis to manufacture urgency.

HASHTAGS

Follow {{hashtagPreference}}.

When no preference is supplied:

- use no hashtags by default;
- use 1–3 only when they help organize a recurring series, event, topic, or branded category;
- place them on a separate final line.

Do not:

- create a hashtag wall;
- add unrelated trending hashtags;
- repeat several variations of the same word;
- invent branded hashtags;
- use hashtags as a replacement for normal context.

LINKS

Use only supplied and approved links.

When a link is included:

- explain what the reader will find after opening it;
- use one primary destination;
- avoid several competing links;
- place the link near the relevant CTA;
- do not conceal a commercial or affiliate destination.

Do not write:

- “ссылка в био”;
- “ссылка в описании”;
- “переходите по ссылке ниже”;

unless the actual publishing setup supports that instruction.

If an affiliate link is used, include the supplied disclosure.

CTA

Use one CTA only when it naturally supports {{goal}} and {{desiredAction}}.

Possible actions include:

- read a supplied article;
- register for an event;
- open a product page;
- download a confirmed resource;
- answer a specific question;
- participate in a poll;
- share a relevant experience;
- contact support;
- join a confirmed group;
- use a confirmed bot;
- place an order;
- try one practical step.

CTA rules:

1. Use one main action.
2. Explain what happens next.
3. Keep the CTA concise.
4. Do not ask readers to react, comment, forward, subscribe, click, and purchase all at once.
5. Do not force a CTA in personal reflections, sensitive posts, corrections, or informational notices.
6. Do not use generic engagement bait.

Avoid endings such as:

- “А что думаете вы?”
- “Согласны?”
- “Кто тоже?”
- “Ставьте плюс”
- “Поставьте огонёк”
- “Отправьте десяти друзьям”
- “Подпишитесь, чтобы не потерять”
- “Срочно переходите”

unless the action is genuinely relevant and not manipulative.

When a CTA would feel forced, end with a concise takeaway instead.

BUTTON

Create a Telegram button only when:

- a real approved destination exists;
- the publishing system supports buttons;
- {{buttonLabel}} or {{desiredAction}} confirms the intended action.

The button label should:

- contain approximately 2–5 words;
- clearly describe the destination;
- use an action verb;
- match the actual page.

Examples:

- Читать статью
- Посмотреть обновление
- Зарегистрироваться
- Открыть каталог
- Скачать инструкцию
- Связаться с поддержкой
- Посмотреть тарифы

Do not write:

- Нажми сюда
- Подробнее

when a more specific label is possible.

Do not imply that something is free unless confirmed.

If no button is required, omit it.

COMMENTS AND DISCUSSION

When comments are enabled and discussion supports the post goal:

- suggest one specific discussion question;
- make it answerable from experience or informed opinion;
- connect it directly to the post;
- avoid generic engagement bait.

Do not ask a question solely to increase comments.

When comments are disabled, do not tell readers to comment.

SOURCES

For factual, historical, political, medical, financial, legal, scientific, or news-related posts, include a source block when approved sources were supplied.

Use this format when relevant:

Источники:
— [Source name]
— [Source name]

Do not invent source names or links.

Do not list sources that do not support the post.

Do not use the source block for ordinary personal, promotional, or community posts unless it adds value.

COMMERCIAL TRANSPARENCY

When the post promotes something owned, created, sponsored, or commercially connected to the author:

- disclose the relationship clearly;
- do not pretend to be an independent customer;
- do not disguise advertising as neutral advice;
- do not hide affiliate status;
- do not create fake testimonials or user comments.

Possible disclosures include:

- Реклама
- Партнёрский материал
- Я работаю над этим продуктом
- Это мой проект
- Материал подготовлен при поддержке [name]

Use only the disclosure appropriate to {{commercialRelationship}} and {{disclosure}}.

Do not add a commercial disclosure when no commercial relationship exists.

SPONSORED POSTS

For sponsored integrations:

- explain the product or service clearly;
- make the sponsorship visible;
- connect the product naturally to the channel topic;
- use only approved claims;
- mention important limitations;
- avoid exaggerated personal endorsement;
- do not claim that the author uses the product unless confirmed;
- use one CTA;
- avoid hiding the advertisement until the end.

VISUAL DIRECTION

Suggest one practical visual concept only when a visual is relevant.

The visual concept should specify:

- the main subject;
- the setting or background;
- composition;
- what product, document, interface, person, or object appears;
- whether short overlay text is useful;
- how the visual supports the post.

Do not suggest:

- fake screenshots;
- fabricated reviews;
- invented interfaces;
- misleading before-and-after images;
- unsupported graphs;
- fake news headlines;
- customer logos without approval;
- excessive text on the image.

When {{visualContext}} describes an existing image or video, make the post complement it instead of repeating exactly what is visible.

ALT OR MEDIA DESCRIPTION

When accessibility text is requested, write a concise media description.

The description should:

- state what is visibly shown;
- include meaningful actions and relationships;
- avoid promotional claims;
- avoid keyword stuffing;
- avoid describing details that are not visible.

If the final media is unknown, write:

[Add media description after the final image or video is selected]

PINNED COMMENT OR FOLLOW-UP

Create a pinned comment or follow-up message only when it adds information that should not overload the main post.

It may contain:

- an additional source;
- a clarification;
- updated information;
- a corrected date;
- a secondary link;
- answers to common questions.

Do not move essential offer conditions or safety information into a comment where readers may miss it.

CORRECTIONS AND UPDATES

For a correction:

- state clearly what was incorrect;
- provide the corrected information;
- avoid hiding the original mistake;
- include the relevant source when available;
- do not use defensive language.

For an updated post:

- distinguish new information from the original content;
- include the update date when relevant;
- do not silently change a time-sensitive claim without explanation.

STYLE

Write in clear, natural language appropriate for {{audience}}, {{tone}}, and {{brandVoice}}.

The post should sound like a real Telegram author or editor.

Avoid:

- press-release language;
- corporate jargon;
- fake familiarity;
- artificial enthusiasm;
- forced slang;
- intentional spelling mistakes;
- keyword stuffing;
- generic motivation;
- excessive rhetorical questions;
- manipulative urgency;
- fake vulnerability;
- repeated exclamation marks;
- excessive parenthetical comments;
- long introductions.

Avoid common AI and marketing phrases such as:

- “В современном быстро меняющемся мире”
- “Вот в чём дело”
- “Позвольте этому осознанию проникнуть”
- “Это настоящий game-changer”
- “Раскройте свой потенциал”
- “Выведите это на новый уровень”
- “Мы рады сообщить”
- “Уникальная возможность”
- “Не упустите шанс”
- “Ваше будущее «я» скажет спасибо”
- “Это не просто X — это Y”
- “Давайте разберёмся”

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the post, silently verify that:

- the post type matches the requested goal;
- the first line clearly establishes the topic;
- the post communicates one central message;
- all facts, dates, offers, prices, results, and links were supplied;
- no personal experience or quotation was invented;
- fact and opinion are clearly distinguished;
- the formatting is compatible with the selected mode;
- paragraphs are readable on mobile;
- emojis and hashtags are limited;
- the CTA is relevant or omitted;
- the button points to a real approved destination;
- comments are requested only when enabled;
- commercial relationships are disclosed;
- the visual concept does not invent product details;
- the post does not sound like generic AI copy;
- the post remains understandable when forwarded outside the original channel.
