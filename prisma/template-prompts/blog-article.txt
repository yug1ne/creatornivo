CONTENT STRATEGY

Identify the reader’s main question or problem and answer it clearly near the beginning.
Match the article format and structure to the search intent. Do not automatically use the same structure for every topic.
Cover the subject comprehensively without adding filler to reach the suggested word count.
Include useful details that go beyond generic information, such as concrete examples, realistic scenarios, limitations, comparisons, common mistakes, decision criteria, or actionable steps.
Use the supplied original experience, data, examples, and brand insights wherever relevant.
Do not copy or closely imitate the structure or wording of existing articles.
Do not force every keyword into the article. Use the primary keyword and related terms naturally where they improve clarity.
Keep each section focused on a distinct reader need.
FACTUAL RELIABILITY
Do not invent facts, statistics, studies, quotations, dates, laws, prices, product specifications, survey results, or expert opinions.
Use only information supported by the supplied sources or reliable, verifiable knowledge.
Clearly distinguish confirmed facts from estimates, opinions, and general recommendations.
For medical, financial, legal, safety, or other high-stakes topics, use careful language and include an appropriate disclaimer when necessary.
Do not claim that a product, treatment, strategy, or service guarantees a result.
Mention dates when information may change over time.
HUMAN WRITING STYLE
Write naturally, as an experienced human writer explaining the subject to a real reader.
Use varied sentence and paragraph lengths. Combine concise statements with more developed explanations. Use contractions when they sound natural in the selected language and brand voice.
Avoid repetitive sentence patterns, unnecessary summaries, exaggerated claims, keyword stuffing, corporate jargon, and robotic transitions.
Do not use generic AI-style phrases such as:
“In today’s fast-paced world”
“In the ever-evolving landscape”
“Whether you’re a beginner or an expert”
“It’s important to note”
“This comprehensive guide”
“Let’s dive in”
“When it comes to”
“By following these tips”
“Unlock the power of”
“Take your journey to the next level”
Do not begin every section with a broad definition. Do not end every section with a summary. Do not repeatedly address the reader by saying “you” in every paragraph.
STRUCTURE
Create:
SEO title:
   * Clear and specific.
   * Designed to attract the intended reader without clickbait.
   * Include the primary keyword naturally when appropriate.
H1:
   * May differ slightly from the SEO title.
   * Must accurately describe the article.
URL slug:
   * Short, descriptive, lowercase, and hyphenated.
Meta description:
   * Write a concise, persuasive description reflecting the page content.
   * Include the primary topic naturally.
   * Do not make unsupported promises.
Introduction:
   * Address the reader’s need immediately.
   * Explain what the article will help them understand or accomplish.
   * Avoid generic background information and long scene-setting.
Main article:
   * Use a logical hierarchy of H2 and H3 headings.
   * Let the subject determine the number of sections.
   * Use numbered steps only for genuinely sequential processes.
   * Use bullet points only when they improve readability.
   * Include examples, tables, checklists, or comparisons only when useful.
   * Keep paragraphs readable without making every paragraph artificially short.
FAQ:
   * Include 2–5 questions only if they address meaningful related questions not already answered clearly in the article.
   * Omit the FAQ when it would merely repeat the main content.
Conclusion:
   * Summarize the most important decision or next step without repeating the entire article.
   * End with a natural, relevant CTA connected to {{desired_action}}.
   * Avoid generic motivational language.
SEO AND LINKING
Place the primary keyword naturally in the SEO title, H1, introduction, and at least one relevant heading when appropriate.
Use secondary keywords only where they fit the meaning and context.
Cover relevant concepts, entities, synonyms, and subtopics without creating unnatural keyword density.
Suggest 2–4 contextual internal-link placements using the supplied internal pages.
Use descriptive anchor text rather than phrases such as “click here” or “read more.”
Suggest authoritative external sources only when they support factual claims.
Provide 2–3 relevant image ideas with descriptive alt-text suggestions.
Do not add links, sources, authors, quotations, or data that do not exist.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the article, silently verify that:


It satisfies the likely search intent.
It provides a complete answer.
It contains no obvious filler or repeated ideas.
It does not sound like a generic AI template.
It does not contain invented facts.
The headings accurately describe their sections.
Keywords are used naturally.
The CTA matches the topic and reader journey.
The article offers at least one useful detail beyond basic information.
Output the finished article and SEO elements only. Do not describe your writing process.
