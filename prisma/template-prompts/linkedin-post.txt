GOAL
Write a LinkedIn post that communicates one clear professional idea, gives the reader a useful insight, and supports {{goal}}.
The post should sound like a real professional sharing a considered observation, not like a motivational template, sales pitch, or generic AI-generated thought-leadership post.
ACCURACY


Use only the experiences, results, examples, facts, opinions, and observations supplied in {{sourceDetails}}.
Do not invent personal stories, client conversations, revenue figures, growth results, failures, promotions, lessons, statistics, quotes, or events.
Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless that information was supplied.
Do not invent authority, credentials, customer outcomes, or insider knowledge.
Clearly distinguish personal opinion from confirmed fact.
Avoid guaranteed business, financial, career, or performance claims.
If there is not enough evidence for a strong claim, use measured language rather than exaggerating.
OPENING
Start with a strong opening that works within the first two visible lines.
The opening should:


introduce tension, contrast, a specific observation, a useful conclusion, or a relevant problem;
connect directly to the main point;
create interest without withholding essential context;
sound professional and natural;
be followed by a line break.
Do not use misleading or empty hooks such as:
“I have a confession”
“Nobody wants to hear this”
“This may be controversial”
“I almost didn’t post this”
“Stop doing this immediately”
“Here’s the harsh truth”
“I made one decision that changed everything”
“You won’t believe what happened next”
“Agree?”
“Read that again”
BODY


Develop one central argument or insight.
Use 3–5 short paragraphs only when the topic supports that structure.
Give each paragraph a distinct purpose, such as:
   * context;
   * problem;
   * observation;
   * example;
   * implication;
   * practical takeaway.
Do not split every sentence into a separate paragraph.
Use short paragraphs for readability, but allow 2–4 sentences when the thought requires development.
Include concrete details or examples when they were supplied.
Explain why the insight matters to {{audience}}.
Avoid repeating the hook in different words.
Do not add generic career advice or motivation unless it directly supports the topic.
Do not turn the post into a list unless a list is the clearest format.
Do not overload the post with multiple lessons, announcements, or CTAs.
STYLE
Write in a clear, credible, conversational professional voice.
Use varied sentence lengths. Prefer plain language over corporate terminology. Sound confident without sounding absolute, self-important, preachy, or performative.
Avoid:


excessive one-line paragraphs;
fake vulnerability;
forced controversy;
exaggerated storytelling;
self-congratulation;
empty inspiration;
vague business jargon;
excessive rhetorical questions;
unnecessary capitalization;
multiple exclamation marks;
emoji decoration;
overuse of dashes;
artificial pauses such as “And then?”
engagement bait.
Avoid common LinkedIn and AI clichés such as:
“I’m humbled to announce”
“Thrilled to share”
“In today’s fast-paced world”
“Here’s the thing”
“Let that sink in”
“It’s not about X. It’s about Y.”
“This changed everything”
“The biggest lesson?”
“Leadership isn’t a title”
“Your network is your net worth”
“Success leaves clues”
“Game-changer”
“Unlock your potential”
“I learned more from failure than success”
“People don’t leave companies, they leave managers”
“Consistency is key”
“Do the work”
“Thoughts?”
ENDING AND CTA
End in a way that naturally supports {{goal}} and {{desiredAction}}.
Possible endings include:
one specific open question;
a concise takeaway;
an invitation to compare experiences;
a request for a concrete example;
an invitation to read, apply, test, contact, or discuss something;
no question, when a question would feel forced.
If using an open question:


Ask one specific question connected to the post.
Make it possible to answer from real experience.
Avoid broad prompts such as “What do you think?” or “Do you agree?”
Do not ask a question solely to increase comments.
Do not repeat a question already answered in the post.
HASHTAGS
Use 0–3 hashtags only when they improve categorization or discovery.
Hashtag rules:


place them on one separate final line;
choose specific professional or industry-relevant hashtags;
do not use broad spam-like tags;
do not create several variations of the same phrase;
omit hashtags entirely when they do not add value;
do not place hashtags inside sentences.
LENGTH
Keep the complete post under 1,300 characters, including spaces and hashtags.
Do not remove clarity or necessary context simply to maximize brevity. Remove repetition, filler, and generic language first.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the post, silently verify that:
the first two lines create interest without clickbait;
the post communicates one main idea;

the post gives the audience a useful insight;
the tone matches the author’s role and goal;
paragraphs are readable but not artificially fragmented;
the ending feels natural;
any question is specific and relevant;
hashtags are limited and useful;
the post is under 1,300 characters;
the post does not sound like a generic LinkedIn template.
