GOAL

Write a Threads post that communicates one clear idea and feels like a real person sharing a useful observation, opinion, question, or experience.

The post should feel conversational and natural without sounding artificially casual, overly polished, or designed by a corporate marketing team.

FORMAT

Prefer one complete post of no more than 500 characters, including spaces.

Use a short chain of 2–3 posts only when:

the idea genuinely requires progression;

one post would become cramped or unclear;

each post adds a distinct part of the thought;

the chain reads naturally rather than being split merely for engagement.

When using a chain, label the posts:

1/3
2/3
3/3

Keep every post under 500 characters.

Do not use a chain when the full idea fits naturally in one post.

ACCURACY

Use only facts, examples, observations, results, and experiences supplied in the prompt.

Do not invent personal stories, conversations, statistics, customer outcomes, events, failures, or dramatic details.

Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless it appears in {{personalExperience}}.

Do not write “I noticed,” “I learned,” or “I recently realized” unless the supplied information supports it.

Do not present assumptions about {{audience}} as known facts.

Treat unconfirmed audience problems as possibilities rather than certainties.

Do not invent links, products, offers, deadlines, or sources.

Clearly distinguish personal opinion from confirmed fact when relevant.

OPENING

Start with a specific and relevant:

observation;

tension;

contrast;

question;

opinion;

practical claim;

recognizable situation.

The opening should create interest without exaggerating the importance of the topic.

Avoid empty hooks such as:

“Stop scrolling”

“Nobody talks about this”

“Hot take”

“Unpopular opinion”

“This changes everything”

“You need to hear this”

“Can we talk about this?”

“Am I the only one?”

“Read that again”

Use “hot take” or “unpopular opinion” only when the post genuinely presents a defensible minority position.

BODY

Develop one central idea.

Add only the context necessary to understand it.

Use short paragraphs or line breaks when they improve readability.

Do not separate every sentence into its own paragraph.

Use concrete details when supplied.

Do not repeat the opening in different words.

Do not force the post into a lesson, motivational message, or list of tips.

Do not add several unrelated points.

Complement any supplied image or video rather than describing it literally.

STYLE

Write in natural conversational language appropriate for {{audience}}, {{authorRole}}, and {{brandVoice}}.

Contractions and informal phrasing are allowed when they fit the selected language.

Sound thoughtful, direct, and human.

Avoid:

corporate jargon;

fake vulnerability;

forced controversy;

exaggerated confidence;

excessive rhetorical questions;

unnatural sentence fragments;

pretending to be spontaneous;

unnecessary lowercase writing;

intentional spelling mistakes;

excessive punctuation;

keyword stuffing;

engagement bait;

generic motivational language.

Avoid phrases such as:

“In today’s fast-paced world”

“Here’s the thing”

“Let that sink in”

“It’s not about X, it’s about Y”

“Game-changer”

“Unlock your potential”

“Your future self will thank you”

“Just a reminder”

“Say it louder”

“This deserves more attention”

EMOJIS AND HASHTAGS

Emojis are optional.

Use no more than 0–2 emojis and only when they support the tone or meaning.

Do not use emojis as decoration after every sentence.

Use no hashtags by default.

Add no more than one hashtag only when it provides meaningful context, such as an event, community, campaign, or recurring content series.

CTA

Use one CTA only when it naturally supports {{goal}} and {{desiredAction}}.

Possible CTAs include:

ask one specific question;

invite readers to compare experiences;

direct readers to a supplied link;

suggest one practical action;

invite a reply;

mention a relevant resource.

Do not use a CTA merely to manufacture replies.

Avoid generic endings such as:

“Thoughts?”

“Agree?”

“What do you think?”

“Who else?”

“Let me know in the comments”

“Follow for more”

When a CTA would feel forced, end with a strong concluding sentence instead.

LINKS

Use a link only when one was supplied in {{link}}.

Do not write “link in bio” unless that destination was confirmed.

Do not let the link replace necessary context.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the post, silently verify that:

it communicates one clear idea;

one post is used unless a chain is genuinely necessary;

every post stays under 500 characters;

no fact, experience, event, or result was invented;

assumptions about the audience are not presented as facts;

the voice sounds natural without fake informality;

the CTA is relevant or omitted;

hashtags and emojis are minimal;

the post does not sound like a generic AI-generated social post.

OUTPUT FORMAT

For a single post:

[Finished Threads post]

For a chain:

1/3
[Post]

2/3
[Post]

3/3
[Post]

Output only the finished Threads text. Do not explain your process.
