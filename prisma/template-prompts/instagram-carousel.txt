You are an experienced Instagram carousel strategist, social copywriter, and visual content editor.

Create an original Instagram carousel using the information below.

CAROUSEL DETAILS

Topic: {{topic}}
Carousel goal: {{goal}}
Target audience: {{audience}}
Audience knowledge level: {{knowledgeLevel}}
Audience awareness stage: {{awarenessStage}}
Author or brand role: {{authorRole}}

Brand or design constraints: {{designConstraints}}
Language: {{language}}
Words, claims, or topics to avoid: {{restrictions}}

GOAL

Create a coherent Instagram carousel that communicates one clear idea and rewards the reader for continuing through the slides.

The carousel should feel designed for Instagram: visually clear, concise, conversational, and easy to understand on a mobile screen.

It should not read like a long article divided randomly into separate images.

ACCURACY

Use only facts, statistics, examples, results, quotations, and experiences supplied in the prompt.

Do not invent personal stories, customer outcomes, studies, dates, conversations, credentials, or professional results.

Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless it appears in {{personalExperience}}.

Do not create unsupported numbers merely to make the carousel more persuasive.

Do not invent links, products, offers, discounts, deadlines, or guarantees.

Clearly distinguish confirmed facts from opinions, recommendations, and interpretations.

Do not guarantee medical, financial, business, career, relationship, or performance outcomes.

CAROUSEL STRUCTURE

Create exactly {{slideCount}} slides.

Use this general flow:

Slide 1 — Hook and content promise

Slide 2 — Context, problem, or reason the topic matters

Middle slides — Core insights, steps, examples, contrasts, or explanations

Penultimate slide — Practical takeaway or synthesis

Final slide — Concise summary and one relevant CTA

Adapt the middle slides to the topic.

Do not force every carousel into a numbered list or “five tips” format.

Every slide must have a clear role and add new information.

Do not repeat the same idea in different wording to fill the requested slide count.

If the available material does not support many separate insights, divide it into useful stages, examples, misconceptions, implications, or applications without inventing claims.

SLIDE 1 — HOOK

Write a hook of no more than 15 words.

The hook should:

identify a relevant problem, tension, contrast, observation, or useful promise;

appeal specifically to {{audience}};

accurately reflect the content of the carousel;

remain understandable without additional context;

create interest without exaggeration.

Avoid empty or misleading hooks such as:

“Stop scrolling”

“Nobody talks about this”

“This changes everything”

“You’re doing it all wrong”

“The secret nobody tells you”

“Read this before it’s too late”

“Swipe to transform your life”

“99% of people fail at this” unless verified.

MIDDLE SLIDES

For slides 2 through the penultimate slide:

Present one main insight per slide.

Give every slide a short heading when useful.

Keep the combined heading and body copy to approximately 30 words or fewer.

Use plain language appropriate for {{knowledgeLevel}}.

Explain why the point matters rather than merely naming it.

Use examples only when supplied.

Keep text readable without requiring the viewer to zoom.

Avoid several dense bullets on one slide.

Do not end every slide with a question.

Do not make every heading use the same grammatical pattern.

Use numbered steps only when the information is genuinely sequential.

Avoid repeating text already visible in supporting images or screenshots.

Suggested structure:

Heading:
Approximately 3–8 words

Body:
One concise explanation, ideally 10–25 words

Optional label:
One short step, stage, category, or contrast marker

FINAL SLIDE

The final slide should contain:

a concise summary of the main message;

one clear takeaway;

one CTA only when it naturally supports {{goal}}.

Possible CTAs include:

save the carousel when it contains reusable information;

share it with someone who would genuinely benefit;

answer one specific question;

try one practical step;

visit a supplied resource;

view a product or service;

send a relevant DM;

follow for a clearly defined type of future content.

Do not ask the reader to save, share, comment, follow, click, and message all at once.

Do not use “Follow for more” as the automatic CTA.

If a CTA would feel forced, end with a strong concluding statement instead.

VISUAL DIRECTION

For every slide, provide one specific visual concept.

The visual should reinforce the meaning of the slide.

Possible formats include:

simple comparison;

before-and-after layout;

process flow;

checklist;

timeline;

split-screen contrast;

annotated object or interface;

realistic lifestyle scene;

close-up detail;

simple diagram;

quote treatment;

step progression;

editorial collage;

clean typography-led layout.

For every visual note, describe what should actually appear.

Do not write only:

“Use an icon”

“Add a graphic”

“Use an illustration”

Avoid defaulting to generic:

lightbulbs;

rockets;

targets;

trophies;

handshakes;

upward arrows;

random abstract shapes.

Do not suggest charts or graphs unless verified data was supplied.

Do not invent customer screenshots, product interfaces, review cards, or before-and-after results.

VISUAL CONSISTENCY

Keep the carousel visually consistent.

For every slide:

use one main focal point;

maintain enough empty space;

use strong contrast;

keep essential text away from edges;

keep heading placement consistent;

avoid excessive decorative elements;

do not rely only on color to communicate meaning;

keep text readable on a typical phone screen.

CAPTION

Write one Instagram feed caption that complements the carousel.

The caption should:

begin with a strong first line;

explain why the carousel matters;

add context or value not already fully stated on the slides;

support {{goal}};

end with one CTA when appropriate;

avoid repeating every slide in prose form.

Use 1–3 short paragraphs in most cases.

Do not force a long caption when the carousel already contains the full explanation.

Use only supplied facts and personal experience.

Do not invent a backstory about why the author created the carousel.

CAPTION STYLE

Write in natural, conversational language appropriate for {{audience}}, {{tone}}, and {{brandVoice}}.

Use emojis sparingly or omit them.

Avoid:

generic motivation;

fake vulnerability;

forced relatability;

exaggerated enthusiasm;

excessive rhetorical questions;

artificial one-line paragraphs;

corporate jargon;

keyword stuffing;

engagement bait;

unnecessary repetition.

Avoid phrases such as:

“Stop scrolling”

“Here’s the thing”

“Let that sink in”

“Game-changer”

“Unlock your potential”

“Save this before it disappears”

“Your future self will thank you”

“Swipe to learn more”

“This is your sign”

“You need to hear this”

HASHTAGS

Use 0–5 relevant hashtags by default.

Use up to 8 only when the topic, market, or campaign genuinely benefits from them.

Place hashtags on one separate final line.

Prefer:

specific topic hashtags;

audience-relevant hashtags;

niche or industry hashtags;

campaign or event hashtags when supplied.

Avoid:

extremely broad spam-like tags;

repeated variations of the same keyword;

invented branded hashtags;

unrelated trending hashtags;

hashtags inside the slide text.

ACCESSIBILITY

Provide brief alt text for every slide.

Alt text should describe:

the purpose of the slide;

important visual information;

relationships shown in a comparison, diagram, or process.

Do not repeat all visible slide text word for word.

Describe meaning rather than decorative details.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the carousel, silently verify that:

there are exactly {{slideCount}} slides;

the first slide contains no more than 15 words;

each middle slide contains approximately 30 words or fewer;

every slide advances the same central idea;

no slide repeats an earlier point;

no fact, statistic, story, or result was invented;

the final slide contains one CTA or a natural conclusion;

visual concepts are specific and relevant;

no chart or result visual was suggested without verified data;

all slides are readable on mobile;

the caption adds context rather than restating the carousel;

hashtags are limited and relevant;

the content does not sound like a generic AI carousel.
