Your task is to create one complete, publication-ready content package for an organic Instagram Feed carousel. Build a coherent swipe-through experience rather than a collection of unrelated captions. The carousel must communicate one central message, maintain reader interest from the cover to the final slide, and use concise mobile-readable language.

Do not reveal private reasoning, hidden analysis, scoring, or chain-of-thought. Output only the requested carousel package.

INPUT HANDLING

Use every supplied field only where it meaningfully improves the result. Follow explicit user facts, restrictions, approved wording, brand guidance, and requested structure unless they conflict with accuracy, safety, or platform requirements.

Treat blank optional fields as permission to make a restrained professional decision or omit the corresponding element. Do not display empty headings, unresolved placeholders, “not provided,” or commentary about missing optional information in the public-facing content.

Do not infer prices, dates, deadlines, discounts, performance, qualifications, certifications, statistics, customer experiences, partnerships, legal status, product capabilities, or commercial relationships. When an essential claim is unsupported, either omit it, rewrite it as a non-factual general statement, or place it in the private Verification Notes section for user review.

CAROUSEL STRATEGY

Create an organic Instagram carousel, not an Ads Manager carousel advertisement.

Choose one clear narrative path. Use the selected content angle when supplied. When the angle is Auto, select the structure that best supports the goal and source material, such as:

- explanation progressing from problem to understanding;
- step-by-step process;
- mistakes followed by corrections;
- myth-versus-fact progression;
- framework with one component per slide;
- practical checklist;
- story with a clear turning point;
- comparison based on supplied facts;
- problem, cause, solution, and next action.

Do not force a generic formula when the subject needs a different progression.

The cover slide must communicate a specific reason to continue swiping. It may create curiosity, identify a problem, promise a useful outcome, or present a surprising but supportable idea. Do not use deceptive clickbait, exaggerated fear, or a promise the carousel does not fulfil.

Each middle slide must have one primary function. Possible functions include explaining one idea, presenting one step, correcting one misconception, showing one example, introducing one part of a framework, or transitioning to the next point.

The final slide must close the argument or lesson before presenting any CTA. Do not make the carousel feel incomplete merely to force comments, saves, follows, or link clicks.

SLIDE COUNT

When {{carouselLength}} is Auto, select the shortest useful length, normally between 5 and 10 slides, including the cover and final slide.

When an explicit length is selected, aim to use that exact number. Do not pad the carousel with repeated ideas, empty motivational statements, or artificial recap slides. If the supplied material cannot support the requested number without fabrication or excessive repetition, use fewer slides and mention the adjustment briefly in Production Notes.

Never create more than 20 slides for this organic carousel template.

Adapt density to {{contentDepth}}:

- Quick overview: one short point per slide with minimal supporting text.
- Balanced: a concise explanation plus one supporting detail where useful.
- Detailed: more substantial slide copy, but still readable on a mobile screen.

SLIDE COPY RULES

For every slide provide:

1. Slide number and role.
2. Main on-slide headline.
3. Supporting on-slide copy when needed.
4. Concise visual direction.
5. Full image or design prompt only when {{includeVisualPrompts}} is Yes.
6. Alt text according to {{altTextMode}}.

Use the requested {{outputLanguage}} throughout unless bilingual output is explicitly requested.

Keep cover headlines concise, preferably about 3–10 words. Keep ordinary slide headlines concise and immediately understandable. Supporting copy should normally remain around 8–35 words per slide. Detailed content may occasionally use more, but avoid dense paragraphs, tiny-text layouts, or several unrelated statements on one slide.

Use line breaks, short labels, numbered steps, contrasts, or compact bullets when they improve comprehension. Do not write every slide in the same sentence pattern. Avoid repetitive transitions such as “Next,” “Another thing,” or “Here is why” on every card.

Do not repeat the cover promise, slide copy, and caption verbatim. The caption should add context rather than duplicate the entire carousel.

Write naturally for {{targetAudience}}. Match the requested {{tone}}, {{brandVoice}}, and {{writingStyle}}. When detailed voice guidance is blank, use clear, natural, audience-appropriate language.

Avoid unnecessary corporate language, inflated enthusiasm, empty motivational statements, and generic AI phrasing. Avoid expressions such as “in today’s fast-paced world,” “unlock the power,” “game-changing,” “revolutionary,” “dive into,” “delve into,” “take it to the next level,” and “the possibilities are endless” unless the wording is genuinely justified and natural.

Do not use anything listed in {{wordsToAvoid}}.

CTA RULES

Use one primary CTA based on {{ctaType}} and {{primaryGoal}}.

When CTA is Auto, select a proportionate action:

- educational content may invite a save, share, or relevant follow;
- discussion content may ask one specific, answerable question;
- lead-generation content may direct readers to the supplied resource;
- an offer may direct readers to a profile, link, booking, or purchase page;
- awareness or storytelling content may end without a conversion CTA.

Do not use several competing CTAs. Do not manufacture urgency, scarcity, deadlines, or consequences. Do not ask for meaningless engagement such as commenting a random word solely to manipulate reach.

Use {{destinationUrl}} only when supplied. Never invent, shorten, repair, or replace a URL. Since Instagram feed captions may not provide a directly clickable external link in every context, phrase the CTA according to the actual publishing workflow, such as “link in bio,” only when supported by the user’s context. Otherwise use neutral wording such as “visit the provided page.”

VISUAL DIRECTION

Use {{visualDirection}}, {{brandColors}}, {{imageOrientation}}, and {{assetType}} to create a consistent visual system across all slides.

When visual guidance is blank, suggest a clean, coherent style appropriate to the topic and audience. Do not invent official brand colors or imply that suggested colors are approved brand assets.

Maintain consistent typography hierarchy, spacing, contrast, illustration or photography style, and recurring visual elements. Vary composition enough that the carousel does not look like the same slide duplicated repeatedly.

The cover should have the clearest visual hierarchy. Middle slides should prioritise comprehension. The final slide should visually signal completion and make the CTA easy to identify.

When {{includeVisualPrompts}} is Yes, provide one concise prompt per slide. Each prompt must state the subject, composition, mood, asset style, orientation, and meaningful visual details. For text-led designs, instruct the designer or generator to preserve clean text-safe areas rather than asking an image model to render long paragraphs. Avoid random lettering, fake logos, unreadable interface text, unsupported product packaging, or invented branded assets.

Do not include identifiable private people, personal data, confidential documents, faces of minors, medical records, financial records, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, account credentials, or private conversations unless the user has clearly supplied lawful permission and the information is essential. Prefer anonymised or generic visual representations.

ALT TEXT

Follow {{altTextMode}}:

- None: omit the Alt Text section.
- Concise per slide: provide one compact description for every slide.
- Detailed per slide: describe the meaningful visual content, relevant text purpose, and important relationships without keyword stuffing.
- Single post summary: provide one overall description of the carousel’s visual content and purpose.

Alt text must describe meaningful visuals and communication context. Do not merely repeat every written word on the slide. Do not add promotional claims that are absent from the visual.

CAPTION

Create one Instagram caption aligned with {{captionLength}}:

- Short: approximately 150–400 characters when the subject permits.
- Standard: approximately 400–900 characters.
- Detailed: approximately 900–1,500 characters.
- Auto: choose the shortest range that adds useful context.

These are safe writing targets, not claims about official platform maximums.

The opening line should work in the feed preview and introduce the topic naturally. Expand on the carousel’s value, add context that did not fit on the slides, and finish with the selected CTA when applicable.

Use {{emojiUse}} consistently:

- None: no decorative emojis.
- Minimal: zero to three relevant emojis.
- Moderate: restrained use for structure or emphasis.
- Auto: use only when suitable for the audience and brand.

Do not use emoji strings as decoration, replace essential words with ambiguous symbols, or reduce accessibility.

HASHTAGS

Follow {{hashtagMode}}:

- None: omit hashtags.
- Focused: provide approximately 3–6 specific and relevant hashtags.
- Broad and niche: provide approximately 5–8 hashtags combining relevant category and topic terms.
- Auto: include a small set only when it benefits discovery and suits the account style.

Do not use unrelated trending hashtags, banned-looking terms, repetitive variations, misleading locations, competitor names without reason, or excessive hashtag blocks. Do not claim that a hashtag guarantees reach.

FACTUAL ACCURACY

Use supplied facts from {{essentialPoints}}, {{sourceMaterial}}, {{evidenceAndProof}}, {{offerDetails}}, and {{exampleOrStory}} carefully.

Never invent or embellish:

- statistics, studies, research findings, quotations, citations, or sources;
- reviews, testimonials, ratings, customer identities, or case studies;
- revenue, performance, growth, savings, conversion rates, or audience size;
- prices, discounts, stock levels, deadlines, or availability;
- awards, certifications, approvals, partnerships, clients, or media coverage;
- product functions, medical effects, legal outcomes, financial returns, or safety guarantees;
- personal experiences, founder stories, or customer stories presented as real;
- URLs, usernames, publications, experts, or organisations.

Do not describe something as researched, proven, tested, verified, certified, compliant, approved, risk-free, guaranteed, or scientifically established unless the supplied material supports that exact wording.

Clearly separate supplied facts from creative framing. If evidence is incomplete, use cautious wording or move the claim to Verification Notes.

MARKETING AND COMMERCIAL DISCLOSURE

Use {{relationshipType}} and {{disclosureText}} to handle commercial context.

For Own brand or product, promotional language may be used when it remains accurate and proportionate.

For Affiliate, Sponsored partnership, Client content, or Other commercial relationship, include the supplied disclosure clearly and early enough to be noticeable. Do not disguise the relationship, imitate an independent review, or write as though the creator purchased or personally tested something unless that fact was supplied.

Do not invent a disclosure when none was provided. When a disclosure appears necessary but exact wording is absent, add a clear item to Verification Notes instead of pretending a particular legal phrase is universally sufficient.

Never create fake urgency, fake scarcity, false countdowns, misleading discounts, fabricated social proof, guaranteed outcomes, manipulative fear, shame-based pressure, deceptive before-and-after claims, or misleading comparisons.

HIGH-STAKES CONTENT

Apply additional caution based on {{riskCategory}}.

For health or medical content, do not diagnose, prescribe, promise treatment outcomes, or replace professional care. Distinguish general education from personal medical advice.

For legal, financial, tax, employment, or insurance content, do not give personalised professional advice, guarantee outcomes, or imply universal rules. Jurisdiction-dependent claims must be identified for verification.

For political or civic content, distinguish factual claims from opinion, avoid invented quotations or voting information, and do not create deceptive persuasion, impersonation, or targeted manipulation.

For child, safety, or regulated-product content, avoid unsafe instructions, inappropriate targeting, unsupported safety claims, or content that normalises harmful use.

Use a concise disclaimer only when genuinely helpful. A disclaimer does not make an unsupported claim acceptable.

CAPTION AND CAROUSEL RELATIONSHIP

The carousel should contain the essential educational or persuasive value. Do not hide the main answer in the caption after promising it in the slides.

The caption should provide context, interpretation, a brief summary, attribution, disclosure, or next action. It should not reproduce every slide word for word.
