GOAL

Create a clear, accurate Amazon listing that helps buyers understand:

- exactly what the product is;
- who it is designed for;
- what is included;
- how the confirmed features support practical benefits;
- which specifications affect the purchase decision;
- what requirements, compatibility conditions, or limitations matter;
- how the product differs from relevant alternatives.

Persuade through accurate product information, usefulness, and clarity rather than hype, keyword stuffing, or unsupported claims.

POLICY AND CATEGORY RULES

1. Follow the supplied marketplace and category rules.
2. Category-specific requirements take priority over generic formatting rules.
3. Do not assume that limits and formatting are identical across Amazon marketplaces.
4. When {{categoryRules}} is missing, create a conservative draft and clearly avoid using the maximum available length.
5. Do not create listing copy intended to bypass Amazon review, restricted-product rules, or category requirements.
6. Do not include HTML, JavaScript, scripts, or unsupported formatting.

TITLE

Write one recommended product title.

Title rules:

1. Follow the supplied category-specific title limit.
2. For non-media listings governed by Amazon rules effective July 27, 2026, keep the title to no more than 75 characters, including spaces.
3. When the older marketplace rule still applies, never exceed the confirmed category limit.
4. Put the most identifying information first.
5. Use this order when appropriate:

Brand + Product Type + Key Differentiator + Size/Quantity/Compatibility

6. Include only attributes that help identify or compare the product.
7. Use keywords naturally.
8. Do not repeat the same meaningful word more than twice.
9. Do not stuff synonyms or search phrases into the title.
10. Do not include:
- price;
- discount;
- coupon;
- shipping claim;
- seller name;
- subjective promotional language;
- competitor brands;
- unsupported certification;
- unsupported “official” wording;
- ALL CAPS;
- decorative symbols.

Avoid claims such as:

- best;
- number one;
- top-rated;
- premium;
- guaranteed;
- ultimate;
- revolutionary;
- must-have;

unless explicitly supported and permitted.

Provide the exact character count.

ITEM HIGHLIGHTS

When the selected marketplace supports Item Highlights, write one Item Highlights field of no more than 125 characters.

Use it for useful information that no longer fits naturally in the shortened title, such as:

- material;
- recommended use;
- compatibility;
- package quantity;
- intended user;
- major practical differentiator.

Do not simply repeat the title.

Do not add unsupported promotional claims.

Provide the character count.

BULLET POINTS

Write five product bullet points unless the supplied category rules require another number.

Each bullet should:

- cover one distinct buying consideration;
- begin with a concise benefit or feature heading;
- connect a confirmed feature to practical buyer value;
- include relevant specifications where useful;
- help the customer compare the product;
- use natural keywords without repetition;
- remain concise and mobile-readable.

Recommended bullet roles:

1. Primary use case and main benefit.
2. Material, construction, or important specification.
3. Usage, compatibility, fit, or setup.
4. Package contents, maintenance, or convenience.
5. Limitations, intended user, warranty, or another important decision detail.

Do not force this order when another sequence better fits the product.

Do not:

- repeat the same benefit in five ways;
- invent results;
- hide limitations;
- include pricing or promotional language;
- use customer reviews or testimonials;
- mention competitor products;
- include URLs, email addresses, or phone numbers;
- use emojis;
- fill bullets with keyword variations.

Keep every bullet within the supplied category limit.

When no category limit was supplied, aim for concise bullets rather than using the maximum possible length.

FEATURES, BENEFITS, AND OUTCOMES

Clearly distinguish:

- Feature: what the product contains or does.
- Benefit: how that feature helps the buyer.
- Outcome: a possible or measured result supported by evidence.

Every benefit must be connected to a confirmed feature, material, specification, or process.

Do not claim that the product:

- eliminates a problem;
- guarantees comfort;
- prevents injury;
- improves health;
- saves a fixed amount of money;
- lasts a specific number of years;
- produces professional results;
- is safe for everyone;

unless the claim was explicitly supplied, verifiable, permitted, and relevant.

PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

Write one clear product description.

The description should:

1. Explain the product and its primary use.
2. Identify the intended buyer.
3. Present the main practical benefit.
4. Explain the most relevant features and specifications.
5. Clarify what is included.
6. Mention compatibility, requirements, care, or limitations.
7. End with a factual purchasing summary rather than a hard-sell CTA.

Use short paragraphs and plain text.

Do not:

- duplicate all five bullets word for word;
- include testimonials or review language;
- include promotional deadlines;
- mention availability or stock unless confirmed and permitted;
- include URLs or contact information;
- invent a guarantee;
- make medical, financial, safety, or performance promises;
- turn the description into a keyword list.

BACKEND SEARCH TERMS

Create one ready-to-paste Generic Keywords field.

Requirements:

- keep it below the confirmed Amazon byte limit;
- use lowercase unless language rules require otherwise;
- separate search terms with spaces;
- use no commas unless the specific Seller Central field explicitly requires them;
- include relevant synonyms, alternate phrasing, spelling variations, and use cases;
- avoid unnecessary punctuation;
- avoid duplicate words;
- avoid repeating terms already well covered in the title and bullets;
- do not include brand names, competitor names, ASINs, product identifiers, subjective claims, or restricted terms;
- do not include temporary wording such as “new” or “sale”;
- do not include irrelevant high-volume searches.

Provide:

- ready-to-paste search term field;
- estimated byte count;
- excluded duplicate or prohibited terms;
- optional backup field.

For non-ASCII languages, calculate bytes rather than assuming that one character equals one byte.

A+ CONTENT

Create an A+ Content outline only when {{aPlusEligible}} confirms eligibility.

The A+ outline should explain:

- the product’s primary use;
- important product details;
- practical differences;

Do not invent:

- product images;
- customer quotes;
- comparison products;
- awards;
- metrics;
- certifications;
- guarantees;
- before-and-after results.

Do not place pricing, promotions, coupons, shipping promises, contact details, URLs, or calls for reviews inside A+ Content.

When eligibility is not confirmed, output:

REGULATED AND SENSITIVE CLAIMS

For health, medical, supplement, cosmetic, safety, children’s, electrical, food, or other regulated products:

- use only approved compliance language;
- do not make diagnosis, treatment, prevention, cure, or risk-free claims;
- do not invent testing;
- do not imply certification from Amazon;
- do not describe the product as FDA-approved, clinically proven, non-toxic, hypoallergenic, organic, or safe unless confirmed and legally supportable;
- clearly state relevant age, usage, compatibility, or safety restrictions.

STYLE

Write in clear, practical, buyer-focused language.

Avoid:

- corporate storytelling in bullet points;
- excessive adjectives;
- hype;
- keyword stuffing;
- artificial urgency;
- ALL CAPS;
- exclamation marks;
- vague lifestyle promises;
- AI clichés.

Avoid phrases such as:

- game-changing;
- revolutionary;
- unmatched quality;
- perfect for everyone;
- buy now;
- don’t miss out;
- transform your life;
- results guaranteed;
- the ultimate solution.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the listing, silently verify that:

- the marketplace and category were considered;
- the title fits the applicable limit;
- Item Highlights fits 125 characters when included;
- all five bullets cover distinct buyer needs;
- every benefit is supported by supplied information;
- no material, dimension, certification, result, warranty, or package item was invented;
- compatibility and limitations are clear;
- no competitor trademark is used;
- no prohibited promotion or contact information appears;
- backend terms fit the byte limit;
- backend terms do not duplicate each other;
- A+ Content is generated only when eligibility was confirmed;
- the listing does not sound like generic AI marketing copy.
