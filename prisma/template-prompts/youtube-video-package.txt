GOAL

Create a YouTube package that accurately communicates what the video delivers and helps the right viewer decide whether to watch.

The title and thumbnail should work as one combined promise.

The description should clarify the value, provide useful context, and direct the viewer toward one relevant next step.

Prioritize:

* clarity;
* audience relevance;
* an accurate content promise;
* meaningful differentiation;
* visual curiosity;
* search clarity where relevant;
* consistency between title, thumbnail, description, and actual video.

Do not use misleading clickbait, unsupported claims, keyword stuffing, or fabricated details.

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, results, dates, names, sources, quotations, products, and experiences supplied in the prompt.
2. Do not invent:

* guest names;
* credentials;
* studies;
* statistics;
* customer results;
* personal experiences;
* revenue figures;
* experiments;
* product features;
* links;
* discounts;
* downloads;
* related videos;
* timestamps from a completed edit.
3. Do not imply that the video contains a tutorial, test, interview, comparison, review, experiment, template, or case study unless it actually does.
4. Do not claim that the creator personally tested or experienced something unless this appears in {{personalExperience}}.
5. Do not make the title or thumbnail promise a result that the video does not deliver.
6. Do not use a number in the title or thumbnail unless that number is supported by the video.
7. Do not add the current year unless the video is genuinely time-sensitive or year-specific.
8. Do not imply that information is new, breaking, official, leaked, banned, secret, or recently changed unless confirmed.
9. Do not use a guest’s name, company, logo, image, or quotation unless supplied and approved.
10. If important information is missing, use neutral wording rather than filling the gap with assumptions.

TITLE STRATEGY

Create one recommended title and four alternate titles.

All titles must:

* accurately match the video;
* remain within YouTube’s 100-character limit;
* put the most important or interesting information early;
* be understandable without seeing the thumbnail;
* communicate a clear reason to watch;
* use the primary topic naturally;
* match the audience and video format;
* avoid keyword stuffing;
* avoid unsupported superlatives;
* use normal capitalization appropriate for {{language}}.

Aim for concise titles when possible. Do not fill the full 100-character limit merely because it is available.

Create the five titles using meaningfully different approaches:

1. Recommended title:
The strongest overall combination of clarity, relevance, and curiosity.

2. Search-led:
Closely match the viewer’s likely query or task.

3. Outcome-led:
Emphasize the confirmed knowledge, capability, or result the viewer will gain.

4. Tension- or contrast-led:
Present a real misconception, trade-off, problem, or contrast supported by the video.

5. Direct or authority-led:
Clearly state what the video examines, demonstrates, compares, or explains.

Do not create alternatives that differ only by replacing words such as:

* how;
* why;
* discover;
* learn;
* understand.

Avoid misleading title patterns such as:

* “You Won’t Believe…”
* “This Changes Everything”
* “Nobody Is Talking About This”
* “The Truth They Hide”
* “Watch Before It Gets Deleted”
* “The Secret Nobody Knows”
* “I Tried This and Got Rich”
* “The Ultimate Guide” unless the video is genuinely comprehensive;
* “Everything You Need to Know” unless that claim is realistic;
* “99% of People Get This Wrong” unless supported.

TITLE AND THUMBNAIL PAIRING

For each title, ensure the thumbnail adds a second layer of meaning.

The thumbnail should not merely repeat the complete title.

A useful pairing may work like this:

* Title explains the topic; thumbnail shows the consequence.
* Title presents the question; thumbnail shows the central contrast.
* Title states the method; thumbnail shows the result or obstacle.
* Title describes the investigation; thumbnail shows the key evidence.

Do not create a contradiction between the title and thumbnail.

DESCRIPTION

Write one complete video description.

The description may contain up to 5,000 characters, but use only the length necessary.

Use this structure:

1. Opening lines:
Write two concise lines that are useful before the description is expanded.

They should:

* clarify the viewer benefit;
* expand on the title rather than repeat it;
* accurately describe the video;
* naturally include one or two important topic phrases when useful;
* avoid generic greetings and channel introductions.

2. Summary:
Explain what the video covers and why it matters.

3. Key points:
Mention the major confirmed sections without turning the description into a transcript.

4. Chapters:
Include them only when appropriate and supported by the supplied outline.

5. Primary CTA:
Include one clear action connected to {{desiredAction}}.

6. Approved links:
Include only supplied links and label them clearly.

7. Sources or references:
Include only supplied sources when relevant.

8. Optional channel context:
Briefly explain what the channel covers when that helps a new viewer.

Do not:

* repeat the title word for word;
* front-load the description with several links;
* include a large keyword list;
* add tags as plain text;
* invent URLs;
* create fake affiliate disclosures;
* invent sponsorships;
* promise a free resource that was not supplied;
* write “link below” when no link exists;
* use several competing CTAs.

DESCRIPTION OPENING

Avoid generic openings such as:

* “Welcome back to the channel”
* “In today’s video, we’re going to…”
* “Make sure you watch until the end”
* “Don’t forget to like and subscribe”
* “In today’s fast-paced world”
* “This video is a game-changer”

Open with the actual value, question, finding, or situation.

KEYWORDS

Use one or two main topic phrases naturally in the title and early description when they fit.

Use keyword variations only where they improve clarity.

Do not:

* repeat the exact keyword unnaturally;
* create a paragraph of search phrases;
* insert unrelated high-volume keywords;
* sacrifice a natural title for exact keyword matching;
* treat the description as a list of tags.

TAGS

Generate 0–8 focused YouTube tags.

Prioritize tags that help with:

* common misspellings;
* alternate spellings;
* abbreviations;
* product or person name variations;
* closely related terminology;
* language variations when relevant.

Do not generate tags merely to fill a quota.

Do not include unrelated popular topics.

Do not repeat a large number of minor variations of the same phrase.

Do not place the tags inside the public description.

If no useful spelling variations or related terms exist, provide fewer tags or write:

No additional tags recommended.

CHAPTERS

Create draft chapters only when:

* the video has clearly defined sections;
* {{sourceDetails}} provides enough information;
* the duration supports meaningful navigation;
* at least three useful chapters can be created.

Chapter requirements:

* begin with 00:00;
* include at least three timestamps;
* list timestamps in ascending order;
* keep each chapter at least 10 seconds long;
* use descriptive chapter names;
* make the final timestamps fit within {{duration}} minutes.

Important:

The generated timestamps are planning estimates, not confirmed final timestamps.

Label them:

Draft chapters — verify after final edit

Do not pretend that estimated timestamps match a video that has not yet been edited.

Do not divide the duration evenly without considering the likely complexity of each section.

Do not create chapters for:

* very short videos where chapters provide little value;
* videos without clear sections;
* Shorts;
* supplied outlines that are too incomplete.

When chapters cannot be created reliably, write:

Chapters should be added after the final edit.

THUMBNAIL TEXT

Create three short thumbnail text options.

Each option should:

* contain approximately 2–5 words;
* be readable at small size;
* add meaning not already fully stated in the title;
* create curiosity through a real contrast, consequence, question, or key detail;
* match the supplied thumbnail assets;
* remain understandable without tiny supporting text;
* avoid unnecessary punctuation.

Do not:

* copy the complete title;
* use a full sentence;
* use several small labels;
* include unsupported numbers;
* use vague phrases such as “Must Watch”;
* imply shock, danger, failure, or success that the video does not contain.

THUMBNAIL CONCEPTS

For each thumbnail text option, suggest a matching visual concept.

Describe:

* the main visual subject;
* composition;
* facial expression only when a creator image is available;
* object, interface, document, or comparison shown;
* visual contrast;
* where the short text should appear;
* what should remain clearly visible on mobile.

Use one clear focal point.

Avoid:

* clutter;
* several unrelated objects;
* tiny screenshots;
* long text;
* fake interfaces;
* invented graphs;
* fabricated comments;
* misleading before-and-after images;
* guest images that were not supplied;
* exaggerated facial expressions when they do not fit the channel.

PINNED COMMENT

Write one optional pinned comment.

It should do one useful job:

* ask a specific discussion question;
* provide a confirmed correction or clarification;
* direct viewers to a supplied related resource;
* summarize one practical next step;
* disclose a relevant commercial relationship.

Do not automatically ask for likes or subscriptions.

Do not invent a link.

If no pinned comment would add value, write:

No pinned comment needed.

CTA

Use one primary CTA matching {{desiredAction}}.

Possible CTAs include:

* watch a related supplied video;
* subscribe for a clearly defined content theme;
* answer one specific question;
* download an approved resource;
* join a newsletter;
* visit an approved product page;
* try one practical step.

Do not combine several actions into one sentence.

Do not automatically write:

“Like, comment, subscribe, and hit the bell.”

When asking for a subscription, explain what future content the viewer will receive.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the package, silently verify that:

* all titles stay within 100 characters;
* all title options use genuinely different approaches;
* the title and thumbnail work together;
* the promise matches the actual video;
* the description opening adds value rather than repeating the title;
* no claim, source, link, guest, result, or personal experience was invented;
* keywords appear naturally;
* tags are limited and genuinely useful;
* draft chapters begin at 00:00;
* there are at least three draft chapters when chapters are included;
* no chapter is shorter than 10 seconds;
* estimated timestamps are clearly marked for verification;
* thumbnail text remains short and readable;
* thumbnail concepts are achievable with the supplied assets;
* the CTA is singular and relevant;
* the package does not sound like generic AI-generated YouTube metadata.
