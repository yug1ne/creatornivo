TASK

Create one production-ready YouTube Video Package for recording, editing, uploading, and publishing. Combine audience fit, accurate packaging, a natural script or outline, practical production notes, and upload-ready metadata without fabrication or misleading clickbait.

HOW TO USE THE INPUTS

Treat supplied facts, claims, quotations, examples, prices, dates, links, and disclosures as the approved factual basis. Do not claim independent research, testing, personal experience, professional review, or verification unless explicitly supported.

Optional fields may be blank, contain “Auto,” or be irrelevant. In those cases:

infer a neutral, useful choice from the core brief;

omit sections or details that would add no value;

never display “not provided” in viewer-facing copy;

never fabricate missing facts, credentials, links, results, stories, sponsors, or proof;

place any necessary verification warning only in the final Claim and Publishing Check, not inside the spoken script or public description.

Resolve conflicts in this order: accuracy and safety; restrictions; required facts and disclosures; audience and goal; platform fit; style; optimization.

STRATEGIC APPROACH

Identify the most useful viewer promise: what the intended viewer should understand, feel, decide, or be able to do after watching. Build one coherent angle around that promise instead of covering every possible subtopic.

Match the format to the selected video type:

tutorial or how-to: clear outcome, prerequisites, ordered steps, demonstrations, mistakes, recap;

educational explainer: question, context, core explanation, examples, implications, takeaway;

commentary or analysis: thesis, evidence, counterpoint, interpretation, limitations, conclusion;

review or comparison: criteria, evidence, trade-offs, suitable users, unsuitable users, verdict without false certainty;

story or case study: setup, tension, decisions, turning points, outcome, lessons, limitations;

list or roundup: clear selection logic, distinct entries, useful comparisons, final recommendation;

product or service video: audience problem, relevant capability, demonstration or explanation, limitations, next step;

interview or podcast-style video: opening context, segment sequence, questions or talking points, transitions, recap;

other: infer the most natural YouTube-native structure.

Do not force drama, controversy, humor, or aggressive sales when they do not fit.

PACKAGING RULES

Generate exactly {{titleVariantCount}} title options, within the allowed range of 3 to 10. Each title must accurately represent the video, lead with the most important idea where possible, and avoid unsupported superlatives, false certainty, deceptive omissions, fake urgency, sensational punctuation, excessive capitalization, and irrelevant keywords. Keep every title under 100 characters and normally target a concise 45–70 character range. Vary the angle meaningfully.

Use {{titleAngle}} when supplied. When blank, select a balanced mix suited to the goal and search intent. Search-led titles should sound human, not like a keyword list. Curiosity-led titles must create an honest information gap that the video actually resolves.

Create three thumbnail concepts. For each, give the composition, focal subject, optional 2–5-word text, core contrast, and matching title.

Follow {{thumbnailStyle}}, available assets, brand voice, and packaging restrictions. A thumbnail must represent the actual video. Avoid tiny text, clutter, fabricated screenshots, shocking or sexualized imagery, graphic violence, and misleading before-and-after concepts.

Select one recommended title-and-thumbnail pairing and explain its fit in one concise sentence. Do not predict views, click-through rate, ranking, virality, or revenue.

OPENING AND RETENTION

Design the opening around {{openingDirection}}. If it is Auto, choose the clearest approach for the viewer promise and format. The first 15–30 seconds should quickly establish relevance, credibility based only on supplied facts, and what the viewer will gain. Avoid long greetings, channel history, generic hype, unnecessary self-introduction, and delayed context.

Create a retention map covering the opening promise, progression, major sections, proof or demonstration opportunities, CTA placement, and closing payoff.

Adapt pacing to {{retentionStyle}}. Use substantive retention devices such as previews, questions, examples, demonstrations, contrasts, recaps, or visual changes. Avoid empty teasers, manipulative withholding, and repeated “watch until the end” lines.

SCRIPT DEPTH AND DURATION

Adapt the main deliverable to {{scriptDepth}}:

Full script: write the complete spoken narration, including natural transitions, concise presenter directions, and selective visual notes.

Detailed outline: write a recording-ready section outline with ordered talking points, examples, transitions, estimated section timing, and the intended takeaway for each section.

Hybrid script + talking points: fully script the hook, introduction, major transitions, CTA, and closing; provide detailed talking points for the main body.

Match {{targetDuration}} using a natural language-appropriate speaking pace. Give an estimated word count or speaking range. Do not pad; if the scope cannot support the requested length, create a stronger shorter structure and flag the mismatch.

Write for speech, not an essay. Use conversational phrasing natural to {{outputLanguage}}. Avoid filler, robotic signposting, repeated summaries, generic motivation, and AI clichés. Maintain {{toneStyle}}, {{brandVoice}}, and {{presenterStyle}} without caricature.

CONTENT DEVELOPMENT

Address {{viewerProblem}} when supplied. Integrate {{mustInclude}}, {{storyExamples}}, and {{visualAssets}} where they improve understanding. Follow {{mustAvoid}} strictly. If an example is supplied but cannot be verified as factual, frame it as hypothetical unless the input clearly identifies it as real.

Use {{sourceMaterial}} only for supported claims. Paraphrase; do not invent citations or links, reproduce long copyrighted passages, provide unlicensed lyrics, or imitate a living creator’s distinctive style.

When evidence is incomplete, qualify the claim, narrow it, or omit it. Distinguish fact, estimate, opinion, and recommendation. For comparisons, state the criteria and avoid declaring a universal winner when suitability depends on the viewer.

PRODUCTION DIRECTIONS

Make production notes achievable with {{filmingResources}}, {{visualAssets}}, and {{editingComplexity}}. Suggest practical A-roll, B-roll, screen recording, graphics, captions, demonstrations, cutaways, or on-screen text only when feasible. Do not require expensive equipment, actors, locations, animation, licensed footage, or complex visual effects unless the inputs support them.

Visual notes should explain what viewers see and why it helps. Separate essential on-screen text from optional visuals, and keep wording concise.

DESCRIPTION, CHAPTERS, AND DISCOVERY

Write an upload-ready description under 5,000 characters. Normally use:

a concise opening summary that makes sense before any “show more” expansion;

the main viewer benefit or context;

one primary CTA;

relevant links from {{descriptionLinks}};

commercial disclosure when required;

chapter timestamps when included;

brief credits or source notes only when supplied and useful.

Do not invent URLs, codes, affiliations, handles, contacts, or resource names.

Handle chapters according to {{chapterMode}}:

Include: provide description-ready chapters when the structure and duration support them;

Omit: do not provide chapters;

Auto: include them for a clearly segmented longer video and omit them for a short or continuous format.

When chapters are included, begin at 0:00, provide at least three timestamps in ascending order, and ensure every proposed chapter spans at least 10 seconds. Timestamps are planning estimates unless the final edit timing was supplied; label them for adjustment after editing.

Provide focused keyword and tag themes from {{searchIntent}} and {{targetKeywords}}. Avoid stuffing, duplicates, unrelated trends, or discovery guarantees; include misspellings only when useful.

PINNED COMMENT AND CTA

Create one pinned comment that adds value rather than repeating the description. It may ask a specific discussion question, clarify a resource, point to a related next step, or disclose an affiliation. Keep it natural and avoid engagement bait, manufactured controversy, or requests for meaningless comments.

Use {{primaryCTA}} when specific. When it is Auto, choose one proportional CTA based on {{primaryGoal}}. Allow one soft engagement prompt and one primary action at most. Place the CTA after relevant value.

COMMERCIAL, DISCLOSURE, AND SAFETY RULES

Use {{commercialRelationship}}, {{promotionDetails}}, and {{disclosureText}} to handle sponsorships, affiliate links, owned products, paid partnerships, or other commercial interests transparently. Do not disguise promotion as independent review or personal recommendation. If a relationship is declared but exact disclosure wording is blank, provide a plain-language disclosure suggestion in the description and Claim and Publishing Check.

For {{regulatedContext}}, use cautious, informational wording. Consider {{jurisdiction}} where relevant. Do not provide personalized medical, legal, financial, safety, employment, tax, insurance, or political persuasion advice. Do not diagnose, guarantee outcomes, present speculation as fact, or claim regulatory approval. Add only a relevant concise disclaimer.

Respect {{privacyRestrictions}}. Do not expose private or sensitive data; anonymize where required. Do not encourage harassment, brigading, fake engagement, covert promotion, or policy evasion.

Never invent facts, statistics, studies, quotations, testimonials, reviews, identities, case studies, results, revenue, users, prices, discounts, deadlines, capabilities, certifications, awards, partnerships, clients, media mentions, funding, approvals, medical outcomes, first-hand experience, links, scarcity, urgency, sponsorships, reactions, or performance guarantees.

Respect {{additionalRequirements}} when compatible with accuracy, safety, platform fit, and the other explicit constraints.

OUTPUT FORMAT

Return only the following completed package in {{outputLanguage}}. Do not include hidden reasoning, chain-of-thought, or a preamble about your process.

1. Video Strategy Snapshot

Include: viewer promise, primary angle, audience fit, format choice, estimated duration, script mode, and one-sentence CTA plan.

2. Title Options

Provide exactly {{titleVariantCount}} numbered titles. Add a short angle label after each title.

3. Thumbnail Concepts

Provide three concepts with visual, optional text, contrast, and matching title number.

4. Recommended Packaging Pair

State the selected title and thumbnail concept, followed by one concise rationale.

5. Retention and Structure Map

Show the opening promise, ordered sections with estimated timing, retention opportunities, CTA placement, and closing payoff.

6. Recording Script or Detailed Outline

Follow the selected {{scriptDepth}}. Use clear section headings. Put spoken words in normal text. Put production cues in concise brackets. Do not clutter every line with directions.

7. Production Notes

List essential A-roll, B-roll, screen captures, graphics, on-screen text, asset usage, and editing notes that fit the available resources.

8. Upload-Ready Description

Provide the final description as one clean block. Keep it under 5,000 characters. Include only supplied or safely inferred links, disclosures, credits, and calls to action.

9. Video Chapters

Include only when required by {{chapterMode}} or selected by Auto. Mark estimated timestamps as “adjust after final edit.” Otherwise state “Chapters omitted for this format” in one line.

10. Keyword and Tag Themes

Provide a focused comma-separated list, followed by up to five natural search phrases when useful.

11. Pinned Comment

Provide one ready-to-post comment.

12. Claim and Publishing Check

List only actionable verification, source, link, timing, disclosure, privacy, high-stakes, or duration issues. If none are apparent, say no obvious unsupported claims or publishing blockers were identified from the inputs; never claim legal or platform approval.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before outputting, silently verify:

every title is accurate and below 100 characters;

the description is below 5,000 characters;

the script or outline matches the chosen depth and approximate duration;

the first section delivers relevance quickly;

the title, thumbnail, description, script, and CTA describe the same video;

chapters follow the required timestamp structure when included;

all supplied must-include and must-avoid instructions are respected;

production notes are feasible with the supplied resources;

commercial relationships are disclosed;

no private or unsupported information is exposed;

no facts, sources, links, results, testimonials, or experiences were invented;

no deceptive clickbait, fake urgency, spam, or manipulation was introduced;

blank optional fields do not create empty headings or awkward placeholders.

Output only the completed YouTube Video Package.
