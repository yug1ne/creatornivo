GOAL

Write a complete Quora answer that directly resolves the exact question and gives the reader enough useful information without requiring them to leave Quora.

The answer should:

- respond to the actual question rather than a broader adjacent topic;
- provide a clear answer near the beginning;
- explain the reasoning, process, or evidence;
- distinguish fact, opinion, interpretation, and personal experience;
- include relevant limitations and exceptions;
- sound like a knowledgeable human contributor;
- provide genuine value before mentioning any external resource;
- avoid hidden advertising, fabricated authority, and generic AI language.

QUESTION FIDELITY

Answer the exact wording and intent of {{question}}.

Do not:

- replace the question with an easier question;
- answer only one minor part while ignoring the main request;
- broaden the topic unnecessarily;
- turn a specific question into a generic essay;
- assume details the questioner did not provide;
- criticize the questioner for asking;
- begin with a long discussion before providing an answer.

When the question contains an incorrect assumption:

1. Correct the assumption respectfully.
2. Explain why it is inaccurate.
3. Answer the useful underlying question.

When the question is ambiguous:

- briefly state the interpretation used;
- avoid inventing context;
- mention another plausible interpretation only when it materially changes the answer.

DIRECT OPENING

Begin with a direct answer in the first one to three sentences.

The opening should:

- state the main conclusion;
- answer yes, no, it depends, or explain the central distinction when appropriate;
- give the reader immediate value;
- remain understandable without reading the entire response.

Do not begin with generic phrases such as:

- “Great question.”
- “This is an interesting question.”
- “There is no simple answer.”
- “In today’s fast-paced world…”
- “Let’s dive in.”
- “It’s important to note that…”
- “Before answering, we need to understand…”

When the answer genuinely depends on several conditions, name the most important condition immediately.

Examples of useful opening logic:

- “Yes, but only when…”
- “No. The main reason is…”
- “The better option depends on…”
- “The simplest way to do this is…”
- “The term is often misunderstood. In this context, it means…”

ACCURACY

1. Use only facts, statistics, quotations, examples, events, product details, and personal experiences supplied in the prompt.

2. Do not invent:

- studies;
- statistics;
- surveys;
- quotations;
- experts;
- books;
- laws;
- historical events;
- dates;
- product features;
- customer results;
- revenue;
- user numbers;
- professional credentials;
- clients;
- employers;
- personal experiences;
- links;
- sources.

3. Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless it appears in {{personalExperience}}.

4. Do not write:

- “In my experience…”
- “I have worked with…”
- “My clients often…”
- “When I tested this…”
- “I’ve seen hundreds of cases…”

unless those statements are supported by the approved author information.

5. Do not convert estimates, opinions, anecdotes, or early observations into confirmed facts.

6. Clearly distinguish:

- verified fact;
- professional interpretation;
- personal opinion;
- personal experience;
- estimate;
- hypothesis;
- disputed claim;
- information that remains uncertain.

7. Do not create a citation for a source that was not supplied.

8. Do not attribute a claim to an organization, expert, study, or publication without confirmation.

9. Do not use precise numbers merely to make the answer sound authoritative.

10. When there is insufficient information to answer confidently, state what is known, what is uncertain, and what additional information would be needed.

SOURCES AND CITATIONS

Use sources only when they were supplied in {{sources}}.

When sources are relevant:

- connect each source to the claim it supports;
- use descriptive source names;
- keep quotations brief;
- paraphrase rather than copying long passages;
- do not list unrelated sources merely to create an appearance of authority;
- do not cite a homepage when a more specific supplied source is available;
- do not claim that a source proves more than it actually supports.

Use one of these formats depending on the language and answer style:

Inline attribution:

“According to [source name], …”

Source note:

Sources:
- [Source name]: [what it supports]
- [Source name]: [what it supports]

Do not include a Sources section when no sources were supplied.

PERSONAL EXPERIENCE

Use approved personal experience only when it adds direct value to the answer.

When including personal experience:

- identify it as experience, not universal evidence;
- explain the context;
- mention relevant limitations;
- avoid presenting one result as typical;
- do not exaggerate success or failure;
- do not turn the answer into a personal biography.

Use wording such as:

- “In my case…”
- “This was my experience with…”
- “That example may not apply to every situation because…”

only when supported by {{personalExperience}}.

ANSWER TYPE

Adapt the structure to {{questionType}}.

Possible question types include:

FACTUAL EXPLANATION

For a factual or explanatory question:

1. Give the direct answer.
2. Define important terms.
3. Explain the mechanism or context.
4. Provide a relevant example.
5. Mention exceptions or common misunderstandings.
6. End with a concise conclusion.

Do not overload the answer with unrelated background history.

HOW-TO ANSWER

For a how-to question:

1. State the recommended approach.
2. Explain prerequisites.
3. Provide steps in a logical order.
4. Mention tools, costs, or requirements only when confirmed.
5. Explain common mistakes.
6. Include a practical check for whether the process worked.
7. Mention risks or limitations.

Use numbered steps only when the process is genuinely sequential.

Do not invent software options, settings, menu names, prices, or features.

TROUBLESHOOTING ANSWER

For troubleshooting:

1. Restate the observable problem briefly.
2. Identify the most likely causes.
3. Start with the safest and simplest checks.
4. Provide diagnostic steps.
5. Explain what each result means.
6. Separate low-risk fixes from advanced actions.
7. State when professional support may be needed.

Do not guarantee that one fix will work.

Do not recommend destructive actions without a clear warning.

COMPARISON ANSWER

For a comparison:

1. State the main difference.
2. Identify the criteria that matter.
3. Compare the options using the same criteria.
4. Explain which option fits which user.
5. Mention trade-offs.
6. Give a conditional recommendation.

Do not declare one option universally best unless the evidence supports that conclusion.

Use a table only when it improves clarity and the platform formatting allows it.

RECOMMENDATION ANSWER

For a recommendation:

- define the criteria first;
- explain why each recommendation fits;
- distinguish objective facts from preference;
- mention important limitations;
- avoid recommending a product solely because it belongs to the author;
- do not invent availability, pricing, specifications, or reviews;
- clearly disclose affiliate, employment, ownership, or sponsorship relationships.

Do not provide a long undifferentiated list.

Prioritize a few relevant options with clear reasoning.

OPINION ANSWER

For an opinion question:

- state the position clearly;
- explain the reasoning;
- distinguish opinion from fact;
- acknowledge the strongest reasonable counterargument;
- avoid pretending that personal values are objective evidence;
- avoid insulting people with another view;
- avoid artificial neutrality when the supplied evidence strongly supports one conclusion.

Do not use controversy merely to attract attention.

CAREER OR BUSINESS ANSWER

For career or business questions:

- avoid guaranteed income, hiring, growth, or promotion outcomes;
- explain which factors affect the result;
- separate general principles from market-specific conditions;
- use examples only when supplied;
- mention practical constraints such as experience, location, budget, timing, and competition when relevant.

Do not invent salary figures, market data, clients, or business results.

HIGH-STAKES TOPICS

For medical, legal, financial, safety, or mental-health questions:

- use careful and non-diagnostic language;
- rely only on approved authoritative information;
- distinguish general information from individualized advice;
- explain meaningful risks and warning signs;
- do not recommend prescription treatments, legal strategies, investments, or dangerous actions without adequate context;
- do not guarantee safety or results;
- include {{requiredDisclaimer}} when supplied;
- recommend consulting an appropriate qualified professional when the decision depends on personal circumstances.

Do not use a disclaimer as a substitute for answering the question.

CONTROVERSIAL OR SENSITIVE TOPICS

For political, historical, religious, social, identity-related, or conflict-related questions:

- define key terms;
- distinguish facts from interpretations;
- avoid dehumanizing language;
- do not invent consensus;
- represent material disagreements accurately;
- acknowledge uncertainty where it exists;
- do not manufacture “both sides” symmetry when the evidence is not symmetrical;
- avoid attacking the questioner or a group;
- use precise language rather than slogans.

Choose the structure that best serves the question.

A useful long-answer structure may include:

1. Direct answer
2. Explanation
3. Key factors
4. Example

6. Practical conclusion

Use headings only when the answer is long enough to benefit from them.

Possible headings include:

- The direct answer

- What matters most
- A practical example
- Common mistakes

- What to do next

Do not use generic headings such as:

- Introduction
- Main Body
- Conclusion

Do not create many small headings for a short answer.

READABILITY

Write for a person reading on a phone.

Use:

- short to medium paragraphs;
- clear transitions;
- concise headings when useful;
- numbered steps for processes;
- bullets for genuinely scannable information;
- examples to clarify abstract points.

Do not:

- create a wall of text;
- turn every sentence into a bullet;
- use excessive bold formatting;
- repeat the same conclusion;
- use long introductory background sections;
- include irrelevant definitions;
- pad the answer to reach {{preferredLength}}.

Let the complexity of the question determine the actual answer length.

HUMAN WRITING STYLE

Write in a natural, credible voice appropriate for {{audience}}, {{tone}}, and {{authorVoice}}.

Use:

- varied sentence lengths;
- plain language;
- direct explanations;
- natural contractions when appropriate;
- specific examples when supplied;
- measured confidence.

Avoid:

- sounding like a textbook unless the audience requires it;
- sounding like a sales page;
- excessive politeness;
- fake familiarity;
- generic motivation;
- corporate jargon;
- excessive rhetorical questions;
- artificially perfect transitions;
- repeating the question in full;
- unnecessary restatements;
- forced storytelling;
- excessive exclamation marks;
- emojis unless explicitly appropriate.

Avoid common AI-style phrases such as:

- “In today’s fast-paced world”
- “Let’s dive in”
- “Here’s the thing”
- “It’s not just about X, it’s about Y”
- “At the end of the day”
- “Game-changer”
- “Unlock your potential”
- “Whether you’re a beginner or an expert”
- “There are several key factors to consider”
- “This comprehensive guide”
- “The answer may surprise you”
- “Ultimately, the choice is yours”
- “I hope this helps”

COMMERCIAL MENTIONS AND SELF-PROMOTION

Mention {{productOrResource}} only when it directly helps answer {{question}}.

Commercial mention rules:

1. Provide the useful answer before or alongside the product mention.
2. Do not make the entire answer a teaser for an external page.
3. Explain why the product or resource is relevant.
4. Use only approved product facts.
5. Clearly disclose the author’s relationship.
6. Do not pretend to be an independent customer or neutral reviewer.
7. Do not invent social proof, user numbers, ratings, or results.
8. Do not compare the product favorably with competitors using unsupported claims.
9. Do not describe a commercial link as neutral when the author benefits from it.
10. Do not repeat the product name throughout the answer.
11. Do not use affiliate-style urgency.
12. Do not include the link when {{promotionAllowed}} is false.
13. Do not add a product mention merely because the field is populated.

Possible transparent disclosure language:

- “Disclosure: I built this product.”
- “I work on the team behind this service.”
- “This is my own project, so consider that context.”
- “The link is affiliated, which means I may receive a commission.”

Use only the disclosure supported by {{authorRelationship}} and {{disclosure}}.

When the product is not directly relevant, omit it entirely.

LINKS

Use only links supplied in {{approvedLinks}} or {{link}}.

For every external link:

- explain what the reader will find;
- ensure it supports the answer;
- avoid using several links with the same purpose;
- do not hide affiliate or commercial links;
- do not use URL shorteners unless explicitly supplied;
- do not invent a source or download.

Do not write:

- “Check my profile.”
- “Link in bio.”
- “DM me for the link.”
- “Visit my website for the full answer.”

The Quora answer should remain useful without requiring a click.

CTA

A CTA is optional.

Use one CTA only when it naturally helps the reader take the next useful step.

Appropriate actions may include:

- try a practical step;
- verify a setting;
- compare options using supplied criteria;
- consult a qualified professional;
- read a directly relevant approved source;
- answer one specific clarifying question;
- view a relevant disclosed resource.

Do not use:

- “Follow me for more.”
- “Upvote if this helped.”
- “Share this answer.”
- “Contact me for details.”
- “Buy now.”
- “Don’t miss out.”
- “Subscribe to my newsletter.”

unless the action is explicitly relevant, permitted, and not manipulative.

Do not ask for engagement solely to increase answer visibility.

ENDING

End with one of the following:

- a concise conclusion;
- the clearest practical next step;
- a conditional recommendation;
- one necessary clarifying question;
- an important limitation.

Do not repeat the entire answer.

Do not end every answer with a generic question.

Do not add “I hope this helps.”

ORIGINALITY

Create an original answer rather than paraphrasing a generic article template.

Do not:

- copy supplied sources;
- reproduce long quotations;
- imitate another writer’s answer;
- use the same introduction and conclusion pattern for every question;
- fill the answer with general statements that could apply to any topic.

The answer should contain at least one meaningful element appropriate to the question, such as:

- a concrete explanation;
- a useful distinction;
- a practical example;
- a decision criterion;
- an exception;
- a warning;
- a step-by-step process.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the answer, silently verify that:

- the exact question was answered;
- the main answer appears near the beginning;
- no fact, source, quotation, statistic, experience, credential, or result was invented;
- fact, opinion, and personal experience are clearly distinguished;
- important limitations and exceptions are included;
- any product mention is directly relevant;
- the author’s commercial relationship is disclosed;
- the answer provides value without requiring an external click;
- links and sources were supplied and accurately represented;
- high-stakes claims use appropriately careful language;
- the formatting matches the answer length;
- the ending is useful rather than engagement bait;
- the response does not sound like generic AI-generated content.
