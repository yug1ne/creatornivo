GOAL
Write a coherent thread that communicates one clear idea, provides useful information, and moves naturally from the opening to the final action.
The thread should feel like one connected piece of writing, not a collection of unrelated posts or a generic AI-generated list.
ACCURACY


Use only the facts, examples, experiences, statistics, results, quotations, and observations supplied in {{sourceDetails}}.
Do not invent personal stories, customer results, revenue figures, studies, dates, conversations, failures, or achievements.
Do not imply that the author personally experienced something unless that information was supplied.
Do not invent sources, links, experts, quotations, or statistics.
Clearly distinguish confirmed facts from opinions, hypotheses, and personal interpretation.
Avoid guaranteed medical, financial, career, business, or performance claims.
If evidence is limited, use measured language rather than presenting assumptions as facts.
THREAD STRUCTURE
Create exactly {{tweetCount}} numbered posts unless the requested number would force unnecessary repetition.
Use this narrative flow when appropriate:
Hook:
   Introduce the main tension, useful promise, surprising contrast, or specific problem.
Context:
   Explain what the topic is and why it matters.
Development:
   Present the core insights, steps, examples, arguments, or evidence.
Practical value:
   Show what the reader can do, avoid, reconsider, or apply.
Conclusion:
   Bring the central idea together.
CTA:
   End with one relevant next step.
Each post should contribute a new idea. Do not repeat earlier points merely to reach the requested count.
The posts should build on one another while remaining understandable when viewed separately. Do not force every post to restate the full context.
HOOK
The first post should attract the right reader without misleading them.
A strong hook may use:


a specific problem;
a clear contradiction;
an unexpected but supported observation;
a practical promise;
a concise position;
a question with real substance;
a result or number only when supplied.
Avoid empty or manipulative hooks such as:
“Stop scrolling”
“Nobody is talking about this”
“This changes everything”
“You won’t believe this”
“I found the secret”
“This thread will make you rich”
“99% of people get this wrong” unless supported;
“Bookmark this before it disappears”
“The algorithm doesn’t want you to know this”
“A thread you can’t afford to miss”
POST LENGTH
Keep every numbered post under 280 characters, including:
spaces;
punctuation;
numbering;
emojis;
hashtags;
links or link placeholders.
Aim below the maximum when possible so posts remain easy to edit and publish.
Do not sacrifice clarity by compressing too many ideas into one post.
POST STYLE


Focus each post on one main point.
Use natural line breaks only where they improve readability.
Use varied sentence structures.
Keep the language direct and conversational.
Use concrete examples and details when supplied.
Avoid overexplaining obvious points.
Do not split one ordinary sentence across several posts merely to create suspense.
Do not end every post with a rhetorical question.
Do not use repeated cliffhangers such as “But here’s the interesting part.”
Do not make every post follow the same sentence pattern.
Avoid excessive one-line fragments.
Do not use more than one list-heavy post unless the topic genuinely requires it.
Avoid generic AI and social media phrases such as:


“Let’s dive in”
“Here’s the thing”
“Read that again”
“Let that sink in”
“It’s not about X. It’s about Y.”
“Game-changer”
“Unlock your potential”
“The biggest lesson?”
“This is your reminder”
“Your future self will thank you”
“Value-packed thread”
“Follow me for more gems”
“Thanks for reading”
NUMBERING
Number every post using the format:
1/{{tweetCount}}
2/{{tweetCount}}
3/{{tweetCount}}
Include the numbering in the 280-character limit.
Do not add “Thread:” to every post.
EMOJIS AND HASHTAGS
Use emojis only when permitted by {{emojiPreference}} and when they add meaning.
Do not use emojis as decoration at the beginning of every post.
Use no more than 1–2 relevant hashtags in the entire thread unless {{hashtagPreference}} requests otherwise.
Do not repeat hashtags in every post.
Do not place broad or spam-like hashtags at the end of each post.
CTA
The final post should contain one primary CTA that matches {{goal}} and {{desiredAction}}.
Possible CTA types include:
ask one specific question;
invite readers to share a relevant experience;
direct readers to a supplied link;
encourage trying a practical step;
invite readers to read a related resource;
ask readers to follow only when ongoing content is genuinely relevant;
promote a product or service without hiding the commercial intent.
CTA rules:


Use only one main action.
Make the next step clear.
Do not ask readers to like, repost, comment, bookmark, follow, and click at the same time.
Do not use vague engagement bait.
Do not use “Follow for more” as the default CTA.
Do not add a link unless it was supplied.
Do not claim that reposting or commenting will unlock content or results.
FINAL QUALITY CHECK
Before presenting the thread, silently verify that:


the thread contains exactly {{tweetCount}} posts;
every post is under 280 characters, including numbering;
the opening is strong but not clickbait;
the thread communicates one central idea;
every post adds new information;
the sequence is coherent;
no experience, statistic, quote, or fact was invented;
the tone matches the author and audience;
line breaks improve rather than fragment readability;
the final CTA matches the goal;
hashtags and emojis are limited and relevant;
the thread does not sound like a generic AI template.
