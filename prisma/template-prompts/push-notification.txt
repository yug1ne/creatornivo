## Core task

Generate exactly {{variantCount}} complete push-notification options. Treat the first option as the recommended version. The remaining options must use meaningfully different message angles, not minor synonym substitutions.

Each notification must communicate one main idea, provide immediate user value, and accurately prepare the user for the destination opened after the tap. Make the relationship between the message and {{tapDestination}} clear. Do not promise content, savings, status, access, or functionality that the destination does not support.

Write in {{outputLanguage}}. Follow the selected {{tone}} and any supplied {{brandVoice}} without making the copy artificially polished, corporate, enthusiastic, or promotional.

## Handling blank or automatic inputs

When an optional field is blank, irrelevant, or set to Auto:

* infer a neutral, platform-appropriate choice;
* omit unnecessary output elements;
* never display “not provided,” empty labels, or unresolved placeholders;
* do not invent missing facts;
* do not request additional information inside the generated content;
* use the supplied key message as the factual boundary.

If {{notificationContext}} is Auto, infer the most suitable notification type from {{primaryGoal}}, {{campaignSubject}}, and {{keyMessage}}.

If {{callToActionPreference}} is Auto or blank, infer one concise action that matches {{tapDestination}}. A push notification is already tappable, so do not force an explicit command when a natural informational message is stronger.

Use {{senderName}} only when it improves recognition or clarity. Do not repeat the brand name mechanically when the app or browser already identifies the sender.

## Push-notification writing rules

Push notifications are interruption-based, lock-screen-visible messages. Write for fast scanning on a small screen.

Use:

* the most important information first;
* concrete nouns and active verbs;
* clear dates or deadlines only when supplied;
* one primary message and one primary action;
* natural sentence structure;
* useful specificity;
* calm wording for service problems or important alerts;
* truthful urgency proportional to the actual situation.

Avoid:

* vague teaser copy that hides the real message;
* clickbait;
* several competing actions;
* unnecessary greetings;
* hashtags;
* long introductions;
* repeated punctuation;
* all caps;
* generic marketing clichés;
* fake conversational familiarity;
* manipulative guilt, fear, or pressure;
* wording that implies the sender knows more about the recipient than the supplied data supports.

Do not use phrases such as “Act now,” “Last chance,” “Only a few left,” or similar urgency unless the supplied facts and expiration context clearly justify them.

A Critical urgency selection does not permit invented danger or exaggerated consequences. Use critical language only when {{keyMessage}} describes a genuine, time-sensitive safety, security, service, or account issue. Otherwise reduce the wording to High or Normal urgency and mention the adjustment in the compliance note.

## Title and body handling

Follow {{titleMode}}:

* Auto: use a title and body when the two-part structure improves clarity; otherwise use body only.
* Title and body: provide both elements for every option.
* Body only: omit titles completely.

When a title is used, it must add context rather than repeat the body.

Because mobile operating systems, browser environments, devices, app states, fonts, languages, and notification providers truncate content differently, do not present any universal character count as an official platform maximum.

Unless stricter limits are supplied, use these conservative writing targets:

* title target: approximately 20–35 characters;
* body target: approximately 45–90 characters;
* title safety ceiling: 40 characters;
* body safety ceiling: 110 characters.

Count title and body separately. Include spaces and punctuation in the displayed character counts. Keep meaningful words early so the notification remains understandable if truncated.

For languages in which characters carry different visual density, prioritize visual brevity and readability rather than mechanically matching English character patterns.

If a required disclosure cannot fit responsibly within a push notification, do not compress it into misleading wording. Create the safest concise notification possible and state that the full disclosure should appear on the destination screen.

## Timing and expiration

Use {{timingContext}} to make the wording appropriate to the send event. Do not repeat operational instructions that belong only in internal delivery notes.

Use {{expirationContext}} only when it contains a verified deadline, expiration point, or relevance window. Never create a countdown, deadline, or scarcity claim that was not supplied.

When timing or expiration information is provided, include a short delivery note after the notification variants. If no timing information is provided, omit that section.

## Personalization and privacy

Use only the tokens explicitly listed in {{personalizationTokens}}. Preserve each token’s exact spelling, braces, capitalization, and formatting.

Do not invent names, locations, purchase behavior, account activity, order details, or other personalized facts. Do not imply that a token is available when it was not supplied.

Personalization must remain useful if the token is empty or unavailable. Avoid awkward greetings or sentences that fail when a value is missing.

Because notifications may appear on a locked screen, do not expose unnecessary sensitive information, including medical details, complete account identifiers, private financial information, legal matters, precise personal status, private locations, eligibility status, or information about children. Use a neutral message such as “You have a new account update” when the full detail belongs inside the authenticated destination. If {{claimsAndRestrictions}} or {{additionalRequirements}} asks to keep a detail private, keep it out of title, body, CTA, and notes.

## Emoji handling

Follow {{emojiUse}}:

* None: do not use emoji.
* Minimal: use no more than one emoji per notification and only when it improves recognition or tone.
* Moderate: use no more than two relevant emoji per notification.

Never use emoji as a substitute for essential meaning. Avoid emoji in serious safety, legal, financial, medical, account-security, or service-failure messages unless clearly appropriate.

## Accuracy, claims, and ethical conversion

Treat {{keyMessage}}, {{expirationContext}}, {{requiredDisclosure}}, and {{claimsAndRestrictions}} as the only approved factual sources.

Never invent or imply:

* prices;
* discounts;
* availability;
* deadlines;
* product capabilities;
* account status;
* delivery status;
* user actions;
* customer data;
* savings;
* results;
* statistics;
* reviews;
* testimonials;
* ratings;
* awards;
* certifications;
* partnerships;
* legal approval;
* medical outcomes;
* financial returns;
* guaranteed outcomes;
* previous relationships;
* consent;
* research or verification that did not occur.

Do not use fake urgency, fake scarcity, misleading discounts, fabricated social proof, deceptive personalization, hidden advertising, shame-based pressure, or engagement manipulation.

For promotional notifications, assume the organization is responsible for obtaining valid permission and following applicable platform and legal requirements. Do not add opt-out language automatically because push preference controls normally belong in the application or device settings, but do not obstruct or discourage user control.

## Sensitive and regulated topics

Apply additional caution when {{sensitiveCategory}} is not None.

Consider {{jurisdiction}} only as supplied context. Do not claim legal compliance, regulatory approval, or professional review.

Use {{requiredDisclosure}} exactly or faithfully condense it only when condensation does not alter its meaning. If the disclosure is too long for a notification, direct users to the destination for full information and flag this in the compliance note. Do not omit a supplied required disclosure to make a variant shorter.

For health, medical, legal, financial, employment, insurance, political, child-related, gambling, or other regulated content:

* avoid personalized professional advice;
* avoid diagnosis;
* avoid guarantees;
* avoid fear-based conversion;
* avoid unsupported eligibility or outcome claims;
* avoid targeting language that reveals a sensitive personal attribute;
* use cautious factual wording;
* keep sensitive details behind an authenticated screen when appropriate.

Apply every instruction in {{claimsAndRestrictions}} and {{additionalRequirements}} unless it conflicts with truthfulness, privacy, safety, or the supplied facts. Treat exact prohibited phrases in those fields as hard exclusions across notification titles, bodies, CTAs, Compliance and Accuracy Notes, and other notes; describe any concern generically instead of repeating prohibited wording.

### Compliance and Accuracy Note

Include only when one or more of the following applies:

* {{sensitiveCategory}} is not None;
* {{requiredDisclosure}} is supplied;
* {{claimsAndRestrictions}} is supplied;
* the requested urgency is not supported by the facts;
* a required disclosure cannot fit safely;
* the message risks exposing sensitive lock-screen information.

Keep this note brief and implementation-focused. Do not include hidden reasoning or chain-of-thought.

## Final quality check

Before returning the package, silently verify that:

* the exact requested number of variants is present;
* every notification matches the supplied goal and audience;
* the notification accurately reflects the tap destination;
* title and body follow the selected structure;
* character counts are correct;
* conservative length targets are respected;
* important information appears early;
* no unsupported fact or personalization was added;
* urgency is justified;
* words such as “now,” “last chance,” “limited,” or deadline language appear only when supported by supplied facts and expiration context;
* privacy-sensitive information is not unnecessarily exposed;
* required disclosures and user restrictions are preserved without exposing private details on the lock screen;
* variants are genuinely distinct;
* optional blank fields do not appear in the output;
* the final response contains only the requested notification package.
