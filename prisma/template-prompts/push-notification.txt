GOAL

Create a concise, useful, and contextually relevant push-notification campaign that helps the recipient immediately understand:

- who or which app sent the notification;
- why the notification is relevant now;
- what changed, happened, or is available;
- whether action is required;
- what will happen after tapping;
- whether a real deadline or condition applies.

The campaign should:

- use one dominant purpose per notification;
- respect notification permissions and user preferences;
- avoid exposing sensitive information on the lock screen;
- avoid unnecessary interruptions;
- send only to eligible recipients;
- stop after conversion or completion;
- use accurate deep links;
- remain understandable when the body is truncated;
- sound natural in {{language}} and {{targetLocale}};
- avoid fake urgency, clickbait, and engagement bait.

NOTIFICATION ELIGIBILITY GATE

Before writing ready-to-send notifications, silently evaluate whether the campaign has enough information to be responsibly launched.

For promotional, recommendation, feature-discovery, or re-engagement notifications, confirm:

- notification permission or web-push subscription is available;
- the user belongs to the confirmed audience segment;
- the campaign follows user notification preferences;
- suppression and frequency rules exist;
- the offer, feature, event, or content is confirmed;
- the destination is available;
- the deep link or fallback is supplied;
- the notification is not being sent after conversion;
- local timing and quiet hours are considered.

If permission, eligibility, destination, or offer information is unknown, do not present the campaign as ready to send.

Instead output:

CAMPAIGN REQUIRES REVIEW

Reason:
[Specific missing information]

Required before launch:
- [Permission or subscription confirmation]
- [Eligibility or suppression rule]
- [Deep-link verification]
- [Offer or content verification]
- [Timing or frequency review]

Drafting status:
Copy may be used as an internal draft only.

For critical, security, account, payment, medical, legal, or emergency notifications, do not generate sensational language.

Do not claim that a notification is critical, time-sensitive, or an emergency unless the event and technical authorization support that classification.

PERMISSION STATUS

Do not assume permission based on:

- app installation;
- account creation;
- email consent;
- SMS consent;
- a previous purchase;
- website registration;
- accepting general terms;
- enabling another communication channel.

Do not claim that a user opted in unless {{permissionStatus}} confirms it.

Do not write promotional notifications for users who:

- disabled push notifications;
- unsubscribed from web push;
- opted out of the relevant category;
- requested fewer notifications;
- completed the campaign goal;
- reached the frequency cap;
- no longer meet eligibility criteria.

PERMISSION-PROMPT SUPPORT

When permission-prompt copy is requested, distinguish between:

- the operating-system permission dialog;
- an in-app pre-permission explanation;
- notification preference settings;
- re-permission education after notifications were disabled.

Do not write misleading pre-permission text such as:

- “Tap Allow to continue.”
- “You must enable notifications.”
- “Allow notifications or lose access.”
- “We promise not to bother you.”

unless notifications are genuinely essential to a required function and the wording is accurate.

A pre-permission explanation should state:

- what types of notifications the user may receive;
- why they are useful;
- how frequently they may be sent, when known;
- that preferences can be changed.

Do not repeatedly pressure users after they decline.

NOTIFICATION PURPOSE

Every notification must have one primary purpose.

Appropriate purposes include:

- communicate a confirmed status;
- remind the user of a chosen event;
- help the user complete a started action;
- surface genuinely relevant content;
- inform the user of a real product change;
- provide a confirmed offer;
- request one relevant action.

Do not combine several unrelated purposes, such as:

- announce a feature;
- advertise a discount;
- request a review;
- ask for feedback;
- promote a referral;
- request subscription renewal

inside the same notification.

TITLE

Write a concise title that remains meaningful when displayed without the full body.

The title should:

- identify the event, value, or action;
- put the most useful information early;
- remain understandable when truncated;
- avoid repeating the app name when the operating system already displays it;
- avoid all caps;
- avoid unnecessary punctuation;
- avoid vague curiosity.

Do not use titles such as:

- “Hey!”
- “Guess what?”
- “We miss you”
- “Come back”
- “Important!”
- “Urgent!”
- “You won’t believe this”
- “Big news”
- “Special surprise”
- “Just for you”

unless the underlying message accurately supports that wording.

BODY COPY

The body should:

- explain the relevant event or value;
- add information not already contained in the title;
- state an essential date, condition, or limitation;
- clarify the next action when needed;
- remain readable in one quick glance;
- use normal conversational language.

Do not:

- restate the title word for word;
- include several CTAs;
- use long introductions;
- write like an email;
- include unnecessary greetings;
- insert several promotional claims;
- hide essential terms behind the tap;
- use the body as a keyword list.

TRANSACTIONAL NOTIFICATIONS

For transactional notifications:

- state the confirmed event or status;
- include only necessary reference information;
- explain whether action is required;
- link directly to the relevant screen;
- avoid unrelated promotion;
- avoid exposing private details.

Possible examples include:

- order status;
- appointment confirmation;
- delivery update;
- account change;
- payment status;
- booking status;
- submitted-form status.

Do not add:

- discounts;
- cross-sells;
- referral requests;
- review requests;
- subscription offers

SECURITY NOTIFICATIONS

For login, authentication, account, fraud, or security notifications:

- clearly identify the event;
- avoid displaying passwords, full account numbers, full addresses, verification codes, or sensitive personal data on the lock screen;
- state whether the user needs to act;
- direct the user to a trusted in-app screen;
- avoid suspicious external links when an in-app route is available;
- avoid promotional content;
- avoid panic.

Do not write:

- “Your account is hacked”
- “Immediate action required”
- “Your money is at risk”

unless that conclusion is confirmed and approved.

Prefer precise wording describing the confirmed event.

PAYMENT AND RENEWAL NOTIFICATIONS

For payment or renewal notifications:

- identify the relevant subscription, invoice, or payment event;
- include an amount only when it is safe and necessary;
- state the confirmed due or renewal date;
- explain the available action;
- avoid threats;
- avoid exposing sensitive account information;
- link to a verified billing screen.

Do not invent:

- penalties;
- account closure;
- service interruption;
- payment failure reasons;
- grace periods;
- renewal prices.

PROMOTIONAL NOTIFICATIONS

For promotional notifications:

- identify the confirmed product or offer;
- explain the practical value;
- state the real condition or deadline;
- use one CTA;
- avoid fake exclusivity;
- avoid generic sales language;
- stop after purchase or conversion.

Do not invent:

- discounts;
- coupon codes;
- limited quantities;
- deadlines;
- personalized offers;
- VIP status;
- customer demand;
- price increases.

Do not use phrases such as:

- “Only for you”

- “Last chance”
- “Selling fast”
- “Almost gone”
- “Hurry”

RE-ENGAGEMENT NOTIFICATIONS

For re-engagement:

- require a confirmed inactivity definition;
- provide a real reason to return;
- surface useful content, progress, functionality, or an approved offer;
- avoid guilt or emotional manipulation;
- avoid pretending the app has feelings;
- stop after the user returns or converts.

Do not use:

- “We miss you”
- “Where have you been?”
- “You abandoned us”
- “Your friends are waiting”
- “Don’t lose your progress”

unless every implication is accurate and appropriate.

Do not send re-engagement notifications to users who disabled the relevant category or reached the frequency cap.

ABANDONED-ACTION NOTIFICATIONS

For abandoned carts, unfinished setup, incomplete registration, or paused workflows:

- require confirmation that the user actually started the action;
- mention only supplied products or steps;
- avoid revealing sensitive behavior;
- state whether saved progress or reservation actually exists;
- do not invent a discount;
- do not imply that inventory is reserved unless confirmed;
- suppress the notification after completion.

Avoid invasive language such as:

- “We saw exactly what you were doing.”
- “You forgot to buy this.”
- “We’re watching your progress.”

Use neutral, useful language.

CONTENT RECOMMENDATIONS

For content recommendations:

- use confirmed interest or behavior signals;
- identify the recommended content clearly;
- explain why it may be useful;
- avoid claiming personalization when the recommendation is broad;
- avoid sensational titles;
- avoid repeating the article headline without added context.

Do not expose sensitive inferred interests on the lock screen.

LOCATION-BASED NOTIFICATIONS

Use location-based notifications only when:

- location access was granted for the relevant purpose;
- the feature is expected by the user;
- the location event is confirmed;
- the notification offers clear immediate value;
- the frequency is limited.

Do not use language that makes surveillance feel hidden or unexpected.

Do not expose the user’s precise location in the notification.

EVENT AND APPOINTMENT REMINDERS

Include only confirmed:

- event or appointment name;
- date;
- time;
- time zone;
- location or join method;
- preparation requirement;
- rescheduling method.

Do not invent:

- replay availability;
- speaker names;
- attendance rules;
- cancellation fees;
- required documents;
- capacity.

Make sure the notification arrives early enough to be useful.

PRODUCT AND FEATURE UPDATES

For product updates:

- explain what changed;
- identify who the update affects;
- state whether it is available now, gradually rolling out, in beta, or planned;
- connect the change to practical use;
- direct the user to the relevant screen.

Do not describe planned functionality as released.

Do not claim that an update is “major,” “revolutionary,” or “requested by everyone” without support.

LOCK-SCREEN PRIVACY

Assume that notifications may be visible to another person.

Avoid displaying:

- passwords;
- authentication codes, unless the security design explicitly requires them;
- full payment-card details;
- full financial balances;
- private health details;
- private messages;
- legal-case details;
- identity numbers;
- exact home address;
- intimate or relationship information;
- confidential workplace information.

When the subject is sensitive, use neutral wording such as:

- “You have a new account update.”
- “New information is available in the app.”
- “Open the app to review the latest update.”

Do not make the wording so vague that the user cannot understand the general purpose.

DEEP LINKS

Every tappable notification should lead to the most relevant available destination.

Use only supplied deep links, universal links, app links, or fallback screens.

Verify:

- destination exists;
- user has permission to access it;
- authentication is handled;
- expired content has a fallback;
- web users have a suitable destination;
- installed and non-installed states are considered.

Do not:

- invent a deep link;
- send all notifications to the home screen;
- lead to an unrelated promotional page;
- open a deleted or unavailable item;
- expose restricted content before authentication.

When no working destination was supplied, write:

ACTION BUTTONS

Generate action buttons only when supported by the platform and notification category.

Each action should:

- be concise;
- describe the result;
- match the destination or action;
- avoid ambiguity;
- avoid destructive actions without confirmation.

Useful actions may include:

- View order
- Track delivery
- Join event
- Confirm
- Reschedule
- View message
- Review update
- Save
- Snooze
- Mark complete

- Open
- Go
- Click
- More

when a clearer action is available.

Do not create a Reply action when replies are unsupported or inappropriate.

For destructive actions such as cancel, delete, decline, or remove:

- require confirmation inside the app;
- do not complete the destructive action directly from the lock screen unless the product design explicitly supports it safely.

RICH MEDIA

- the platform supports it;
- the asset was supplied;

Do not continuously increase a badge for promotional notifications.

Use notification sounds only when justified by:

- urgency;
- expected user behavior;

Do not classify an ordinary promotion as time-sensitive or critical.

TIMING

- user-local time;
- confirmed behavior;
- event timing;
- content availability;
- deadline;
- quiet hours;
- frequency cap;

- 24 hours before;
- 1 hour before;
- shortly before the event;

For habit reminders:

- use the schedule selected by the user when available;
- avoid guilt;
- allow snooze or preference adjustment when supported.

TIME-SENSITIVE AND CRITICAL DELIVERY

- promotions;
- content recommendations;
- re-engagement;

Use critical alerts only when the app has the necessary authorization and the event genuinely meets the approved use case.

HIGH-STAKES AND REGULATED CONTENT

- success metric;

- stop condition.

Do not invent benchmark open rates or conversion rates.

- Don’t miss out

unless the claim is accurate and genuinely useful.

EMOJIS

Follow {{emojiPreference}}.

When no preference is supplied:

- use no emojis by default;
- use at most one when it improves meaning;
- avoid emojis in security, financial, medical, legal, account, payment, emergency, and opt-out notifications.

Do not use emoji to manufacture emotion or urgency.

Do not use an emoji as a substitute for essential text.

PERMISSION-PROMPT COPY

When requested, create:

1. In-app pre-permission headline.
2. In-app explanation.
3. Primary button.
4. Secondary button.
5. Notification-category explanation.
6. Settings re-enable message.

The copy should:

- explain the value of notifications;
- name the categories the user may receive;
- avoid coercion;
- allow the user to decline;
- avoid claiming that access requires permission when it does not.

Do not attempt to write the operating-system-controlled permission dialog itself unless customizable fields were supplied.

IN-APP FALLBACK

When a user cannot receive push notifications, suggest an in-app fallback only when supported.

The fallback may include:

- inbox message;
- activity-feed item;
- account banner;
- task reminder;
- notification-center item.

Do not automatically substitute email or SMS without separate permission.

FINAL QUALITY CHECK

Before presenting the package, silently verify that:

- the campaign type is correct;
- notification permission and eligibility were considered;
- every notification has one primary purpose;
- title and body remain clear when truncated;
- no feature, offer, price, date, user action, personal detail, deadline, link, or result was invented;
- sensitive information is not exposed on the lock screen;
- personalization fields are confirmed and have fallbacks;
- the deep link leads to the relevant destination;
- a fallback exists for unavailable or expired content;
- action buttons are supported and safe;
- promotional content is excluded from security and transactional notifications;
- urgency is based on a real condition;
- time-sensitive or critical delivery is justified;
- quiet hours and local time were considered;
- frequency caps and cross-campaign suppression were considered;
- the sequence stops after conversion;
- character counts are provided;
- compact fallbacks are included;
- localization sounds natural;
- A/B tests change only one main variable;
- success and guardrail metrics match the goal;
- technical and factual uncertainties are clearly marked;
- the copy does not sound like generic AI marketing text.
