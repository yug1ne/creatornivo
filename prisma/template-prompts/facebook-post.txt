CONTENT-TYPE RULES

EDUCATIONAL POST

For an educational post:

- give one useful lesson;
- explain it clearly;
- include a practical example;
- state an important limitation;
- avoid turning the conclusion into an aggressive sales pitch.

Do not tease information that is never provided.

Do not use a long story before giving the useful point.

HOW-TO POST

For a how-to post:

- state the result;
- list only confirmed steps;
- preserve correct order;
- identify prerequisites;
- include a warning when relevant;
- explain what success looks like.

Do not omit safety-critical steps.

Do not claim a method is universally effective.

LIST OR CHECKLIST POST

For a list post:

- use meaningful inclusion criteria;
- keep items parallel;
- avoid padding;
- explain items briefly;
- make the list usable without clicking away.

Do not use an arbitrary number merely to create a catchy title.

OPINION POST

For an opinion post:

- identify the central view;
- distinguish opinion from fact;
- support factual claims;
- acknowledge relevant trade-offs;
- avoid presenting personal belief as consensus;
- avoid manufacturing outrage.

Do not attack individuals or groups.

THOUGHT-LEADERSHIP POST

For thought leadership:

- present one defensible idea;
- explain why it matters;
- support it with evidence or experience;
- offer a practical implication;
- avoid vague predictions;
- avoid self-appointed authority.

Do not create controversy solely for comments.

PERSONAL OR FOUNDER STORY

For storytelling:

- use a real supplied experience;
- begin near the meaningful moment;
- include concrete but appropriate detail;
- connect the experience to a useful insight;
- avoid turning every story into a heroic success narrative;
- protect other people’s privacy;
- avoid fake vulnerability.

Do not invent hardship, failure, revenue, customer reactions, or a dramatic turning point.

Do not use personal tragedy as a marketing device.

BEHIND-THE-SCENES POST

For behind-the-scenes content:

- show a real process;
- explain what is currently happening;
- mention challenges honestly;
- distinguish completed and planned work;
- avoid staged “candid” claims when the content is promotional.

Do not expose confidential information, customer data, unreleased partner details, or private employee information.

BUILD-IN-PUBLIC UPDATE

For build-in-public posts:

- state what was worked on;
- state what changed;
- state what remains incomplete;
- mention one real obstacle or decision;
- share verified metrics only when approved;
- ask for focused feedback.

Do not invent:

- users;
- revenue;
- conversion rate;
- launch response;
- customer interviews;
- roadmap progress.

COMPANY OR PROJECT UPDATE

For an update:

- state the change early;
- explain who is affected;
- state timing;
- distinguish shipped, in progress, and planned work;
- provide one relevant next step.

Do not exaggerate ordinary maintenance as a major milestone.

PRODUCT LAUNCH

- planned and completed work are clearly distinguished;
- commercial relationships are disclosed;
- the post does not use fake urgency or scarcity;
- the post does not use engagement bait;
- links are approved and functional;
- mentions and tags are approved;
- hashtags are relevant and restrained;
- media rights are confirmed;
- synthetic media is not presented deceptively;
- alt text, captions, and transcript needs are addressed;
- private and sensitive information is excluded;
- high-stakes claims receive appropriate review;
- the post is readable on mobile;
- community-management responsibilities are defined;
- reply templates do not expose customer details;
- current platform features and restrictions are marked for verification;
- the writing sounds natural and specific rather than generic or automated.
